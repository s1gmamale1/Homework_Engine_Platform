## 1. What Is This Family

"Aniq" translates to precise, exact, and derivable. This family is defined by its reliance on formal reasoning, logical proofs, and quantitative accuracy. Unlike linguistic or social sciences, knowledge in this family is not "interpreted"—it is derived from first principles and laws.

**What it teaches:** Formal reasoning through definitions, logical derivations, and practical applications of exact laws.

**What it focuses on:** 
- Calculations and arithmetic fluency.
- Conceptual understanding of abstract laws.
- Formula manipulation and transformation.
- Multi-step derivation and proof sequences.
- Spatial and algorithmic thinking.
- Critical Thinking

**What it is NOT about:** Rote memorization of long texts, narrative interpretation, or free-form creative expression.

**Cognitive Demand Profile (Priority Order):**
1. Calculation & Execution
2. Concept Understanding
3. Formula Recall & Manipulation
4. Error Detection & Diagnosis
5. Spatial Reasoning
6. Algorithmic Thinking

## 3. What This Family Focuses On (The Content Filter)

Content producers must prioritize these cognitive anchors:

- **Concept BEFORE Procedure:** Ensure the student understands *why* a formula works before making them execute it 10 times.
- **CPA Progression is MANDATORY:** Every new concept must move through **Concrete → Pictorial → Abstract** stages (See [[UNIFIED-Buzan §5.2]]).
- **Worked Examples (Completion Effect):** Use partial solutions where students fill in the logical gaps.
- **Error Detection:** Present deliberately incorrect solutions for the student to diagnose and fix.
- **Dual Coding:** Every formula must be paired with a verbal/conceptual explanation.
- **Interleaved Practice:** Mix different problem types (e.g., area and perimeter) to prevent mindless pattern matching.
- **Bidirectional Thinking:** Every session must work BOTH directions — real world → formula (model a situation as an equation) AND formula → real world (given a result, explain what it means physically). A student who can solve `2x + 5 = 15` but can't recognize a real problem IS that equation hasn't learned.
- **Error Analysis is PRIMARY:** Not occasional — minimum every 2 sessions. Students must find WHERE an error is and WHY it's wrong, not just "is this correct?"
- **Answer Completeness:** Every numerical answer must include correct units/context. `x = 5` is incomplete. `x = 5 metr` or `x = 5 ta olma` is complete.

**Do NOT Focus On:**
- Free-form writing or essays.
- Subjective interpretation of stories.
- Opinion-based debates without derivation.

## 4. Best Methods & Games (Filtered from UNIFIED-Buzan)

### Primary Mechanics (Priority Usage)

#### **Tile Match (Formula Assembly)**
- **What it is:** Dragging symbolic components into correct structures ([[UNIFIED-Buzan §6]]).
- **Why:** Builds symbolic fluency without the friction of typing complex syntax.
- **Good Example:** Assemble the quadratic formula by dragging `-b`, `±`, `√`, and `2a` into their slots. Concept Matching. Definition Matching. Formula matching
- **Bad Example:** Match the word "Algebra" to its dictionary definition (L1 Recall).

#### **Notebook Capture (Handwritten Calculations)**
- **What it is:** Photo submission of handwritten work.
- **Why:** **Generation Effect**—writing math by hand improves retention by ~30% over typing.
- **Good Example:** "Solve this system of 3 equations on paper and upload the photo." Draw this graphic. Draw the outcome based on the formula. Decode the graphic and find the formula.
- **Bad Example:** "Write the definition of a triangle" (Notebook Capture is for derivation, not text).
- **Frequency:** G5-8 (1 in 3 sessions); G9-11 (1 in 2 sessions).

#### **Error Analysis (Metacognitive Fix)**
- **What it is:** Finding the "bug" in a pre-solved problem.
- **Why:** Forces students to think like a teacher/expert.
- **Good Example:** "A student calculated `2+3*5 = 25`. Find the error and state the correct rule." Give solved problems with easter eggs and have them detect it.
- **Bad Example:** "Is this answer correct? [Yes/No]" (Too binary).

#### **Worked Examples (Scaffolded Completion)**
- **What it is:** Step-by-step solutions with missing pieces.
- **Why:** Reduces cognitive load (Sweller’s Load Theory).
- **Good Example:** Step 1: `3x = 12`. Step 2: `x = 12 / __`. Step 3: `x = __`.
- **Bad Example:** Providing a blank box for a 5-step problem with no hints.

### Secondary Mechanics
- **Adaptive Quiz:** For skill-drilling once the concept is acquired.
- **Speed Match:** Arithmetic fluency (Multiplication tables, unit conversions).
- **Why Chain:** Socratic drilling. Cap at 2 levels for G5, 3 levels for G6+.

### Phase 5 Buzan Alternatives
- **Peg System:** Use Number-Shape associations for math rules.
- **Radiant Summary:** Hierarchical mind-maps for classifying number systems or shapes.
- **Link System:** For sequential processes (e.g., steps of long division).

### Phase 1 Format Mixing + Construction (Family-Level Rule)

**MUST use at least 2 of 3 formats** (Multiple Choice, True/False, Yes/No/Not Given) in every Phase 1 Memory Sprint across all Aniq Fanlar subjects. No single-format sprints.

**Per-format construction recipe:**

**Multiple Choice (4 options):**
- Stem: clear question with one correct answer. Include a diagram or formula when the question involves a shape or expression.
- Distractors: based on common student errors, not random. Example: for `3(x+2)`, distractors include `3x+2` (forgot to distribute), `3x+5` (arithmetic slip), `6x` (combined incorrectly).
- Tag: `[Bloom: L1-L2 | PISA: L1-L2]`
- Good: *"Qaysi ifoda 2(a+3) ga teng? A) 2a+3 B) 2a+6 C) a+6 D) 2a+5"*
- Bad: *"Algebra nima? A) Fan B) O'yin C) Sport D) San'at"* (definitional, no math reasoning)

**True / False:**
- Statement must be precise and testable. No ambiguity.
- Always include the rule or identity being tested.
- Tag: `[Bloom: L1-L2 | PISA: L1-L2]`
- Good: *"(a+b)² = a² + 2ab + b² — To'g'ri yoki Noto'g'ri?"*
- Bad: *"Matematika qiyin — To'g'ri yoki Noto'g'ri?"* (opinion, not testable)

**Yes / No / Not Given:**
- Must reference the current chapter explicitly: *"Bobda aytilganiga ko'ra..."*
- "Not Given" must be genuinely plausible — the chapter must be silent on the claim.
- Tag: `[Bloom: L2 | PISA: L2]`
- Good: *"Bobda aytilganiga ko'ra, har qanday to'rtburchak parallelogramm — Ha / Yo'q / Aytilmagan."*
- Bad: *"Matematika foydali — Ha / Yo'q / Aytilmagan?"* (not chapter-dependent)

### Phase 3 Game Construction Recipes (Family-Level Defaults)

These recipes apply across all Aniq Fanlar subjects. Subject frameworks specify which games go in which slot.

**Tile Match (Default Pool — all subjects):**
- **Structure:** 6-8 pairs. Left tile = formula/expression/theorem name. Right tile = equivalent form/visual/definition.
- **Difficulty progression:**
  - L1: Direct match — formula ↔ its name. *Left: "a² + 2ab + b²" → Right: "(a+b)²"*
  - L2: Transformation match — expression ↔ simplified form. *Left: "3(x+2) − x" → Right: "2x + 6"*
  - L3: Cross-representation — formula ↔ graph/diagram. *Left: "y = 2x + 1" → Right: [graph image]*
- **Good pair:** `"S = πr²"` ↔ `"Doira yuzasi"` (formula ↔ meaning)
- **Bad pair:** `"Algebra"` ↔ `"Fan"` (too vague, no mathematical content)

**Worked Examples / Scaffolded Completion (Default Pool):**
- **Structure:** 4-6 step solution with 1-3 blanks. Student fills missing steps.
- **Blank placement:** Never blank the FIRST step (student needs orientation) or the LAST step (they need closure). Blank the MIDDLE reasoning steps.
- **Difficulty progression:**
  - L1: 1 blank in a 4-step solution. Blank is a single operation. *Step 2: "x = 12 / __ "*
  - L2: 2 blanks. One blank requires choosing the correct operation.
  - L3: 3 blanks including the justification: *"Bu qadam [__] xossasi asosida amalga oshirildi."*
- **Good:** *"Step 1: 3x + 6 = 18. Step 2: 3x = 18 − __ . Step 3: 3x = __ . Step 4: x = 4."*
- **Bad:** All steps blank with only the problem and answer given (no scaffolding).

**Error Analysis (Default Pool — minimum every 2 sessions):**
- **Structure:** Complete worked solution with 1-2 deliberate errors. Student finds WHERE the error is and explains WHY it is wrong.
- **Error types:** Sign error, order-of-operations violation, forgotten distribution, wrong theorem application, unit omission.
- **Difficulty progression:**
  - L1: 1 obvious error (sign flip). *"2 − 5 = 3" — xato qayerda?*
  - L2: 1 subtle error in a multi-step solution. Student must trace through all steps.
  - L3: 2 errors — one obvious distractor, one subtle. Student must find BOTH.
- **Good:** *"Talaba yechimi: 2 + 3 × 5 = 25. Xatoni toping va to'g'ri qoidani ayting."* (Order of operations)
- **Bad:** *"Bu javob to'g'rimi? Ha / Yo'q"* (binary, no analysis)

2. **Metacognitive Prompts (45 sec, pick 3):**

3. **BOST Goal Recall (15 sec):** *"Bugun Siz '[stored goal]' ni bilmoqchi edingiz. Bildingizmi?"* Student taps: Fully / Partially / Not yet. Result logged for adaptive engine.

4. **Spaced Repetition Schedule:** Display next review dates: *"Bu mavzu ertaga (1 kun), 3 kundan keyin va 7 kundan keyin qaytariladi."* Tag items for spaced rep queue based on session accuracy.

## 1. Subject Identity

**Algebra va Analiz Asoslari in Grades 10-11** is the apex of the Aniq Fanlar symbolic reasoning track. It is where algebra's study of relationships meets calculus's study of *change* — the most powerful mathematical lens ever developed for understanding the natural and economic world.

**What Algebra va Analiz IS:**
- Advanced functions: exponential, logarithmic, trigonometric — domains, ranges, graphs, transformations
- Limits: the foundational concept of analysis — what a function approaches as its input approaches some value
- Derivatives: instantaneous rate of change, tangent lines, optimization, curve sketching
- Integrals: accumulation, area under a curve, Fundamental Theorem of Calculus (introductory)
- Sequences and series: arithmetic and geometric, convergence concepts
- Mathematical induction: proving statements true for all natural numbers
- Polynomial algebra: completing the square, division, Vieta's formulas

**What Algebra va Analiz is NOT:**
- A repeat of G7-9 Algebra — no revisiting basic equations without extension
- Formal real analysis (epsilon-delta proofs — university scope)
- Multivariate calculus, differential equations — university scope
- Spatial geometry — belongs to Geometriya Advanced

**Core Cognitive Demand (Priority Order):**
1. Analytical thinking — understanding limiting processes and infinite behavior
2. Function analysis — reading, interpreting, and transforming complex functions
3. Derivative reasoning — connecting rates of change to function behavior
4. Formal reasoning — constructing inductive proofs, deriving from first principles
5. Modeling — setting up calculus-based models of real phenomena
6. Computational fluency — differentiating, integrating, evaluating limits accurately

**Subject Color:** `#4F46E5` (indigo primary) · `#F59E0B` (amber accent)

### 4.1 Memory Sprint (Phase 1)

**Inherits:** UNIFIED §5.1. Duration ≤2 min (hard cap). Current chapter warm-up — key terms, prerequisite concepts, foundational facts needed for this session.

### 4.2 Story Mode (Phase 2)

**Inherits:** UNIFIED §5.2. 4-beat arc mandatory. Stranger Test. IELTS-style checkpoints.

**Algebra va Analiz story arc — Analytical Investigation:**

| Arc Beat | Algebra va Analiz Version |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | A real phenomenon that cannot be described with a simple formula — it changes, accelerates, approaches a limit. "A new electric car company models battery charge over time. The charge drops fast at first, then slower. How do engineers predict exact remaining range at any moment?" |
| **Struggle** | Average rate of change (G7 rise-over-run) gives a rough answer — but it's imprecise. "At exactly minute 37, what is the rate?" The answer keeps depending on the interval chosen. |
| **Discovery** | Making the interval smaller and smaller — the limit process emerges naturally. "The derivative is the limit of the average rate as the interval shrinks to zero." Formal notation follows intuition. |
| **Solution** | The derivative function is found and interpreted: "At minute 37, the charge drops at exactly 2.3% per minute." The power of calculus made tangible. |

**Hard rules:**
- **Intuition before notation** — no limit or derivative notation introduced before the student understands what it represents
- The "shrinking interval" visualization is mandatory for introducing derivatives for the first time in a new session context
- ONE continuous story (5-7 min, slightly longer for G10-11). Arc beats are invisible ingredients, NOT segments or labels.
- CPA: Concrete (table of instantaneous values) → Pictorial (tangent line on graph, area under curve) → Abstract (formal limit/derivative notation)
- **No "trust the rule" teaching** — every differentiation rule shown as derivable from the limit definition at least once
- Checkpoint questions test **conceptual understanding**: "Intervalimizni qisqartirish nima beradi?" — not "Differentiate f(x) = x³" (Phase 3)
- Grade narrative: **G10-11 — subject-specific professional narratives** (Uzbek tech and economic contexts)
- Uzbek contexts: Tashkent tech startup, UzbekTelecom network optimization, UzAuto production curve, irrigation water flow in Fergana valley

**Phase 3 Game Construction Recipes — Algebra va Analiz:**

**Error Analysis (Slot 1 Primary — minimum every 2 sessions):**
- **Structure:** Complete multi-step differentiation or integration solution with 1-2 deliberate errors. Student finds WHERE and explains WHY.
- **Error types for calculus:** Forgotten chain rule, wrong power rule exponent, sign error in trig derivative, incorrect constant of integration, misapplied product/quotient rule.
- **Difficulty progression:**
  - L1: 1 obvious error in a 3-step derivative. *"d/dx[3x² + 5x] = 6x + 5x = 11x. Xatoni toping."* (Error: 5x should become 5, not stay as 5x)
  - L2: 1 subtle error in a multi-step chain rule application. *"d/dx[sin(x²)] = cos(x²). Xatoni toping va to'g'rilang."* (Missing inner derivative: should be 2x·cos(x²))
  - L3: 2 errors in an optimization problem — one in the derivative, one in the interpretation. Student must find BOTH.
    - *"f(x) = x³ − 3x. f'(x) = 3x² − 3 = 0 → x = 1. f(1) = −2 — bu minimum. Xatolarni toping."* (Error 1: x = ±1, not just x = 1. Error 2: need second derivative test to confirm min/max)
- **Good:** Full worked solution with annotation where error lives + correct version.
- **Bad:** *"Bu javob to'g'rimi? Ha / Yo'q"* (binary, no analysis)

**Reaction Chain (Slot 2 Primary):**
- **Structure:** 6-10 nodes in a sequential derivation chain. Each correct step unlocks the next.
- **Algebra va Analiz chain example (finding and interpreting critical points):**
  - Node 1: *"f(x) = x³ − 6x² + 9x + 1. f'(x) ni toping."* → Answer: 3x² − 12x + 9
  - Node 2: *"f'(x) = 0 tenglamani yeching."* → Answer: 3(x² − 4x + 3) = 0 → x = 1 va x = 3
  - Node 3: *"f''(x) ni toping."* → Answer: 6x − 12
  - Node 4: *"f''(1) = ? Bu nuqta minimum yoki maximum?"* → Answer: f''(1) = −6 < 0 → maximum
  - Node 5: *"f''(3) = ? Bu nuqta minimum yoki maximum?"* → Answer: f''(3) = 6 > 0 → minimum
  - Node 6: *"f(1) va f(3) ni hisoblab, grafik eskizini chizing."* → Answer: f(1) = 5, f(3) = 1
  - Node 7: *"Funksiya qaysi oraliqda o'sadi?"* → Answer: (−∞, 1) va (3, +∞)
- **Bad chain:** Unrelated derivative calculations (no logical flow from one node to the next).

**Connect Four vs AI (Slot 3 Primary):**
- **Structure:** ~18 questions gating column placement. Strategic placement + calculus reasoning.
- **Question types:** Function analysis (domain, asymptotes), quick derivative evaluation, limit calculation, critical point identification.
- **Difficulty:** Questions alternate: Easy (direct derivative rule), Medium (chain/product rule), Hard (interpretation question). Every 4th = Hard.
- **Example gating questions:**
  - Easy: *"d/dx[x⁴] = ?"* (Answer: 4x³)
  - Medium: *"d/dx[sin(2x)] = ?"* (Answer: 2cos(2x))
  - Hard: *"f'(a) > 0 bo'lsa, x = a atrofida f(x) qanday?"* (Answer: o'suvchi)
- **Bad gating question:** *"Nyuton qachon tug'ilgan?"* (trivia, not calculus)

**Why Chain (Slot 3 Backup — Secondary mechanic, 3 levels):**
- **Structure:** 3-level Socratic chain on a calculus concept.
- **Example:**
  - L1: *"d/dx[x³] = 3x². Bu qoidaning nomi nima?"* → Answer: Power rule
  - L2: *"Power rule nima uchun ishlaydi? Limit ta'rifidan ko'rsating."* → Answer: lim(h→0) [(x+h)³ − x³]/h = 3x²
  - L3: *"Bu hosila x = 2 da nima deydi? Funksiya haqida nima bilasiz?"* → Answer: f'(2) = 12, funksiya x=2 da tezlik 12 bilan o'smoqda

**Worked Examples / Scaffolded Completion (Slot 1 Backup):**
- **Structure:** 5-7 step derivation with 2-3 blanks. Student fills missing derivation steps.
- **Example (chain rule):**
  - Step 1: f(x) = sin(x²)
  - Step 2: Tashqi funksiya: sin(u), ichki funksiya: u = __
  - Step 3: d/dx[sin(u)] = __
  - Step 4: du/dx = __
  - Step 5: f'(x) = cos(x²) · 2x = 2x·cos(x²)
- **Blank the REASONING steps (Steps 2-4), not the final answer.**

**Games to AVOID for Algebra va Analiz:**
- Tile Match with simple formula-answer pairs — too low-level for G10-11
- Speed Match with basic arithmetic
- Puzzle Lock — spatial (Geometry Advanced's domain)
- Tic Tac Toe — too low-cognitive for this grade band
- Any game that does not require written justification

## 5. Content Rules

### 5.1 Narrative Voice

| Element | Rule |
|---|---|
| **POV** | Second-person: "Siz muhandissiz. Model sizga bu funktsiyani beradi. t=5 dagi hosila nima deydi?" |
| **Tone** | Intellectually rigorous, curious, collegial. G10-11 students are near-adults — speak to them as junior colleagues. No hand-holding. |
| **Language** | Uzbek or Russian. Terms: limit → "limit", derivative → "hosila", integral → "integral", continuous → "uzluksiz", asymptote → "asimptota". |
| **Notation formality** | Leibniz (dy/dx) and prime notation (f'(x)) both used throughout. Students must be fluent in both. |
| **Cultural anchors** | Uzbekistan's technology sector (Tashkent IT Park, UzAuto, UzbekTelecom), scientific history (Al-Biruni's calculations), economic context (GDP modeling, agricultural yield optimization). |

### 5.2 Visuals

- **Function graphs are primary** — every function analysis session requires at least one coordinate plane graph
- **Tangent line animations** — animated tangent sliding along curve showing slope changing; mandatory for derivative introduction
- **Area shading** — color-shaded area under curve for integral introduction
- **Limit approach animations** — interval shrinking, secant approaching tangent
- **Sign charts** — horizontal sign chart (++−−+) for monotonicity and concavity; required visual tool
- Mobile-legible; graph axes must have clear labels and scales

### 5.3 Content Constraints

| Constraint | Rule |
|---|---|
| **Intuition before formalism** | Every rule motivated intuitively before formal statement |
| **No "just apply the formula"** | Every formula has a derivation students must see at least once |
| **Limit notation mandatory** | Derivatives derived using lim notation in introductory session — never just "here is the power rule" |
| **Continuity before differentiability** | A function must be continuous to be differentiable — order enforced |
| **Scope boundary** | Single-variable calculus only. Partial derivatives, multivariable — university scope. |
| **Trig derivatives derived geometrically** | Sine and cosine derivatives shown from unit circle before memorized |
