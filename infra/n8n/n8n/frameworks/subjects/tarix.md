## 1. What Is This Family (The Philosophy)

Ijtimoiy Fanlari (Social Sciences) in NETS is defined by the **Memorize + Understand + Recall** model. Unlike Science or Math, where truth is observed or derived, social sciences in the national curriculum are 70% about building a robust mental database of facts (dates, names, terms, laws) and 30% about understanding systemic connections.

**The Recall-Ready Mandate:**
The primary goal of homework in this family is to build a robust knowledge base through rapid recall and understanding under pressure.
- **Memorize:** Hard facts (Who, When, Where, What).
- **Understand:** Systemic logic (Why it happened, Cause -> Effect).
- **Recall:** Performance under pressure (Phase 1 and Phase 3 drills).

**Transform Philosophy:** Textbooks are often dry lists of events or laws. NETS must transform them into **narrative experiences** (Story Mode) and **mnemonic systems** (Buzan) without diluting the factual density required for exams.

**What it is NOT about:** 
- Formula manipulation or arithmetic derivation.
- "Soft" creative expression without factual anchoring.
- Abstract modern problem-solving detached from the textbook era.

**Cognological Demand Profile:**
1. Factual Retention (Dates/Names/Terms)
2. Chronological Sequencing
3. Causal Modeling (Cause -> Effect)
4. Principle Application (Law/Economy)
5. Perspective-taking

## 3. The Content Filter (Universal Production Rules)

All subjects in this family must follow these three filters added in v0.6:

1. **Bidirectional Thinking:** Every event or principle must be tested both ways: **Event -> Cause** (Why did it happen?) and **Cause -> Prediction** (If this happens, what is the result?).
2. **Error Detection (Primary):** Mandatory every 2 sessions. Students must find the "bug" in a historical timeline, a legal argument, or an economic scenario (e.g., wrong date, wrong sequence, wrong article applied).
3. **Answer Completeness:** Claims must be supported by evidence. An answer is not "1238," but "1238, because that is when the event occurred."

## 4. Game Mechanics — Ijtimoiy Filter

### Always Primary (The "Big Three")

#### **Spaced Flashcards (THE CORE)**
- Mandatory every session. This is where dates, names, and terms live.
- **Good:** "Front: 1206 / Back: Temuchinning 'Chingizxon' unvonini olishi."
- **Bad:** "Front: Define history." (Too vague).

#### **Memory Palace (Chronology)**
- Use Registan Square or other landmarks to "place" events in chronological order.
- **Good:** "Place the 4 Timurid rulers at the minarets in order of their reign."
- **Bad:** "List the rulers."

#### **Tile Match (Pairing)**
- Connect Event <-> Date, Figure <-> Achievement, or Term <-> Definition.
- **Good:** "Match Article 1 to its Constitutional text."
- **Bad:** "Match the word to its translation." (Reserved for Languages).

### Secondary Mechanics
- **Speed Match:** Rapid-fire recall of terms/dates.
- **Story Mode:** Narrative wrap to make the era/law feel alive.
- **Why Chain:** Max 2-3 levels for historical or economic causality.

### Phase 5 Buzan Options
- **Peg System:** For memorizing lists of dates or articles.
- **Link System:** For sequential event chains.
- **Radiant Summary:** For classifying branches of law or types of economies.

### Games to AVOID
- **Blackjack / Gambling:** Removed for ethical compliance.
- **Bridge Builder / Minefield:** Too abstract for factual subjects.
- **Word Ladder:** Linguistic focus only.
- **Notebook Capture (Calculations):** Reserved for quantitative subjects.

**Dual-Catalog Rule:** Phase 3 requires ≥1 Interactive and ≥2 Default mechanics.

## 4-A. Phase 0-A Panel Construction Guide (Social Sciences)

Phase 0-A is an **8-panel visual comic** that immerses the student in the era, event, or principle BEFORE any homework begins. For Social Sciences, every panel set must feel like stepping into a historical moment or a living system (law, economy).

**Panel Length Rules:**
- Panels 1-5: **8-10 sentences each.** These are the narrative core — setting, characters, tension, discovery.
- Panels 6-8: **5-10 sentences each.** These are resolution, reflection, and hook-forward.

**Panel-by-Panel Construction Recipe:**

| Panel | Purpose | Social Science Pattern | Example (Amir Temur chapter) |
|-------|---------|----------------------|------------------------------|
| **1 — Setting** | Establish the era/place. Sensory immersion. | Open with a specific year and location. Use 2-3 sensory details (sights, sounds, smells). Name the civilization or system. | "1370-yil. Samarqand shahrining katta darvozasi oldida turibsiz. Oftob botayapti, va shahar devorlari oltin rangda porlab turibdi. Savdogarlar karvoni endi keldi — tuya qo'ng'iroqlari jaranglayapti. Bozorda ziravor hidi taralyapti. Siz buyuk voqealarning boshlanishida turibsiz..." |
| **2 — Character** | Introduce the key figure or concept. | Name the historical figure, law, or economic actor. Give 2-3 vivid traits. Show them in action, not described abstractly. | "Amir Temur — baland bo'yli, kuchli qo'lli sarkarda. U shaxmatni yaxshi ko'radi, chunki bu strategik fikrlashni o'rgatadi. Uning ko'zlari kelajakni ko'rayotgandek. Bugun u yangi qaror qabul qilmoqda..." |
| **3 — Problem** | Present the central challenge of the chapter. | State the historical problem, legal gap, or economic crisis. Make it concrete and urgent. | "Movarounnahr parchalangan. Har bir shahar o'z hokimiga ega. Savdo yo'llari xavfli. Xalq birlashtiradigan rahbar kutayapti..." |
| **4 — Struggle** | Show the difficulty of solving the problem. | Describe failed attempts, opposition, or complexity. Use specific textbook details. | "Boshqa sarkardalar ham birlashtirmoqchi bo'lgan, lekin muvaffaqiyatsizlikka uchragan. Sabab: ular faqat kuch ishlatgan. Temur boshqacha yo'l tanladi — u nafaqat qilich, balki diplomatsiya ham ishlatdi..." |
| **5 — Discovery** | The turning point. Key fact or principle revealed. | Reveal the specific date, decision, battle, article, or invention that changed things. This is the chapter's core fact. | "1370-yilda Temur Samarqandni poytaxt qilib e'lon qildi. Bu qaror hamma narsani o'zgartirdi. Nima uchun aynan Samarqand? Chunki u Ipak Yo'lining markazida joylashgan edi..." |
| **6 — Solution** | Show the outcome. | Describe what happened after the turning point. Use 2-3 concrete results with dates/names. | "Temur 35 yil ichida ulkan imperiya qurdi. Samarqand dunyoning eng go'zal shaharlaridan biriga aylandi. Olimlar, me'morlar, san'atkorlar yig'ila boshladi." |
| **7 — Reflection** | Connect to bigger meaning. | Link to national identity, legal principle, or economic lesson. Use one quote or proverb if available. | "Temur shunday degan: 'Kuch — adolatdadir.' Bu tamoyil bugungi O'zbekiston Konstitutsiyasida ham aks etgan. Nega?" |
| **8 — Hook** | Create curiosity for the homework session. | Ask 1-2 questions that the homework will answer. End with anticipation. | "Temur qanday qilib shuncha mamlakatni boshqara olgan? Uning 'Temur Tuzuklari' — bu qanday kitob? Bugun bularni bilib olasiz!" |

**Universal Panel Rules for Ijtimoiy Fanlari:**
- Every panel set MUST name at least one specific date and one specific historical figure/law/term.
- Panel 5 (Discovery) MUST contain the chapter's single most important fact.
- Panel 8 (Hook) MUST directly reference what Phase 1 will test.
- Use "Siz" (formal) throughout. Never "sen."
- No modern slang. Language should feel era-appropriate but accessible.

## 4-B. Phase 1 Format Mixing + Construction (Social Sciences)

### Mixing Rule (Mandatory)

Every Phase 1 Memory Sprint MUST use **at least 2 of the 3 allowed formats:**
1. Multiple Choice (MC)
2. True/False (T/F)
3. Yes/No/Not Given (Y/N/NG)

**No other formats in Phase 1.** No drag-and-drop, no typing, no open-ended. Phase 1 is rapid warm-up recall from the CURRENT chapter only.

### Per-Format Construction Recipes

#### Multiple Choice (MC) — Construction Recipe
- **Stem:** Always a specific factual question. Include a date, name, or term in the stem.
- **Correct answer:** Directly from the textbook. No interpretation needed.
- **Distractors (3):** Must be plausible but clearly wrong to a student who studied. Use real names/dates from DIFFERENT chapters or adjacent eras.
- **Tag:** Every MC item carries `[Bloom: L1 | PISA: L1]` (Knowledge/Recall).

**Good MC Example:**
```
Amir Temur Samarqandni poytaxt qilib qachon e'lon qildi?
A) 1336-yil
B) 1370-yil  ✓
C) 1405-yil
D) 1441-yil
[Bloom: L1 | PISA: L1]
```
**Bad MC:** "Tarix nima?" (Too vague, not from current chapter.)

#### True/False (T/F) — Construction Recipe
- **Statement:** One clear factual claim from the current chapter. Must be unambiguously true or false based on textbook.
- **If False:** The error must be a specific wrong detail (wrong date, wrong name, wrong place) — not a vague claim.
- **Balance:** Aim for 50/50 True vs. False in any session.

**Good T/F Example:**
```
"Amir Temur 1336-yilda Kesh (Shahrisabz) shahrida tug'ilgan." — To'g'ri yoki Noto'g'ri?
Javob: To'g'ri. ✓
[Bloom: L1 | PISA: L1]
```
**Good T/F (False):**
```
"Amir Temur Buxoroni poytaxt qilib tanlagan." — To'g'ri yoki Noto'g'ri?
Javob: Noto'g'ri. Samarqandni poytaxt qilgan.
[Bloom: L1 | PISA: L1]
```

#### Yes/No/Not Given (Y/N/NG) — Construction Recipe
- **Statement:** A factual claim that MAY or MAY NOT appear in the textbook chapter.
- **"Not Given"** is the hardest option — it tests whether the student remembers what the chapter actually said vs. what it did NOT say.
- **Rule:** At least 1 "Not Given" item per session to train textbook fidelity.

**Good Y/N/NG Example:**
```
"Darslikda Amir Temurning shaxmat o'ynagani haqida ma'lumot berilgan." — Ha / Yo'q / Ma'lumot berilmagan?
[Bloom: L2 | PISA: L2]
```

### Social Science Phase 1 Distribution
- **70% recall items:** Dates, names, places, terms (MC and T/F).
- **30% comprehension items:** Cause-effect or sequence verification (Y/N/NG and T/F with causal claims).

## 4-C. Phase 3 Game Construction Recipes (Social Sciences)

### Big Three — Mandatory Construction

#### 1. Spaced Flashcards — Construction Recipe

Flashcards are the **backbone** of Ijtimoiy Fanlari sessions (70% memorization mandate).

**Card Structure:**
- **Front:** One specific trigger — a date, a name, or a term. Never a full sentence question.
- **Back:** The paired fact + one vivid image cue (Buzan principle: association with visual).
- **Count:** 8-12 cards per session.
- **Sequence:** Cards should follow the chapter's chronological order on first pass, then randomize on review.

**Construction Examples:**
```
Front: 1370
Back: Amir Temur Samarqandni poytaxt qildi. 🏰 (Registon maydonini tasavvur qiling)

Front: Movarounnahr
Back: "Ikki daryo orasidagi yer" — Amudaryo va Sirdaryo orasidagi hudud. 🗺️

Front: Al-Xorazmiy
Back: Algoritm va algebra tushunchalarining asoschisi. Kitob: "Al-jabr val-muqobala." 📐

Front: Temur Tuzuklari
Back: Amir Temurning davlat boshqaruvi bo'yicha qoidalari. ⚖️ (Adolat tarozisini tasavvur qiling)
```

**Bad Flashcard:** `Front: Tell me about Temur / Back: He was a great leader.` (Too vague, no date, no image cue.)

#### 2. Memory Palace — Construction Recipe

**Structure:**
- **Landmark:** Choose a real Uzbek or world-famous landmark with 4-8 distinct "stations" (rooms, gates, towers, courtyards).
- **Stations:** Each station holds ONE event/fact in chronological order.
- **Placement rule:** Walk the student through the landmark left-to-right or entrance-to-center. Each stop has a vivid scene.
- **Count:** 4-8 stations per session.

**Construction Example (Registon Maydoni, Temuriylar):**
```
Station 1 — Ulug'bek Madrasasi eshigi: "1370 — Temur hokimiyatga keldi."
Station 2 — Ichki hovli: "1380 — Temur Eronni zabt etdi."
Station 3 — Kutubxona xonasi: "1394 — Samarqand qurilishi boshlandi."
Station 4 — Minora tepasi: "1405 — Temur vafoti."
```

**Rules:**
- Stations MUST be in chronological order.
- Each station description = 1-2 sentences with a visual anchor.
- The landmark must be culturally appropriate (no fictional places).

#### 3. Tile Match — Construction Recipe

**Structure:**
- **Layout:** 6-8 pairs. Left column = triggers (dates, names, terms). Right column = targets (events, achievements, definitions).
- **Pair types:** Figure↔Achievement, Date↔Event, Cause↔Effect, Term↔Definition.
- **Distractor rule:** No extra tiles. Every left tile has exactly one correct right tile.
- **Difficulty:** G5-6 = 6 pairs, G7-9 = 7 pairs, G10-11 = 8 pairs.

**Construction Example:**
```
LEFT (Triggers)          RIGHT (Targets)
1336                  →  Amir Temur tavalludi
1370                  →  Samarqand poytaxt bo'ldi
Al-Xorazmiy           →  Algebra asoschisi
Ibn Sino              →  "Al-Qonun fit-tib" muallifi
Ipak Yo'li            →  Sharq-G'arb savdo yo'li
Temur Tuzuklari       →  Davlat boshqaruv qoidalari
```

**Bad Tile Match:** Left: "History" → Right: "Is important." (No factual content.)

### Secondary Game Construction

#### Reaction Chain — Construction Recipe
- **Nodes:** 6-10 events in strict chronological order.
- **Each node:** A short event description (1 sentence) + a gate question the student must answer to proceed.
- **Pattern:** Event A → Gate Q → Event B → Gate Q → Event C...
- **Great for:** Dynasty successions, war sequences, reform timelines.

**Example:**
```
Node 1: "Temur 1370-yilda hokimiyatga keldi." → Gate: "Qaysi shaharda?" → "Samarqand"
Node 2: "1380-yilda Eron yurishini boshladi." → Gate: "Bu necha yil keyin?" → "10 yil"
Node 3: "1394-yilda Samarqand qurilishi boshlandi." → Gate: "Nima qurildi?" → "Bibi-Xonim masjidi"
```

#### Puzzle Lock — Construction Recipe
- **Tiles:** 6-9 sliding tiles that reconstruct a timeline, map, or dynasty tree.
- **Good for:** Silk Road routes, empire boundary maps, chronological ordering.
- **Each tile:** Contains a date + short label. Student slides to correct position.

**Example:** "Temuriylar sulolasi tartibini to'g'ri joylashtiring: Temur → Shohrux → Ulug'bek → Husayn Boyqaro"

#### Error Detection — Construction Recipe (Mandatory every 2 sessions)
- **Format:** Present a "historical document" or timeline with 1-2 deliberate errors.
- **Errors must be specific:** Wrong date, wrong name, wrong sequence — never vague claims.
- **Student task:** Find the error AND provide the correct fact.

**Example:**
```
"Amir Temur 1336-yilda Samarqandda tug'ilgan. U 1370-yilda hokimiyatga keldi."
Xato: Temur Samarqandda emas, Kesh (Shahrisabz) shahrida tug'ilgan. ✓
```

## 5. Boss Format: Recall + Explain

Ijtimoiy Bosses do not use abstract analysis; they test the ability to **retrieve and connect**.

- **Structure:** 1. Retrieve the fact (Who/When) -> 2. Explain the connection (Why/Result).
- **HP, damage, stars, and hints:** see [[UNIFIED-Buzan §5.6]].
- **Feedback:** Use TEFCAS **"Hali emas!"** (Not yet) framing.

**Good Boss Question:** "Identify the year the Constitution was adopted. List 3 key changes it introduced and explain how one affects your life today."
**Bad Boss Question:** "You are a diplomat at the UN. Write a speech about Uzbekistan's history." (Too abstract, lacks textbook anchoring).

---

## 6. Phase 4: Historical Projection

In this family, Phase 4 is **NOT a real-world application** scenario. It is **Historical Projection**.

- **Goal:** Connect the past/principle to today or to a "what if" scenario.
- **Pattern:** "This system Temur built is similar to modern X" or "What would have happened if X didn't occur?"
- **POV:** Student is a **Lead Analyst** or **Guardian of Heritage**.

**Good Example:** "Compare the Silk Road routes to the modern 'One Belt, One Road' initiative. What similarities do you see in the trade goods?"
**Bad Example:** "Act as a modern merchant and calculate your profit margins."

## Phase 7: Reflection (Social Sciences)

Phase 7 closes every session with a structured reflection that reinforces what was learned, activates metacognition, and sets up spaced repetition.

### 7.1 Session Summary (Chronological Recap)

Provide a **3-5 sentence summary** of the session's content in chronological order. For history: timeline recap. For law/economics: principle chain recap.

**Construction Pattern:**
```
"Bugun Siz quyidagilarni o'rgandingiz:
1. [Earliest event/concept with date]
2. [Middle event/concept with date]
3. [Latest event/concept with date]
Bular bir-biriga bog'liq, chunki [1-sentence causal link]."
```

**Example:**
```
"Bugun Siz quyidagilarni o'rgandingiz:
1. 1336-yil — Amir Temur Keshda tug'ildi.
2. 1370-yil — Samarqandni poytaxt qilib e'lon qildi.
3. 1405-yil — Temur vafot etdi, lekin imperiya davom etdi.
Bular bir-biriga bog'liq, chunki Temurning tug'ilishidan boshlab har bir qadami buyuk imperiyaga olib keldi."
```

### 7.2 Metacognitive Prompts (3 questions)

Ask exactly **3 metacognitive questions** to activate self-assessment:

1. **Retrieval check:** "Bugun o'rgangan sanalaringizdan qaysi biri eng qiyin edi?"
2. **Connection check:** "Qaysi voqea boshqa voqeaga sabab bo'lganini tushundingizmi?"
3. **Confidence check:** "Agar do'stingiz so'rasa, bugungi mavzuni 2 gapda tushuntira olasizmi?"

These are display-only prompts (student reflects internally or taps a confidence level). They do NOT require typed answers.

### 7.3 BOST Goal Recall (Mandatory)

Every session stores a learning goal at the start (Phase 0-B). Phase 7 MUST recall it:

**Template:**
```
"Bugun Siz [stored goal from Phase 0-B] ni bilmoqchi edingiz. Bildingizmi?"
```

**Examples:**
```
"Bugun Siz Amir Temur qachon hokimiyatga kelganini bilmoqchi edingiz. Bildingizmi?"
"Bugun Siz Konstitutsiyaning 1-moddasini bilmoqchi edingiz. Bildingizmi?"
```

The student taps Yes/No/Partially. This closes the goal loop.

### 7.4 Spaced Repetition Schedule

Display the next review timing based on session performance:

| Performance | Next Review | Message |
|-------------|-------------|---------|
| ≥80% | 3 kun keyin | "Ajoyib! 3 kundan keyin qayta takrorlaymiz." |
| 60-79% | 2 kun keyin | "Yaxshi! 2 kundan keyin qayta ko'rib chiqamiz." |
| <60% | Ertaga | "Tashvishlanmang! Ertaga yana mashq qilamiz." |

### 7.5 Closing Line

**Score ≥60% — Third Renaissance line (celebratory, identity-affirming):**
```
"Siz bugun ajdodlarimiz merosini o'rgandingiz. Uchinchi Renessans — Siz bilan boshlanadi! 🌟"
```

**Score <60% — Encouraging neutral (no shame, forward-looking):**
```
"Har bir buyuk olim ham bir vaqtlar o'rganuvchi bo'lgan. Ertaga yana birga davom etamiz!"
```

**Rules:**
- Never use negative language ("You failed," "Not good enough").
- Always use "Siz" (formal).
- The Third Renaissance reference is specific to Social Sciences — it connects learning to national legacy.

## 1. What Tarix Teaches (Unified)

History in Grades 5-6 is the study of **causality, sequence, and foundational identity**. It transforms history from a dry list of dates into a **Chronological Narrative** where students build a mental map of human progress.

- **Factual Foundation:** 70% of homework is about memorizing dates, names, places, and key events from the textbook.
- **Causal Linking:** 30% is about understanding why events happened (Cause -> Effect).
- **Recall Training:** Homework must build rapid recall under pressure — dates, names, sequences.
- **Narrative Focus:** Use Story Mode to make the ancient world feel alive, not as a list of abstract facts.

**What Tarix is NOT about:**
- Complex historiographical debate or modern political analysis.
- Real-world "expert" roleplay (e.g., "You are a modern diplomat").
- Arithmetic or formula work.

**Core production rule:** Factual retention comes first. A student must know **what** happened and **when** before they can explain **why**.

## 4-A. Phase 0-A Panel Construction Guide (Tarix G5-6)

Phase 0-A for Tarix G5-6 is an **8-panel visual comic** focused on **memory and chronology**. The goal is sensory immersion — make the student FEEL the ancient world before testing recall.

**Panel Length Rules:**
- Panels 1-5: **8-10 sentences each.** Narrative core with sensory detail.
- Panels 6-8: **5-10 sentences each.** Resolution and curiosity hook.

**Tarix G5-6 Panel Priorities:**
- **Sensory immersion is mandatory.** Every panel set must include at least 2 sensory details (smell, sound, sight, texture).
- **Chronology focus:** Panel 5 (Discovery) must anchor the chapter's KEY DATE.
- **Simple language:** G5-6 vocabulary. Short sentences. No academic jargon.

**Panel-by-Panel Recipe (Tarix-specific):**

| Panel | Tarix G5-6 Focus | Example (Ancient Khorezm chapter) |
|-------|------------------|-----------------------------------|
| **1 — Setting** | Open with year + place. Use 2-3 sensory details. Make the student "arrive" at the location. | "Miloddan avvalgi VI asr. Siz Xorazm shahri darvozasi oldida turibsiz. Issiq shamol yuzingizga urilayapti. Darvoza oldida savdogarlar tuya va eshaklar bilan navbatda turibdi. Devorlar loydan qurilgan — baland va mustahkam. Ichkarida bozor shovqini eshitilayapti. Bu eng qadimgi shaharlardan biri..." |
