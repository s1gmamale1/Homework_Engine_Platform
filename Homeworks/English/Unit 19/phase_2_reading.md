# Phase 2: Reading / Story Mode

Dilshod and his classmates are preparing for their end-of-term school trip to Zaamin National Park. Mr. Yusupov, their science teacher, **gives a lesson** about the park's beautiful wildlife and explains the strict rules. "You **have to** wear comfortable trainers," he says, "because we will be **going on a nature walk** through the mountains." Dilshod writes everything down carefully. He knows he **has to** **take notes** to remember the important details. Some students ask about the evening activities and the camp rules. "We are staying at a special activity centre," the teacher explains. "You **don't have to** go to bed very early, but you **have to** wake up at 7:00 a.m. the next morning. If you want to enjoy the trip, you **have to** follow this schedule."

The next day, the large bus arrives at the camp. The students are very excited to explore. First, they are **going round a museum** of local history near the park entrance. Dilshod reads all the information signs carefully because they will **do a project** about the trip when they return. "We **have to** **study from a textbook** sometimes, but learning outside in nature is much better," his friend Aziz says. In the afternoon, they have free time, and the camp offers many different activities. Students can try **canoeing** on the small, quiet lake, or they can go **sailing**. "You **don't have to** do the water sports if you are afraid," Mr. Yusupov tells the group. "You can stay on land and help the guides with **cooking on a fire**." Dilshod decides to try the boats, even though he is a bit nervous. He puts on his life jacket because everyone **has to** wear one near the water.

By the end of the three-day trip, everyone is tired but very happy. On the last night, the camp organises a fun farewell party. The students spend the evening **dancing at a disco** under the bright stars. The next week back at school, they **have to** present their projects to the class. Dilshod is worried because he wants to **get marks** that are high enough to **pass a test** for the school's advanced science club. He **does homework** every evening to make his presentation perfect. He remembers his teacher's words from the trip: he **doesn't have to** be perfect, he just **has to** do his absolute best. When he finishes his speech about the Zaamin ecosystem, Mr. Yusupov smiles proudly. The trip was not just about having fun in the mountains; it was about learning to make good choices and understanding when you **have to** follow important rules.
