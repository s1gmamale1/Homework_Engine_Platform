# Phase 5: Consolidation

We are going to use a special memory trick to lock everything you've learned into your brain before the Final Boss. Because this unit teaches two different grammar rules (obligation vs. choice) and two vocabulary sets (school and trip activities), we will build a **Memory Palace**.

Close your eyes and imagine we are standing in the centre of **Registan Square** in Samarkand. We will place four strange, unforgettable images around the square to help you remember the rules forever.

---

### Station 1: The Main Gate (Registan Darvozasi)
**Concept:** `have to` = Obligation (You have no choice)
**Image:** Imagine a giant, strict teacher standing right in the middle of the huge gate. He is holding a massive, heavy book. To walk through the gate, you **have to** *study from a textbook*. There is no other way inside!

### Station 2: The Open Courtyard (Markaziy Maydon)
**Concept:** `don't have to` = No Obligation (You have a choice)
**Image:** You step into the wide, sunny courtyard. A student is *dancing at a disco* with no backpack and no worries. The student shouts, "You **don't have to** *do homework* here!" The space is completely open, showing your freedom to choose.

### Station 3: The Left Minaret (Chap Minora)
**Concept:** School Collocations (pass a test, get marks, take notes)
**Image:** Look at the tall minaret on your left. It is not made of bricks—it is made of stacked exam papers and notebooks! A student is climbing to the top by *taking notes* on the walls. At the very top, a golden star waits for those who *get high marks* and *pass a test*.

### Station 4: The Right Minaret (O'ng Minora)
**Concept:** Trip Activities (canoeing, sailing, nature walk)
**Image:** Now look at the minaret on your right. It is completely filled with blue water! Students are *canoeing* and *sailing* out of the top windows. At the bottom of the minaret, a beautiful forest grows out of the stone, where students are going on a *nature walk* and *cooking on a fire*.
