### Question 3 (Medium)
`[UZ-ENG8-UNIT19-03] | Bloom: L4 | PISA: L2 | Damage: -20 HP`

**Task:** A student wrote this sentence in their essay:
*"We haven't to pay for the museum tickets because they are free."*

Find the grammar error, explain *why* it is wrong, and rewrite the sentence correctly.

<details>
<summary><b>Hint 1 (-5 HP)</b></summary>
Look closely at the verb "haven't to". Is this how we make "have to" negative?
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Hint 2 (-5 HP)</b></summary>
To make the negative form of "have to" in the present simple, we need a helping verb (do/does).
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Hint 3 (-5 HP)</b></summary>
The error is "haven't to". Change it to "don't...".
</details>

**Model Answer:** 
The error is "haven't to". We do not use "haven't to" to show no obligation; we must use the present simple auxiliary verb "don't" or "doesn't" before "have to". 
Correct sentence: "We **don't have to** pay for the museum tickets because they are free."
**Failure Response:** Hali emas! "Have to" works like a normal present simple verb, so its negative form requires "don't" or "doesn't" + "have to". Review Panel 2 in the Preview.

### Question 5 (Hard)
`[UZ-ENG8-UNIT19-05] | Bloom: L5 | PISA: L3 | Damage: -30 HP`

**Task:** You are a student attending an amazing summer camp in Zaamin National Park. Write an **80-word email** to your parents telling them about your experience. Your email must include:
1. Two different **trip activities** you are doing (e.g., canoeing, dancing at a disco).
2. Two **rules** at the camp: one thing you are forced to do (`have to`), and one thing you are not forced to do (`don't have to`).
3. At least one **school collocation** (e.g., take notes, get marks, do homework).

Your paragraph must flow naturally like a real letter to your parents, not just a random list of grammar sentences!

<details>
<summary><b>Hint 1 (-5 HP)</b></summary>
Start with "Dear Mum and Dad, I am having a great time at camp..." then describe the activities you did today.
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Hint 2 (-5 HP)</b></summary>
Next, add the rules: "The rules here are strict. We have to... but luckily we don't have to..."
</details>

<details>
<summary><b>Hint 3 (-5 HP)</b></summary>
Finish by mentioning how this trip will help you at school: "I am taking lots of notes so I can get good marks..."
</details>

**Model Answer:**
"Dear Mum and Dad,
I am having a wonderful time at the summer camp in Zaamin! The trip activities are amazing. Yesterday, we went canoeing on the lake, and tonight we are cooking on a fire with our guides. The camp has some strict rules, though. We have to wake up at 7:00 a.m. every day, but luckily, we don't have to do any homework here! However, I am still taking notes during our nature walks because I want to get good marks when we do a project back at school.
Love,
Aziz"

**Failure Response:** Hali emas! Your paragraph must contain the required word count (~80 words) and combine the grammar and vocabulary naturally. Check if you included two trip activities, one `have to` rule, one `don't have to` rule, and a school collocation like "take notes" or "get marks". Read the model answer for an example of good flow.
