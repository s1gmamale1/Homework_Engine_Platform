# Phase 4: Real-Life Challenge

You are a **CoderDojo mentor** in Tashkent. You are organising an exciting end-of-term educational trip for your younger coding students to a robotics activity centre in the mountains. Your students are very excited to go on a nature walk and do a project, but they need to know the camp's strict schedule before they leave. Your task is to write an official information sheet that explains the rules clearly. You need to make sure the students understand what is absolutely necessary and what is optional, so there is no confusion during the trip. Your accuracy meets Global Standards.

### Challenge Questions

**Q1.** Write 3 sentences for your students explaining the rules from the schedule above. You must use **have to** twice and **don't have to** once, along with the vocabulary from the unit (like *take notes* or *canoeing*).
`[Bloom: L3 | PISA: L2]`

**Model Answer:**
"You **have to** wake up early at 7:00 a.m. During the morning robotics class, you **have to take notes** for your final presentation. In the afternoon, you **don't have to** go **canoeing** if you want to stay inside."

**Q2.** A student reads your schedule and asks: "Teacher, does 'you don't have to go canoeing' mean we are not allowed to go?" How do you explain the difference between having a choice and not being allowed? Explain how "don't have to" works in this context.
`[Bloom: L4 | PISA: L3]`

**Model Answer:**
"No, it means you have a choice. 'You **don't have to** go canoeing' means it is not an obligation (UZ: bajarish shart emas), but you can still do it if you want to have fun. If you were not allowed, I would say 'you must not go'."

**Q3.** The camp director gives you a new update: "The students are not forced to study from a textbook on Sunday, but they are required to do a project in teams." Translate this into professional, student-friendly English using your target grammar.
`[Bloom: L5 | PISA: L3]`

**Model Answer:**
"On Sunday, you **don't have to study from a textbook**, but you **have to do a project** with your team."
