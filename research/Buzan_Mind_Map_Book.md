## 1. Core Definitions & Laws

### Radiant Thinking
Radiant Thinking is the associative thought process that originates from a central point and radiates outward in all directions simultaneously. It mimics the physical structure of the brain's neuronal networks. Unlike linear note-taking, it reflects how the brain naturally processes information: through non-linear, multi-dimensional associations.

### The Laws of Mind Mapping
To maximize brain engagement (engaging both hemispheres), Buzan defined strict laws:
1.  **Central Image:** Start with a colorful image in the center (landscape orientation). This focuses the brain and serves as the anchor.
2.  **Organic Branches:** Use curved lines radiating from the center. Thickest at the center, thinning toward the tips. Straight lines are "dead"; organic curves are "alive."
3.  **Hierarchy:** Use Basic Ordering Ideas (BOIs) for main branches to establish a clear conceptual hierarchy.
4.  **Keywords (One Per Line):** Use exactly one keyword per branch. This allows for maximum flexibility and further associations.
5.  **Emphasis:** Use different sizes of branches, fonts, and bolding to create visual hierarchy.
6.  **Association:** Use arrows, colors, and codes to show links between different parts of the map.
7.  **Images & Colors:** Use icons, symbols, and at least 3 colors per map to stimulate visual memory and creativity.

## 2. Book Summary: Techniques & Problem Solving

### Core Aspects
*The Mind Map Book* serves as a "user manual for the brain," moving from the theory of brain function to the practical application of visual thinking.

*   **Group Mind Mapping:** A collaborative process where individuals brainstorm individually, then combine their maps into a master group map. This pools collective intelligence and ensures all perspectives are "seen."
*   **Problem Solving:** 
    *   **Mapping the Problem:** Radiate "Who, What, Where, When, Why, How" from a central problem image.
    *   **Holistic View:** By seeing the "whole picture," users identify hidden connections and root causes that linear analysis misses.
    *   **Decision Making:** Comparing two maps or using a "decision map" to weigh weights/probabilities on radiant branches.

## 3. Mapping to NETS Project

### Phase 5 (Consolidation): Radiant Summaries
*   **Application:** Replace or augment the standard "Memory Palace" mnemonic with a "Radiant Summary" task.
*   **Implementation:** Students are shown a partially completed Mind Map of the lesson's key concepts. They must drag-and-drop the correct "Keywords" or "Images" onto the appropriate branches (consolidating hierarchy and association).
*   **Goal:** Moving from "Remembering" (Phase 1) to "Creating/Evaluating" (Phase 5) by showing how all concepts in the lesson connect radiantly.

### Notebook Capture: Evaluation Rubrics
*   **Task:** Students draw a Mind Map in their physical notebook and photograph it.
*   **AI Vision Rubric (Tier 3 Evaluation):**
    1.  **Centrality:** Is there a central image? (Presence detection)
    2.  **Radiant Structure:** Are branches connected to the center? (Graph topology check)
    3.  **Hierarchy:** Are there clear main branches (BOIs) and sub-branches?
    4.  **Multi-modality:** Presence of icons/sketches alongside keywords.
    5.  **Color Usage:** Identification of at least 3 distinct color zones (requires color-sensitive vision analysis).

### UI/UX: Radiant Navigation
*   **Navigation Maps:** Instead of a linear sidebar, the "Learning Journey" for a subject (e.g., G7 Biology) should be presented as a Radiant Map. The current chapter is the "active branch," while past/future chapters are visible but faded, showing the "Growth of Knowledge."
*   **Content Visualization:** When a student enters "Story Mode," the narrative unfolds radiantly rather than in a linear scroll, allowing students to explore sub-plots or deeper "Why" branches without losing the central theme.

## 4. Specific Use Cases for NETS Students

### G7 Biology: Kingdom Animalia Classification
*   **Central Image:** An iconic animal (e.g., a Lion or an Amoeba).
*   **Main Branches (BOIs):** Invertebrates vs. Vertebrates.
*   **Sub-branches:** Phyla (Mollusca, Arthropoda, Chordata, etc.).
*   **Leaf Nodes:** Specific characteristics (e.g., "Gills," "Exoskeleton," "Warm-blooded") or local examples (e.g., "Karakurt spider," "Amu Darya fish").

### G7 History: The Silk Road Impact
*   **Central Image:** A camel caravan or the Registan Square.
*   **Branches:** Economics (Trade goods), Culture (Religion/Science), Geography (Main cities: Samarkand, Bukhara, Khiva), Technology (Paper, Silk).
*   **Objective:** Visualizing the multi-directional flow of influence.
