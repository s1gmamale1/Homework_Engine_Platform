# Employer & University Expectations and Skills Gaps: Uzbekistan (2024–2026)

This document summarizes the current landscape of expectations for students entering higher education and the workforce in Uzbekistan, based on government reports, international assessments, and sociological surveys.

---

## 1. Employer Expectations & Reported Skills Gaps
Uzbekistan's transition to a market-driven economy (Strategy 2030) has highlighted a "qualitative mismatch" between education and industry needs.

### Key Skills Gaps (The "Soft" and "Hard" Divide)
- **Critical Thinking (Cognitive Skills):** Employers report a heavy deficit in the application of theoretical knowledge to non-routine problems. PISA 2022 data confirms only 19% of 15-year-olds reach basic math modeling proficiency.
- **English Proficiency:** Identified as the #1 technical barrier. 49% of formal sector employers cite language barriers as a primary hiring difficulty. There is a massive demand for **English for Specific Purposes (ESP)** in IT and engineering.
- **Advanced IT Literacy:** Moving beyond basic office apps. There is a "talent war" for software engineering, data analytics, and AI integration. 74% of professionals use AI tools, but often without formal strategic training.
- **Communication & Socio-emotional Skills:** Ranked as the #1 missing attribute in entry-level hires. This includes professional teamwork, accepting responsibility, and constructive feedback.

---

## 2. University Expectations for Incoming Students
Top-tier universities in Uzbekistan (e.g., New Uzbekistan University, AKFA, Inha) have shifted their selection criteria toward international benchmarks.

### Current Trends
- **International Validation:** Universities now place extreme value on international certificates. Possession of **SAT, IELTS (6.5+), or A-Levels** often grants maximum scores or complete exemptions from entrance exams.
- **Shift from Rote to Competency:** Entrance exams (Cambridge-designed) now test logical reasoning and the ability to solve complex, non-standard problems rather than memorized textbook facts.
- **"Test First, Select Later":** A policy change (2024/25) requires students to take entrance exams before choosing their institution, forcing realistic alignment between score and goal.
- **Unified State Examination:** Transitioning toward a single, internationally accredited graduation/entrance exam system.

## 3. Government Reports & Official Surveys
Key data points from official and international agency assessments in Uzbekistan.

| Report / Source | Key Insight |
| :--- | :--- |
| **World Bank (2024): The Skills Road** | 11 years of schooling equals only 9.2 years of actual learning. Skills gaps, not lack of jobs, are the primary hurdle. |
| **PISA 2022 (Uzbekistan Results)** | Baseline score: Math (364), Reading (336), Science (355). Significant deficit in logical deduction and scientific inquiry. |
| **MoHESI / World Bank TVET Survey (2024)** | 37% of vocational graduates struggle to find work due to outdated curricula and lack of hands-on experience. |
| **EY Salary & Compensation Survey (2024/25)** | 66% of firms are expanding but face high turnover due to "soft skill" deficits and poor internal communication. |
| **Uzbekistan – 2030 Strategy** | Mandates 100% of vocational curricula be modernized to meet international PISA and CEFR standards. |

---

## 4. Strategic Targets for 2030
- **Enrollment:** Increase higher education enrollment to 50% of school leavers.
- **Ranking:** Aim to have at least 10 local universities in the Top 1000 world ranking.
- **Digital Portfolio:** Implementation of the `erp.portfolio` system to track student "soft skills" and extracurricular achievements officially.
- **PISA Improvement:** National programs like *Eduten* (AI-gamified) are being piloted to bridge the math gap, showing a 16% skill increase in early trials.
