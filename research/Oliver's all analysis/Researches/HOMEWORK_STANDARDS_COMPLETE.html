Foundation

📚 Principle 1: Textbook is Source of Truth

Homework MUST NOT change core concepts from textbooks. It MUST adapt delivery method to active, gamified, AI-personalized learning.

DO:

  • Preserve core concepts exactly
  • Add narrative layer for engagement
  • Convert passive examples to active problems
  • Embed Uzbek cultural context

DON'T:

  • Change mathematical formulas
  • Alter historical dates
  • Modify grammar rules
  • Remove prerequisite knowledge

🎯 Principle 2: Flow State is Mandatory

Based on Csikszentmihalyi (1990), every student MUST be maintained in the flow channel:

AI adjusts difficulty every 3-5 questions:
  IF success_rate < 60%:
    difficulty_tier -= 1
    provide_scaffolding = True
  ELIF success_rate > 90%:
    difficulty_tier += 1
    reduce_hints = True
  ELSE:
    maintain_current_tier()

Target: 70-80% success rate (challenging but achievable)

💎 Principle 3: Intrinsic > Extrinsic

All rewards MUST celebrate LEARNING PROGRESS, not compliance.

✅ Intrinsic (DO) ❌ Extrinsic (DON'T)
"You mastered fractions!" "You logged in 7 days!"
"Your reasoning improved!" "You clicked 100 times!"
"You helped a classmate!" "You watched an ad!"

STANDARD: Zero pay-to-win mechanics. Zero real-money purchases.

Section 2

The 7-Step Learning Journey

Every homework session across ALL subjects and grades follows this structure (20-30 minutes total)

01

Memory Sprint

⏱️ 2 minutes

Purpose: Activate prior knowledge, prime neural pathways, connect old → new

Research Basis: Retrieval Practice (Roediger & Karpicke, 2006) - active recall strengthens memory traces 300% more than re-reading.

STANDARDS:

Element Specification
Duration Exactly 60 seconds (visible countdown)
Question Count 5-8 questions
Content Source PREVIOUS chapters ONLY (not current lesson)
Question Type Pure recall - NO multiple choice for Grades 3+
Hints NONE (this is recall, not learning)
XP Award 100 XP per correct + 10 XP streak bonus

Adaptation Rules:

IF accuracy >= 80% → Student ready for new content
IF accuracy 60-79% → Proceed but flag for teacher review
IF accuracy < 60% → System routes to remediation BEFORE new content

Subject Examples:

Mathematics (Grade 5 - Fractions):
Q: What is the formula for calculating the area of a triangle?
A: A = ½ × base × height

Q: Convert ¾ to a decimal.
A: 0.75

Science (Grade 7 - Photosynthesis):
Q: What gas do plants absorb from the air?
A: Carbon dioxide (CO2)

Q: What do plants produce during photosynthesis?
A: Glucose (sugar) and oxygen
02

Story Mode

⏱️ 5-7 minutes

Purpose: Deliver new content through narrative, leverage brain's natural story processing

Research Basis: Narrative retention is 22x higher than abstract presentation (Willingham, 2004).

STANDARDS:

Element Specification
Duration 5-7 minutes total
Segments 3 segments × 90 seconds each
Comprehension Checks 1-2 questions between each segment
Media Format AI-generated video OR illustrated panels
Cultural Integration MUST feature Uzbek locations, names, scenarios

Structure:

SEGMENT 1 (90 sec): Story introduction + concept preview → Comprehension Check
SEGMENT 2 (90 sec): Concept development with examples → Comprehension Check
SEGMENT 3 (90 sec): Real-world connection → Transition to Game Breaks

Grade-Level Narrative Framework:

Grade Semester Narrative Cultural Elements
3 "The Young Explorer of Samarkand" Registan, Silk Road, traditional crafts
4 "The Silk Road Expedition" Caravans, trade, cultural exchange
5 "The Cotton Harvest Challenge" Agriculture, family work, rural life
6 "Building the Registan" Architecture, mathematics in design
7 "The Amir Temur Campaign" History, strategy, leadership
8 "Modern Tashkent" Urban life, technology, progress

Example Adaptation (Mathematics - Grade 5, Fractions):

Textbook Concept: "Adding fractions with like denominators"

Story Adaptation:
"Alisher and his grandmother are preparing plov for a family gathering.
The recipe calls for ¾ cup of rice for 4 people. But 8 people are coming!

'How much rice do we need now?' his grandmother asks.

Alisher thinks: 'If 4 people need ¾ cup, then 8 people need...
¾ + ¾ = 6/4 = 1½ cups!'

'Correct!' his grandmother smiles. 'Now show me how you calculated it.'"

Real-Life Challenge

⏱️ 3-5 minutes

Purpose: Transfer learning to authentic contexts, develop cross-subject reasoning

Research Basis: Finland's Phenomenon-Based Learning (Silander, 2015) - 1-2 PISA level increase in transfer ability.

STANDARDS:

Element Specification
Duration 3-5 minutes
Context MUST be authentic real-world scenario
Cross-Subject SHOULD integrate 2+ subjects when possible
XP Award 400 XP for complete solution
Work Shown REQUIRED - student must show reasoning

Example (Mathematics + Science, Grade 5):

Scenario: Family trip planning from Tashkent to Bukhara

Math Skills: Distance calculation, fuel consumption, cost calculation
Science Skills: Desert climate effects, heat and engine efficiency
Geography: Map reading, city locations

Problem:
- Distance: 270 km
- Car uses 8 liters per 100 km
- Fuel costs 6,500 sum per liter

Questions:
1. How many liters of fuel needed?
2. What's the total fuel cost?
3. Why does desert climate affect fuel efficiency?

Consolidation

⏱️ 2-3 minutes

Purpose: Lock in learning before assessment, reduce cognitive load

Research Basis: Method of Loci (Maguire et al., 2003) - 300% retention improvement with mnemonic systems.

Consolidation Options:

Element Specification

  → "What would help you understand this better?"

1

Tile Match

Dual Coding Theory (Paivio, 1986)
📈 Expected Gain: +25-40% recognition

Purpose: Visual-verbal association for rapid concept recognition

Complete Mechanics:

1. INITIAL STATE:
   - Grid displays 8-12 face-down tiles
   - Timer starts at 2:30 (visible countdown)
   - Score: 0 XP

2. PLAYER ACTION:
   - Click first tile → reveals content
   - Click second tile → reveals content
   - System evaluates match

3. MATCH EVALUATION:
   IF match correct:
     - Tiles animate (fade out)
     - +100 XP displayed
     - Play success sound (C major chord, 200ms)
     - Timer +3 seconds bonus (max 3:00)
   ELSE:
     - Tiles shake (3x, 100ms each)
     - Tiles return face-down
     - Timer -5 seconds
     - Play gentle thud sound

4. WIN CONDITION:
   - All pairs matched before timer = 0
   - Award bonus: +100 XP for perfect completion

5. LOSS CONDITION:
   - Timer reaches 0 with pairs remaining
   - Award partial XP: 50 XP per completed pair

Accessibility Requirements:

  • Color-blind mode: Add patterns in addition to colors
  • Screen reader: Announce tile position and content
  • Motor impairment: Extended timer option (+30 seconds)

Subject Examples:

Mathematics (Fractions):
Tile A: "½"          Tile B: [Visual: 1 of 2 parts shaded]
Tile C: "⅓"          Tile D: [Visual: 1 of 3 parts shaded]
Tile E: "¼"          Tile F: [Visual: 1 of 4 parts shaded]

Science (Photosynthesis):
Tile A: "Chloroplast"    Tile B: [Diagram: plant cell]
Tile C: "Stomata"        Tile D: [Diagram: leaf underside]
Tile E: "Glucose"        Tile F: [Chemical formula: C₆H₁₂O₆]

Reading (Literary Devices):
Tile A: "Metaphor"       Tile B: "The classroom was a zoo"
Tile C: "Simile"         Tile D: "Her smile was like sunshine"
Tile E: "Personification" Tile F: "The wind whispered secrets"
2

Spaced Flashcards

Forgetting Curve (Ebbinghaus, 1885) + Spaced Repetition
📈 Expected Gain: +50-70% retention

Purpose: Long-term retention through spaced repetition algorithm

SM-2 Spacing Algorithm:

Initial State:
  - Card deck: 10-20 cards from previous chapters
  - Each card has: ease_factor (default 2.5), interval (days), repetitions

Player Self-Rates:
  - "Again" (wrong) → interval = 0, ease_factor -= 0.2
  - "Hard" (difficult) → interval = interval × 1.2, ease_factor -= 0.15
  - "Good" (correct) → interval = interval × 2.5, ease_factor unchanged
  - "Easy" (effortless) → interval = interval × 3.5, ease_factor += 0.15

Next Review Date:
  - interval = 0 → Review today
  - interval = 1 → Review tomorrow
  - interval = 3 → Review in 3 days
  - interval = 7 → Review in 1 week
  - interval = 14 → Review in 2 weeks
  - interval = 30+ → Review in 1 month

UI Requirements:

  • Card flip animation (300ms, ease-in-out)
  • Self-rating buttons color-coded (red, orange, green, blue)
  • Progress indicator: "5 cards remaining today"
3

Memory Sprint

Retrieval Practice (Roediger & Karpicke, 2006)
📈 Expected Gain: Automaticity building

Purpose: Rapid activation of prior knowledge

Complete Mechanics:

SETUP:
  - Select 5-8 questions from PREVIOUS chapters
  - Questions tagged by: chapter, difficulty, skill_type
  - Ensure coverage: 2 recall, 2 understanding, 2 application

EXECUTION:
  - Timer: 60 seconds (large, visible countdown)
  - Question appears one at a time
  - Student types answer (no multiple choice for Grades 3+)
  - Immediate feedback: ✓ or ✗
  - Streak counter updates live

SCORING:
  - Base: 100 XP per correct
  - Streak bonus: +10 XP × current_streak
  - Time bonus: +10 XP per 5 seconds remaining

Example Session:

Q1: "What is ½ + ½?" → Student: "1" → ✓ (Streak: 1, XP: 100)
Q2: "Convert ¾ to decimal" → Student: "0.75" → ✓ (Streak: 2, XP: 210)
Q3: "What is ⅓ × 3?" → Student: "1" → ✓ (Streak: 3, XP: 330)
Q4: "½ ÷ 2 = ?" → Student: "¼" → ✓ (Streak: 4, XP: 460)
Q5: "2/5 + 1/5 = ?" → Student: "3/5" → ✓ (Streak: 5, XP: 600)

Final: 600 XP + 50 (time bonus) = 650 XP
4

Mystery Box

Interleaving (Rohrer & Taylor, 2007)
📈 Expected Gain: +43% transfer

Purpose: Interleaved practice for problem type discrimination

Complete Mechanics:

SETUP:
  - 3-5 closed boxes displayed
  - Each box contains problem from DIFFERENT category
  - Categories mixed: current chapter + 2-3 previous chapters

PLAYER FLOW:
  For each box:
    1. Click box → box opens with animation
    2. Problem revealed
    3. FIRST: Student identifies problem type (dropdown)
    4. THEN: Student solves problem
    5. Submit → feedback on both type ID and solution

SCORING:
  - Correct type ID: +50 XP
  - Correct solution: +150 XP
  - Both correct: +50 bonus XP
  - Total per box: 250 XP maximum
5

Adaptive Quiz

Item Response Theory (IRT)
📈 Expected Gain: +30% measurement accuracy

Purpose: Precise ability measurement through IRT-based adaptation

IRT Implementation:

Question Bank Requirements:
  - Each question pre-calibrated with:
    - difficulty (θ): -3.0 to +3.0
    - discrimination (α): 0.5 to 2.0
    - guessing (c): 0.1 to 0.25

Adaptation Algorithm:
  1. Start with θ = 0 (grade-level expectation)
  2. Present question with difficulty ≈ current θ
  3. Student responds:
     IF correct: θ += 0.3 (increase estimated ability)
     IF incorrect: θ -= 0.3 (decrease estimated ability)
  4. Select next question with difficulty ≈ new θ
  5. Repeat for 5-7 questions
  6. Final θ = student's ability estimate

ABILITY INTERPRETATION:
  θ < -2.0: Significantly below grade level
  θ -1.0 to -2.0: Below grade level
  θ -0.5 to +0.5: At grade level
  θ +0.5 to +1.5: Above grade level
  θ > +1.5: Significantly above grade level
6

Why Chain

Elaborative Interrogation (Pressley et al., 1992)
📈 Expected Gain: +2 PISA levels

Purpose: Build causal reasoning through Socratic dialogue

AI Dialogue Pattern:

Level 1 (Surface):
  AI: "Why do plants need sunlight?"
  Student: "To grow."
  AI: "✓ Good start! But WHY does sunlight help them grow?"

Level 2 (Mechanism):
  Student: "Sunlight gives them energy."
  AI: "✓✓ Excellent! What do plants DO with that energy?"

Level 3 (Process):
  Student: "They make food from CO2 and water."
  AI: "✓✓✓ Perfect! You've described photosynthesis!"

SCORING:
  - Each level completed: +150 XP
  - Complete all 5 levels: +250 bonus XP
  - Hint used: -10 XP per hint

AI RESPONSE REQUIREMENTS:
  - NEVER give direct answer
  - ALWAYS acknowledge partial correctness
  - ALWAYS probe one level deeper
  - Use student's words in follow-up questions
7

Sentence Fill

Cloze Testing (Taylor, 1953)
📈 Expected Gain: +35% vocabulary

Purpose: Deep comprehension through cloze processing

Passage Generation:

Source: Current chapter text (Story Mode or textbook excerpt)

Blank Selection Rules:
  - Remove every 5th word (Grades 8-11)
  - Remove every 4th word (Grades 5-7)
  - Remove every 3rd word (Grades 2-4)
  - NEVER remove: Proper nouns, numbers, key formulas
  - ALWAYS keep first and last sentence intact

Word Bank Generation:
  Grades 2-4: Correct answers + 3 distractors per blank
  Grades 5-7: Correct answers + 2 distractors per blank
  Grades 8-11: No word bank (free recall)

SCORING:
  - Correct fill: +100 XP
  - First-attempt correct: +25 bonus XP
  - All blanks correct: +100 bonus XP

Real-Life Challenge

Phenomenon-Based Learning (Silander, 2015)
📈 Expected Gain: +1-2 PISA levels

Purpose: Transfer learning to authentic contexts

Challenge Structure:

Phase 1 - Context Setting (30 seconds):
  - Present authentic scenario
  - Include relevant data, images, maps
  - State clear objective

Phase 2 - Problem Analysis (60 seconds):
  - Student identifies: What do I know? What do I need?
  - Student selects: Which concepts/tools apply?

Phase 3 - Solution Construction (90 seconds):
  - Student works through multi-step solution
  - Must show reasoning (text, diagram, or calculation)

Phase 4 - Reflection (30 seconds):
  - "How does this connect to what you learned?"
  - "Where else could you use this?"

SCORING RUBRIC:
  | Component | Points | XP |
  |-----------|--------|-----|
  | Correct identification of concepts | 25% | 100 |
  | Correct solution process | 50% | 200 |
  | Correct final answer | 25% | 100 |
  | Total | 100% | 400 |

Final Boss

📈 Expected Gain: +2 PISA levels in 6mo

Purpose: Mastery assessment under productive struggle

Boss Battle Flow:

INITIALIZATION:
  - Display boss avatar and HP bar
  - Show combo counter, hint counter

QUESTION PRESENTATION (5-7 questions):
  IF correct:
    - Calculate damage: 10/20/30 HP
