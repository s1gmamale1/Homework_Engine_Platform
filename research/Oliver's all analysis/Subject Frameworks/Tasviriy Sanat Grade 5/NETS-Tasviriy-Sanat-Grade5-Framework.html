Textbook analysis showed ~65% hands-on creation and ~30% theory/history. The user's directive: Maker-First — every session, even theory lessons, ends with the student physically making something inspired by today's content.

§1Audience Profile

Inherited from qwen-grade5-psychology-findings.md. Tasviriy Sanat-specific deltas noted inline.

Cognitive (universal G5)

  • Late concrete operational (Piaget); abstract reasoning emerging
  • Working memory 4–5 chunks (Cowan 2001) → max 3 new technique concepts per session
  • Sustained attention 20–30 min academic, extends to 35–40 min for engaged creative work
  • Lexile 830–1010L for art history texts
  • Spatial reasoning still developing — 2D before 3D, simple shapes before complex figures
  • Fine motor coordination still maturing — pencil grip varies, watercolor control limited

Social/Emotional (universal G5)

  • Self-concept fragile under public evaluation (Harter 2012)
  • Art self-concept especially fragile — "I can't draw" is a common life-long self-limiting belief at this age
  • Failure tolerance low — never frame the Make as "good vs bad"
  • Peer comparison sensitivity peaking — no leaderboards, no public ranking of Makes

Tasviriy Sanat-Specific Cognitive Load

  • Creative making is intrinsically motivating IF the prompt is age-appropriate and the framing is non-evaluative
  • The "blank page" problem is real — students freeze when given unlimited choice. The "3 options" pattern (Phase 4) eliminates this
  • 10–12 min making sessions are at the upper edge of G5 sustained fine-motor attention; longer = fatigue

Uzbek Context

  • Mother is primary supervisor; mother-as-co-art-teacher is welcomed (especially for naqsh, embroidery)
  • Shared family smartphone — mobile-first photo upload
  • Art supplies vary wildly — urban families have full kits, rural families may have only pencil + notebook. The digital fallback is essential.
  • Traditional Uzbek crafts (kashtachilik, naqsh, miniature, ceramics) are part of cultural identity

§2Subject Identity

Tasviriy Sanat = Visual Arts / Fine Arts. Mandatory in Uzbek schools. Grade 5 covers drawing fundamentals, color theory, composition, art history, and traditional Uzbek folk arts.

Pedagogical posture: Make, observe, wonder, share. The textbook teaches by demonstrating technique step-by-step (4-phase scaffolds), then asking students to create. NETS preserves this rhythm: theory feeds making, making feeds reflection.

Content Domains (5 thematic clusters)

  1. Drawing fundamentals (Lessons 2–7) — pencil, line, value, geometric form, perspective, landscape, sculpture
  2. Color & painting (Lessons 8–10, 17–23) — color theory, watercolor, still-life, contrast, harmony
  3. Composition & ornament (Lessons 12, 14–16, 21, 24–26) — sketching, decorative naqsh, themed compositions, wall newspapers
  4. Genres & art history (Lessons 1, 8–9, 11, 13, 27) — expressive means, color painters, portrait genre
  5. Portraiture & character (Lessons 28–34) — cartoon characters, clay relief portraits, historical figures (Amir Temur, Bobur)

§4Psychology Filter — Applied Parameters

Session Timing

  • Standard session: 25–35 min (Maker-First override; longer than Tarbiya 15–25, shorter than Science 35–45)
  • The Make alone: 10–12 min (extendable to 15)
  • Single non-Make phase max: 4–5 min
  • Inter-phase break: 5–10 sec with Did You Know art facts
  • 1 session per day max (creative fatigue is real)
  • Deploy window: 17:00–19:00 (after-dinner, mother nearby for supplies)

Adaptation Calibration

  • No success rate target for The Make — completion = success
  • No difficulty labels, no tiering for The Make
  • Recovery framing — never "Wrong"; use "Boshqacha qarash"
  • Productive struggle ceiling for non-Make tasks: 30 sec
  • The Make has no struggle ceiling — student paces themselves

Banned Techniques

  1. Banned Public ranking of student artworks
  2. Banned Comparative grading of Makes
  3. Banned "Good drawing / bad drawing" framing
  4. Banned Forced sharing of Makes
  5. Banned Failure states for The Make
  6. Banned "You're talented" praise
  7. Banned Boss combat metaphor
  8. Banned Mandatory written reflection
  9. Banned Western-only artist canon (always pair with Uzbek master)
  10. Banned Religious doctrinal framing
  11. Banned Real-time multiplayer drawing competitions
  12. Banned Adaptive Quiz mechanic
  13. Banned Why Chain ≥3 levels
  14. Banned Cosmetics purchasable with money
  15. Banned Visible timer countdown on The Make (opt-in only)

Mandatory Inclusions

  1. Required Every session ends with The Make
  2. Required Digital fallback always available
  3. Required 3-option choice pattern in Phase 4
  4. Required Effort/process praise only
  5. Required Master Painters Hall + Galereya dual collection
  6. Required Cultural anchors with Uzbek/world master pairing
  7. Required Warm Tier 3 vision feedback on every Make
  8. Required Optional family-bridge prompt
  9. Required Calm pacing — no countdown timers by default
  10. Required Did You Know art facts between phases

§6The 7-Phase Engine — Tasviriy Sanat Maker-First

Total session: 25–35 min. The Make alone is 10–12 min.

§6.1 Phase 1 — Warm-up Sketch (Qo'l Mashqi) · 3 min

Replaces universal Memory Sprint. The hand warms up before the mind.

  • 3-min warm-up exercise tied to today's lesson (e.g., "Draw 5 different eyes, 30 sec each" for portrait day)
  • No upload required, no timer pressure
  • Soft recall of last session (1 visual MC)
  • XP awarded for tapping through
  • If "no supplies" → digital tap-and-trace exercise

§6.2 Phase 2 — Hikoya + Demo · 5–6 min

Story Mode + interleaved technique demos.

  • 3 narrative segments × 90 sec, each followed by 30-sec embedded technique demo video
  • Story arc: Problem → Discovery → Solution
  • Demos are textbook's step-by-step scaffolds turned into video
  • After each segment: optional "try it real quick" invitation in notebook
  • Tier 1 pre-generated narrative + pre-recorded demo videos

§6.3 Phase 3 — Skill Drill (Mahorat Mashqi) · 4 min

Replaces universal Game Breaks. One light game + one micro-make.

  • Game (2 min): Tile Match / Memory Match / Mystery Box / Sentence Fill — rotating
  • Micro-make (2 min): Small drawing exercise, optional photo upload, no scoring

§6.4 Phase 4 — Tanlov + Reja (Choice + Plan) · 3 min

Student picks WHAT to make today.

  1. 3 prompt options with starter thumbnails (eliminates blank-page paralysis)
  2. Free-text "Boshqa fikr bor" option (Tier 2 affirms warmly)
  3. 1-min plan prompt — "Qog'ozni qaysi shaklda joylashtirasan? Yorug'lik qayerdan keladi?"
  4. Plan is private — never graded

§6.5 Phase 5 — Master Painters Hall (Ustozlar Saroyi) · 1–2 min

  • Today's painter card unlocks (or paired card on theory days)
  • Brief animation, card slides into persistent Hall
  • 4 zones: Renaissance Masters · Modern Masters · Uzbek Masters · Folk Tradition Masters
  • Card pairing rule: theory days unlock world master + Uzbek master together
  • 34 cards total for Grade 5
  • Calm, slow — the breath before the Make

§6.6 Phase 6 — KARTINA (The Make) · 10–12 min ⭐

REPLACES Final Boss. The session climax — the entire engine builds to this moment.

  1. Setup screen (15 sec) — prompt locked from Phase 4, materials reminder
  2. Optional reference panel — master examples stay visible in collapsible sidebar
  3. Optional calm music (off by default)
  4. Optional timer (hidden by default, extendable to 15 min)
  5. Mid-make checkpoint at 5 min — gentle "Davom et yoki bir nafas ol?"
  6. Photo upload when done — phone camera + auto-crop assist
  7. Tier 3 vision evaluation (≤10 sec): identifies what's working, notes 1 thing to try next time, generates warm 2-sentence response
  8. Always passes. Photo joins persistent Galereya. XP awarded.
  9. Optional self-assessment (3 prompts, 30 sec)

Digital fallback if "no supplies" tapped → in-app digital make tool (drawing / composition builder / style picker), Tier 2 evaluation, full XP, tagged for teacher analytics.

No HP, no hints, no retries, no defeat, no fail screen, no difficulty tier. The Make is the climax through effort, not combat.

§6.7 Phase 7 — O'ylab Ko'rish (Reflection) · 1–2 min · OPTIONAL

Optional reflection. Skip is a valid path.

  1. Voice — record up to 30 sec
  2. Text — write up to 2 sentences
  3. Draw — quick sketch in app
  4. Skip — tap "Bugun yetarli"

Optional family-bridge prompt: "Buvingga ko'rsat?" (Show your grandmother?) — pure suggestion, no XP, no requirement.

§7Content Rules

Narrative Voice

  • Second-person ("Sen") — warm, partner-tone, never preachy
  • ≤14 word sentences, active voice, Lexile 830–1010L
  • Master pairing rule: every world master mentioned must be paired with a Uzbek master

Emotional Tone

  • Warm, affirming, never evaluative for The Make and reflection
  • Effort/process praise only — "Yaxshi mehnat qilding," not "Sen rassomsan"
  • Dweck growth-mindset language

Visuals

  • Every concept has a visual. Dual coding mandatory.
  • Mobile-first, 5" screen legible.
  • Demo videos: ≤30 sec, no music, slow hand-cam style — student can mimic
  • Subject color: deep purple #7C3AED primary + warm cream #FEF3C7 accent (creativity + warmth + tradition)

Tier 3 Vision Evaluation Rubric

The vision call is never grading. It identifies what's working and offers one gentle suggestion. Sample warm responses:

"Aziza, sening natyurmortingda olmaning shakli juda yaxshi chiqibdi. Keyingi safar yorug'lik qaysi tomondan kelishini ko'rsatib ko'r — bu rasmni yanada jonliroq qiladi."

Cost: ~$0.02 per vision call. ~34 calls/year = ~$0.68/student/year.

Digital Fallback Spec

When "no supplies" tapped:

  • Engine offers 1 of 3 in-app digital tools (depending on lesson type): drawing tool / composition builder / style picker
  • Tier 2 evaluates choices, gives warm feedback (cheaper than vision)
  • Full XP awarded
  • Galereya entry tagged make_type: digital_fallback
  • Teacher dashboard alert if same student uses fallback ≥3 sessions in a row → soft outreach for supplies

Galereya — Persistent Gallery

  • Every Make joins the Galereya (physical or digital)
  • Organized by date, filterable by lesson topic
  • Visual layout: grid of thumbnails, tap to view full size + read warm response + see master pairing
  • Optional "Sinfga ulashish" (Share to class) — moderated by teacher
  • End-of-quarter "Mening Choragim" auto-collage
  • Cosmetic frame unlocks via Master Painters Hall zone completion
  • No leaderboards. No ranking. No comparison with classmates.

Master Painters Hall — Second Collection Meta-Game

  • 34 painter cards unlock over the year (1 per session)
  • 4 zones: Renaissance · Modern · Uzbek · Folk Tradition
  • Theory days unlock paired cards (world master + Uzbek master)
  • Card details: portrait, dates, famous work, quote, link to today's story
  • Zone completion unlocks Galereya frame + cosmetic accessory
  • Pure cosmetic / collection joy — never scored, never ranked

§17Sample Walkthrough — Lesson 27 "Portret Janri"

Reference implementation. This is a theory lesson showing how Maker-First handles art history days — the session still ends with The Make.

Metadata

  • Lesson 27, textbook pages 92–95
  • Standard: UZ-TSV-5-PORT-01 / TSV.5.27.1
  • Lesson type: Theory (art history)
  • Primary VA level: VA-L4 (Express)
  • PISA tags: Creative Thinking (primary) + Reading L1–L2 (secondary)
  • Session length: ~28 min
  • Master Painters Hall unlocks: Leonardo da Vinci + Malik Nabiyev (paired)

Pre-homework (3 min)

0-A Theme Preview (2 min, 8 panels): Mona Lisa + Nabiyev's Amir Temur side-by-side · Concept explained · 3 portrait examples · Leonardo painted Mona Lisa for 4 years · Personal hook · Significance · Modern context · "Bugun sen ham bir portret chizasan — 10 daqiqada, oddiy qalam bilan."

0-B Flash Cards (1 min, 6 cards): Portret · Avtoportret · Profil · Anfas · Xarakter · Ruhiy holat (each with mini-demo sketch)

Phase 1 — Warm-up Sketch (3 min)

"Daftaringga 5 ta turli ko'z chiz. Har biri 30 soniya. Hayron / xafa / xursand / charchagan / hayratlangan." No timer pressure, no upload required.

Phase 2 — Hikoya + Demo (6 min)

Segment 1: 1503 Florence. Leonardo + savdogar's wife. Demo 1: Face shape with simple proportions.

Segment 2: Sfumato — smoke-like blending. Demo 2: Pencil shading with finger blending.

Segment 3: 1968 Tashkent. Nabiyev paints Amir Temur — but he never met him. How? Demo 3: Adding character — eyebrow direction = mood.

Phase 3 — Skill Drill (4 min)

Game: Memory Match — 6 master/painting pairs (Leonardo↔Mona Liza, Rembrandt↔Avtoportret, Van Gogh↔Avtoportret, Nabiyev↔Amir Temur, Choriyev↔Keksa, Ahmedov↔Yoz ne'matlari)

Micro-make: "Bitta ko'zni his-tuyg'u bilan chiz: hayron / xafa / xursand."

Phase 4 — Tanlov + Reja (3 min)

3 options:

  1. Avtoportret — oynaga qarab
  2. Oilangdagi kimnidir — onangni, dadangni, bobongni
  3. Tasavvuringdagi qahramon — adabiy yoki tarixiy

Aziza picks Option 2 — onasi.

1-min plan: "Onangning yuzini qaysi tomondan ko'rasan? Eng muhim narsa — ko'zlarmi yoki tabassummi? Yorug'lik qayerdan keladi?"

Phase 5 — Master Painters Hall (1 min)

Today's paired card unlocks: Leonardo da Vinci (Renaissance) + Malik Nabiyev (Uzbek). Both slide into the Hall with brief animation.

Phase 6 — KARTINA (10–12 min) ⭐

Setup: "Sening tanlovingni eslab qoldim — onangning portreti. Tayyormisan? Qalam, daftar, va sevgi kifoya."

Reference panel (sidebar, collapsible): Leonardo Mona Lisa + Nabiyev Amir Temur thumbnails.

Aziza draws her mother for ~10 minutes. Pencil on notebook. She tries to remember her mother's eyes from memory, the way her hair falls, her gentle smile.

Mid-make checkpoint at 5 min: "Qanday ketyapti? Davom et yoki bir nafas ol?" → Aziza taps "Davom et."

At 10 min, Aziza taps "Tayyor" and takes a photo.

Tier 3 vision (8 sec):

"Aziza, sening onangning portretida ko'zlarning his-tuyg'usi juda kuchli — xuddi mehrli onaga qaragandek. Keyingi safar yorug'lik qaysi tomondan kelishini ham ko'rsatib ko'r — bu portretni yanada jonliroq qiladi. Sening rasming Galereyangga qo'shildi."

Galereya updated. ~150 XP awarded.

Phase 7 — O'ylab Ko'rish (1 min, optional)

Aziza taps voice mode and says: "Onamning ko'zlarini chizishda qiyinchilik bo'ldi, lekin uning tabassumi yaxshi chiqdi. Men bu rasmni unga ko'rsataman."

Family-bridge prompt: "Buvingga yoki onangga ko'rsat?" → Aziza taps "Ha."

Post-session

  • Total XP: ~155 (75 base + 50 Make + 20 Reflection + 10 family-bridge)
  • Galereya updated with onasi portret
  • Master Painters Hall: Leonardo + Nabiyev unlocked
  • Teacher dashboard: "Aziza completed Lesson 27 — uploaded Make, shared reflection, will show family"
