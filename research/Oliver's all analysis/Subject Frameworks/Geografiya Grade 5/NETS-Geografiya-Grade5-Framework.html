§0Resolution Log

Geografiya G5 is the third NETS subject built on the PISA-rigorous shape (after Science G5 and Texnologiya G5). It maps cleanly to PISA Science (Earth & space systems) + PISA Reading L1→L2 (map literacy). Unlike Tarbiya or Tasviriy Sanat, it does NOT need a major shape override — Final Boss, 35–45 min sessions, and standard 7-phase engine all work. The decisions below are deltas, not reinventions.

§1Audience Profile

Inherited from qwen-grade5-psychology-findings.md. Geografiya-specific deltas noted inline.

Cognitive (universal G5)

  • Late concrete operational (Piaget); abstract reasoning emerging
  • Working memory 4–5 chunks (Cowan 2001) → max 3 new geographic concepts per session
  • Lexile 830–1010L
  • Spatial reasoning still developing — 2D maps before 3D globes; simple coordinate grids before complex projections
  • Scale comprehension is hard at this age — proportional reasoning is fragile; CPA progression mandatory for scale concepts

Geografiya-Specific Cognitive Load

  • "Why" questions are the engine — G5 students can handle 2-step causation but struggle with 3+ step chains. Why Chain capped at 2 levels.
  • Coordinate systems are abstract — CPA mandatory: real globe → simplified grid diagram → numerical coordinates.
  • Map symbols are a vocabulary — visual scaffolding mandatory; flash cards essential; spaced retrieval works well.
  • Temporal scale is invisible — tectonics happens over millions of years. Use comparative anchors ("when dinosaurs lived").

Uzbek Context

  • Mother is primary supervisor; mother-as-co-learner welcomed for maps and weather observation
  • Strong cultural pride in Central Asian geographic heritage — leverage this
  • Resonant historical figures: Al-Beruni, Al-Khorazmi, Nasir Khosrow, Mahmud Kashgari, Ibn Sino

§2Subject Identity

Geografiya = Geography. Grade 5 is the first year of formal geography education, focused on physical geography. Subtitle: Tabiiy geografiya boshlang'ich kursi (Introductory Course in Physical Geography).

Pedagogical posture: Curiosity, observation, explanation. The textbook teaches by asking "why" — why do seasons change, why are mountains where they are, why is the Aral Sea shrinking.

Content Domains (7 Bobs)

  1. Bob I — Geografiya fani va uning rivojlanishi (Geography as a science) — ancient + modern, geographers across history
  2. Bob II — Yer — Quyosh sistemasidagi sayyora (Earth as a planet) — celestial mechanics, seasons, coordinates, scale, map symbols
  3. Bob III — Geografik xaritalar (Maps) — types, scale, legend, coordinate systems, practical work
  4. Bob IV — Yerning qattiq qobig'i — Litosfera (Lithosphere) — crust movements, landforms, ocean floor relief, weathering, soil, minerals
  5. Bob V — Yerning suv qobig'i — Gidrosfera (Hydrosphere) — water cycle, oceans, seas, rivers, lakes, glaciers
  6. Bob VI — Yerning havo qobig'i — Atmosfera (Atmosphere) — composition, pressure, temperature, wind, weather, climate
  7. Bob VII — Biosfera va inson (Biosphere + humanity) — ecosystems, human-environment interaction, Uzbek oases

§3PISA Mapping + Map Literacy (ML) Custom Track

Primary tag: PISA Science L1→L2

The dominant fit. Covers all Earth-system content: solar mechanics, tectonics, water cycle, atmosphere, ecosystems, scientific reasoning, evidence & observation.

Secondary tag: PISA Reading L1→L2

Used only on map literacy content: reading maps, interpreting coordinates, locating places, cross-referencing thematic maps.

Custom Map Literacy (ML) Track

Mandatory transition_skill Tags

  • L1→L2: identify a feature on a map and name it correctly
  • L1→L2: explain a familiar Earth phenomenon with one cause
  • L1→L2: read map symbols and interpret a simple legend
  • L2→L3: use coordinates and scale to locate a place and measure distance
  • L2→L3: interpret a 2-step causal chain (latitude → temperature → vegetation)
  • L2→L3: read a thematic map and explain a spatial pattern
  • L3→L4: combine information from multiple maps to draw a conclusion
  • L3→L4: predict consequences of an Earth process (climate change → Aral Sea)

§4Psychology Filter — Applied Parameters

Session Timing

  • Standard: 35–45 min · Extended: 45–60 min
  • Single phase max: 5–9 min · Inter-phase break: 5–15 sec
  • 1–2 sessions/day · Deploy window 17:00–19:00

Difficulty Calibration

  • Flow state: 75–85% (G5 floor higher than universal 70–80%)
  • Adapt every 3–4 questions
  • Hidden tiering · 60% pass threshold
  • Tier-down trigger: <70% (catch slipping students 10 pts earlier)
  • Productive struggle ceiling: 30 sec
  • "Hali emas" language — never "wrong"
  • Max 1–2 new technical terms per session (geography vocab is dense)
  • Max 3 new concepts per instruction (Cowan WM)

Banned Techniques

  1. Banned Public wrong answers / leaderboards
  2. Banned Real-time multiplayer competition
  3. Banned Adaptive Quiz mechanic
  4. Banned Peer Teaching primary feedback
  5. Banned Why Chain ≥3 levels
  6. Banned Visible difficulty labels
  7. Banned "You're smart" praise
  8. Banned Brain breaks every 5 min
  9. Banned Western-only geographer canon (must always pair with Central Asian)
  10. Banned Forcing students to draw maps from scratch (G5 too early)
  11. Banned Memorization-only place lists (no spatial reasoning context)
  12. Banned Cosmetics purchasable with money

Mandatory Inclusions

  1. Required CPA progression (real photo → diagram → definition)
  2. Required Dual coding
  3. Required Worked examples before independent practice
  4. Required Spaced retrieval (Day 1/3/7/14)
  5. Required Effort + strategy feedback
  6. Required Cultural anchors in every session
  7. Required Scholar pairing rule
  8. Required Mobile-first portrait, low-bandwidth
  9. Required 30-second productive struggle window
  10. Required Hidden difficulty tiering
  11. Required Uzbek-first language

§6The 7-Phase Engine — Geografiya G5

Total session: 35–45 min.

§6.1 Phase 1 — Memory Sprint (≤2 min, 5–7 items)

  • Format mix: Tile Match (place ↔ symbol), Quick MC (climate zone), Sentence Fill (process sequences)
  • Spaced retrieval Day 1/3/7/14
  • Visual scaffolding mandatory
  • Map symbols get priority placement
  • Remediation gate: <60% → route to remediation before Story Mode

§6.2 Phase 2 — Story Mode (5–7 min)

3 segments × 90 sec. Real geographer/scholar/explorer narratives. Scholar pairing rule applies.

Story arcGeografiya version
Problem"Beruni 1000 yil oldin Yer yumaloqligini bilardi. Lekin u qanday isbotladi?"
StruggleFirst attempts fail. "Kerakli asboblar yo'q edi."
Discovery"U o'lchov ipini va astrolyabiyani ishlatib, soyaning uzunligini va quyosh burchagini o'lchadi."
Solution"Beruni Yer aylanasini 40,000 km deb topdi — bu hozirgi qiymatga juda yaqin!"

§6.3 Phase 3 — Game Breaks (6–9 min, 3 games)

  • Slot 1: Tile Match / Memory Match (place↔symbol, scholar↔contribution)
  • Slot 2: Why Chain (max 2 levels) / Codebreaker / Mystery Box
  • Slot 3: Notebook Capture in 1-of-4 sessions; otherwise Tic Tac Toe / Escape Room / Connect Four

§6.4 Phase 4 — Real-Life Challenge (3–5 min)

First-person expert POV mandatory. Examples: "Sen meteorologsan…" · "Sen geologsan…" · "Sen Aral Sea atrofidagi qishloqlarni o'rganadigan ekologsan…" Real Uzbek context. Tricky distractors. Justification required. Map literacy required — at least one RLC per session involves reading a map.

Default: Labeled diagram / map recall.

Boss content (5 questions, Easy 40% / Medium 40% / Hard 20%): place identification + coordinate calculation + causal explanation + map interpretation + optional cross-map synthesis (Hard).

MC allowed: up to 30%. Each Q has worked solution + 2–3 hints + Common Errors. Hint tax −10 HP. Combo bonus 3-in-row → 2× damage. Max 3 attempts. Mastery Stars 1–3.

§6.7 Phase 7 — Reflection (1–2 min)

Tier 2 generates 1 prompt by accuracy bucket. Min 1 sentence. Effort + strategy framing only. Privacy student-only.

§7Content Rules

Narrative Voice

  • Second-person ("Sen") · ≤14 word sentences · active voice · Lexile 830–1010L
  • ~12k receptive vocab ceiling · Define new terms with visual + 3+ repetitions
  • Scholar pairing rule: every world geographer paired with a Central Asian one

Visuals

  • Every concept has a visual — CPA mandatory
  • Mobile-first sizing (5" screen legible)
  • Maps are first-class content — every Bob III, IV, V, VI lesson includes at least one interactive map element
  • Subject color: deep teal #0D9488 primary + sand #FCD34D accent (earth + sky + Uzbek desert)

Notebook Capture (1-in-4 sessions)

  • Label a blank map — student gets a blank Uzbekistan map; AI vision evaluates filled labels
  • Draw a weather symbol — simple icons (sun, rain, wind direction)
  • Sketch a landform diagram — cross-section of mountain, river valley
  • Plot coordinates on a grid — find a place from given coordinates

AI vision rubric: lenient on artistic skill, strict on conceptual accuracy. ≤10 sec Tier 3 vision.

§11Cultural and Regional Anchors

Names

Aziza, Bekzod, Gulnora, Sardor, Malika, Jasur, Dilnoza, Otabek, Farrux, Nodira, Komila, Anvar

Uzbek Places (heavily used)

  • Cities: Tashkent, Samarkand, Bukhara, Khiva, Fergana, Margilan, Andijan, Urgench, Nukus, Termez
  • Mountains: Tian Shan, Pamir, Chimgan, Hisar
  • Rivers: Amu-Darya, Syr-Darya, Zarafshon, Chirchiq
  • Lakes/Reservoirs: Aral Sea (environmental theme — used carefully), Charvak reservoir
  • Valleys: Fergana Valley (tectonic formation example)
  • Deserts: Kyzylkum, Karakum

Central Asian Scholars (Scholar Pairing Rule)

  • Abu Rayhan Beruni (973–1048) — Earth radius, Northern Hemisphere globe
  • Muhammad al-Khorazmi (783–850) — "Surat ul-Arz" (Description of the Earth)
  • Nasir Khosrow (1004–1088) — "Safarnama" (Travel Book)
  • Mahmud Kashgari (11th c.) — linguistic + geographic documentation
  • Ibn Sino (Avicenna) (980–1037) — climate-and-health writings

World Geographers (always paired with Central Asian)

  • Eratosthenes (Earth circumference) ↔ Beruni
  • Ptolemy (Geographia, world maps) ↔ Khorazmi
  • Magellan / Columbus (exploration) ↔ Nasir Khosrow
  • Modern European cartography ↔ Kashgari

Things to Avoid

  • Direct comparisons to Western lifestyles
  • Pork or alcohol references
  • Politically sensitive topics (Aral Sea OK as environmental theme)
  • Family poverty implications
  • Doctrinal religious framing
  • Ranking Central Asian scholars as "below" world figures — always treat as equal

§14Teacher Overrides

  • Map Detective availability per session
  • Did You Know facts (default ↔ teacher-curated subset)

Cannot Override

  • 7-phase structure
  • Sessions 0-A and 0-B mandatory
  • Boss is mandatory
  • Stranger Test on Story Mode
  • transition_skill tag requirement
  • 60% pass threshold
  • Banned mechanics list
  • Cultural framing (Uzbek language, scholar pairing rule)
  • ML track tagging requirement on map content
  • Doctrinally-neutral framing of Central Asian scholars
