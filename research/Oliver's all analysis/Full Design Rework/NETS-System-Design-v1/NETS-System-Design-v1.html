The 5-Layer Architecture

Strict inheritance — lower layers cannot contradict higher layers
▶

A strict hierarchy of five layers. Each layer inherits from the one above. Nothing in a lower layer may contradict a higher layer. Click any layer to expand its purpose.

Layer 4 — Metagame + App Surfaces

Persistent, cross-subject motivation layer
Bilim Bazasi visual model, Bilim Token economy, streak system, Boss tournament events, hero collection, app surface specs (Today, Library, Tournaments, Teacher Dashboard). Locked: no real-money currency, no BT spend on academic advantage. Mystery boxes earned by mastery only.

Layer 3 — Tier Overlay

Basic + Premium delta — not a duplicate content set
Difficulty band extension for Premium, AI tier assignment per tier, game unlock schedule, specialized course access rules, weekly PISA lesson format delta, metagame token reward multipliers. Locked: Basic delivers full learning mission.

Layer 2 — Subject Frameworks

Per subject × grade × language (~110 total, 1 complete)
Textbook chapter index, per-chapter content blocks and assessment items, subject-specific PISA transitions, worked example banks, Uzbek-context illustrations. Current state: 1 of ~110 complete. G5 Science v3.0 is the prototype and gold standard.

Layer 1 — Subject Family Frameworks

5 families — NEW this sprint (highest-leverage work)
Family-level PISA domain mappings, mechanic emphasis table (primary/secondary/banned), cognitive demand profile, answer format guidance, Boss format rules. Heavy design done once; all Layer 2 subject frameworks inherit it as a fast delta.

Layer 0 — Universal Framework v2.0

The constitution — unchanged, all modifications require founder approval
7-phase session structure, 16 default + 12 interactive game mechanics, dual-catalog rule, PISA framework mappings, 3-tier Boss system, Notebook Capture spec, anti-cheat framework, AI tier cost model, mandatory tagging schema, difficulty calibration rules.

03

Subject Family Classification

5 families grouped by cognitive demands and mechanic fit
▶

The five families group subjects by shared cognitive demands and mechanic fit. This is the single most important architectural decision for scaling content production. Once a family framework exists, writing a subject framework beneath it is dramatically faster.

Aniq Fanlar — Exact Sciences

Subjects: Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry, Informatics

Cognitive demands: Multi-step calculation, formal notation, derivation from first principles, error detection, spatial reasoning for geometry/physics

CPA progressionTile MatchNotebook CaptureWorked examplesError AnalysisSpeed Match

Boss format: Open numerical/derivation problem, not multiple-choice for Grade 6+. G5 may use up to 30% MC (ADR-005). Rubric-based partial credit. Every new formula introduced via concrete scenario first.

Til Fanlari — Languages

Subjects: Uzbek, Russian, English, Native Language (Tajik/Karakalpak/etc.), Literature

Cognitive demands: Vocabulary acquisition, grammar rule application, reading fluency, listening comprehension, writing production, narrative interpretation

Spaced RepetitionCloze/Sentence FillListening tasksDictationStory ModeWord Ladder

Boss format: Writing production task evaluated by Tier 2 LLM. G5 uses guided production (stem provided). G6+ uses prompted free production. Grammar always introduced in story context first.

Tabiat Fanlari — Natural Sciences

Subjects: Biology, Geography, G5 Combined Science (transitional)

Cognitive demands: Concept model construction, biological/geographic classification, diagram interpretation, hypothesis formation, cause-effect chains

Diagram LabelingMemory PalaceStory ModeWhy ChainSpeed MatchTile Match

Boss format: Explanation task — "describe the process" or "explain why." Diagrams may be photographed via Notebook Capture at G5. G5 Science v3.0 is the family prototype.

Ijtimoiy Fanlari — Social Sciences

Subjects: History, Law, Economy, Civics

Cognitive demands: Source analysis, critical thinking, case reasoning, chronological modeling, perspective-taking

Story ModeMemory PalaceTimeline Tile MatchSource ComparisonWhy Chain

Boss format: Case reasoning task — apply a principle to a situation. History Boss may include primary source excerpt. No MC for Grade 6+. Legal/economic principles introduced via Uzbek everyday scenario first.

Tarbiya/Sanat — Soft and Formative

Subjects: Art, Music, Physical Education, Tarbiya (character education)

Cognitive demands: Engagement, expressive output, character formation, aesthetic appreciation, physical skill, emotional literacy

Reflection JournalMovement BreaksExpressive tasks

Boss format: Optional formative prompt — "share one thing you created" — with encouraging response only. No scoring. Intentionally minimal-gamification: over-gamifying character education produces the extrinsic-reward plateau problem.

AI Tier Strategy

80% rule-based — $3–5/student/year Basic cost target
▶

A cost-constrained design principle. At $3–5 per student per year for Basic Tier, the vast majority of AI interactions must be cheap or free. The system is designed so 80% of tasks never require inference at all. All API routes implement static fallbacks — session failure on AI outage is architecturally impossible.

Tier 1 — Rule-based / Hardcoded

~80% of all tasksEffectively $0Both tiers

Hardcoded answer checker and fuzzy-match library (app/lib/fuzzy-match.ts). Response time under 100ms. No network call. Covers all multiple-choice, matching, numeric answer, and exact-string tasks.

Tier 2 — Small LLM (cheap inference)

~15% of interactionsCents/student/yearBoth tiers

Boss evaluation, Socratic prompts, anti-cheat analysis, story checkpoints. Current local implementation: Ollama + quantized Qwen model. Anti-cheat (behavioral baseline comparison + semantic anomaly detection) runs at Tier 2 for both Basic and Premium.

Tier 3 — Opus-grade LLM

~5% of interactionsDollars/student/yearPremium only

Multi-turn Socratic tutoring (guides toward understanding without revealing answers), personalized cross-subject study plans, detailed writing feedback, exam preparation adaptation. Economically viable only for Premium.

Implementation Roadmap

Sequenced by dependency — shipping sequence over calendar pressure
▶

The roadmap is sequenced by dependency, not time estimate. Time estimates are excluded per SOUL principle: shipping sequence matters; calendar pressure does not override design quality. Click any phase to expand details.

Phase 1 — 5 Subject Family Frameworks (Layer 1)

One document per family: Aniq Fanlar, Til Fanlari, Tabiat Fanlari, Ijtimoiy Fanlari, Tarbiya/Sanat. Tabiat Fanlari written first (G5 Science v3.0 is the prototype). Each specifies cognitive profile, mechanic emphasis table (primary/secondary/banned), Boss format rules, PISA domain mappings.

Phase 2 — Per-family G5 Subject Frameworks (Layer 2)

With family frameworks in place, each G5 subject framework is a delta document. Target: Math, Biology, History, Literature, Geography, English, Russian, Native Language, Informatics. Family framework does heavy cognitive-science work; subject framework adds textbook-specific content and item banks.

Phase 3 — Tier Overlay Contracts (Layer 3)

Formal delta contract: for each subject family, what changes between Basic and Premium (difficulty band, game unlock schedule, AI tier assignment, specialized course access). Platform engineers implement against this contract as an extension to platform/docs/.

Phase 4 — Metagame Spec (Layer 4)

Bilim Bazasi data model (base state schema, building unlock dependencies), BT economy formulas (post-playtesting with real G5 students), hero collection schema, tournament event data model, Teacher Dashboard query specifications.

Phase 5 — Platform Implementation

FastAPI backend + React Router frontend per platform/docs/ contracts extended with Tier and Metagame contracts from Phases 3 and 4. CEO agent (platform/docs/11) coordinates sub-agent breakdown per platform/docs/09. Can be built in parallel with content production.

Phase 6 — G6–11 Expansion + Russian Siblings

Repeat the family→subject framework process for G6–11. Russian-language siblings follow same subject frameworks with surface-language substitution and Russian-textbook-specific content deltas. Family frameworks do not change — only Layer 2 documents are added. Linear, parallel, delegable.

Open Questions and Deferred Decisions

8 unresolved items — each requires a formal decision log entry when resolved
▶

These questions must not be resolved by unilateral assumption. Click any card to flip it and see context.

Premium business model?
Family-paid / school-licensed / government-sponsored / corporate / hybrid. Affects pricing tier design, teacher dashboard features, and BT economy. Not resolved.
Informatics placement?
Default: Aniq Fanlar. If dominant demands are closer to creative/constructive, raise an ADR when the G5 Informatics framework is drafted.
Literature placement?
Default: Til Fanlari. If a Humanities subfamily is created (History + Literature + Civics), Literature is the migration candidate. Resolve when drafting G5 Literature.
Tournament regional tier?
Class-level default. School/regional opt-in. Whether school-level becomes default for Premium classes defers to product team after foundation sprint.
BT economy formulas?
Must be calibrated through playtesting with actual G5 students. Target: completing one session feels like visible progress; mastering a skill feels like a meaningful unlock.
G5 Science v3.0: standalone vs. family-derived?
Recommendation: derive family framework upward from v3.0, note deltas in v3.1 resolution log. Faster and architecturally cleaner. Decision authority: founder.
PISA enforcement: async vs. strict deadline?
Launch default: async 7-day window. Strict deadline (fixed weekly cutoff) is a configurable option. Requires school admin buy-in and student consequence design per Principle 3.
Bilim Bazasi visual metaphor?
3 candidates: (a) historical Uzbek academy, (b) Ulugh Beg observatory with star-map, (c) composite Silk Road complex (Samarkand, Bukhara, Khiva). Requires cultural consultants + student testing.

11

SOUL Constraints Carried Forward

5 unbreakable principles with architectural accountability
▶

The 5 unbreakable principles, each with an explicit account of how the new design architecture honors them. Click to expand.

📖

Textbook is Truth

Layer 2 follows textbook chapter sequence. NETS does not reorder or supplement without curriculum authority approval. If AI at any tier generates a claim contradicting the textbook, the textbook wins. Not a fallback — the system's primary epistemological commitment.
✅

Every Task Earns Its Existence

The mandatory transition_skill tag is not relaxed at any layer. A task that cannot specify its PISA transition skill is removed at Layer 2 draft stage. "Harder" difficulty is not a sufficient justification for a task's existence.
🧒

10-Year-Old ≠ Small Adult

G5 psychology constraints (4–5 chunk WM ceiling, "Hali emas" framing, hidden difficulty, weekly-reset competition, no public wrong answers) apply at every layer for G5 content. Competition is class-scoped only. Anti-cheat flags go to teacher, not to the class.
🌊

Flow State, Not Anxiety

75–85% success target enforced at Layer 2 (calibration) and Layer 3 (difficulty band). Silent adaptive difficulty: below 70% triggers tier-down, above 90% triggers tier-up. Metagame calibrated for normal engagement — no extraordinary performance required for visible progress.
⚡

Intrinsic > Extrinsic. Zero Pay-to-Win.

Basic delivers the full educational mission. BT has no real-money entry point. Mystery Boxes purchased with BT only. A student whose family cannot afford Premium has the same PISA progression path. This is the design constraint that makes NETS deployable as national infrastructure without reproducing economic inequality in learning outcomes.
