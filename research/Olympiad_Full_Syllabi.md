# Research Brief: Uzbekistan National Olympiad Full Syllabi (Phase 1)

## 🏆 Overview of the National Olympiad Movement
The **Asosiy Fan Olimpiadasi** (Main Subject Olympiad) is the primary competitive track for students in Uzbekistan (Grades 9–11), managed by the **Ministry of Preschool and School Education** and the **Agency for Specialized Schools (PIIMA)**. The system is designed to identify talent for International Olympiads (IMO, IPhO, IChO, IBO).

### 🔄 The Four Stages
1. **School Stage:** Open to all students.
2. **District (Tuman) Stage:** Top performers from schools.
3. **Regional (Viloyat) Stage:** Highly competitive; format shifts towards international standards.
4. **Republic (Respublika) Stage:** The final national level; winners represent Uzbekistan internationally.

## 📐 Mathematics (Matematika)
The syllabus transitions from standard curriculum to advanced competitive problem-solving (IMO style).

### 1. Algebra
*   **Polynomials:** Roots, Vieta's formulas, remainder theorem, functional equations.
*   **Inequalities:** AM-GM, Cauchy-Schwarz, Bernoulli's, and Jensen’s inequalities; rearrangement inequality.
*   **Sequences & Series:** Arithmetic/geometric progressions, recursive sequences, limits of sequences.
*   **Functions:** Domain/range, monotonicity, periodicity, and complex functional relations.

### 2. Geometry
*   **Planimetry:** Triangle centers (Incenter, Orthocenter, etc.), cyclic quadrilaterals, Power of a Point, Menelaus and Ceva’s theorems, circle properties (tangents, chords).
*   **Stereometry:** Spatial relations, volumes and surface areas of polyhedra and solids of revolution (cones, spheres).
*   **Transformations:** Homothety, rotation, and symmetry in problem-solving.

### 3. Number Theory
*   **Divisibility:** GCD/LCM, Euclidean algorithm, prime factorization, Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic.
*   **Congruences:** Modular arithmetic, Fermat's Little Theorem, Euler’s Totient Theorem, Chinese Remainder Theorem.
*   **Diophantine Equations:** Linear and non-linear (e.g., Pell's equation basics).

### 4. Combinatorics
*   **Counting:** Permutations, combinations, binomial coefficients, Inclusion-Exclusion principle, "Stars and Bars" method.
*   **Graph Theory:** Vertex/edge properties, trees, Eulerian and Hamiltonian paths, planar graphs.
*   **Principles:** Pigeonhole Principle (Dirichlet), Extremal Principle, Invariance.

## 🍎 Physics (Fizika)
Heavily focused on theoretical depth and mathematical modeling (IPhO style).

### 1. Mechanics
*   **Kinematics:** 1D/2D motion, relative motion, projectile and circular motion.
*   **Dynamics:** Newton’s Laws, friction, centripetal forces.
*   **Statics & Elasticity:** Torque, equilibrium of rigid bodies, Hooke's Law.
*   **Energy & Momentum:** Work-energy theorem, conservation of momentum, elastic/inelastic collisions.
*   **Fluid Mechanics:** Archimedes' principle, Bernoulli's equation, viscosity.

### 2. Thermodynamics
*   **Molecular Kinetic Theory (MKT):** Ideal gas laws, Maxwell distribution.
*   **Laws of Thermodynamics:** Internal energy, heat, work, entropy.
*   **Processes:** Isobaric, isochoric, isothermal, and adiabatic processes; heat engines (Carnot cycle).

### 3. Electromagnetism
*   **Electrostatics:** Coulomb’s Law, Gauss’s Law, electric potential, capacitance.
*   **DC/AC Circuits:** Kirchhoff’s Laws, RC/RLC circuits, alternating current properties.
*   **Magnetism:** Lorentz force, Ampere’s Law, Biot-Savart Law.
*   **Induction:** Faraday’s Law, Lenz’s Law, self-induction.

### 4. Optics
*   **Geometric Optics:** Reflection, refraction (Snell's Law), thin lens formula, optical instruments.
*   **Wave Optics:** Huygens' principle, interference (Young’s double slit), diffraction, polarization.

### 5. Modern Physics
*   **Quantum Basics:** Photoelectric effect, Compton scattering, wave-particle duality.
*   **Atomic/Nuclear:** Bohr’s model, radioactivity, half-life, nuclear binding energy, fission/fusion.

## 🧪 Chemistry (Kimyo)
Aligned with IChO preparatory standards, emphasizing both theory and lab logic.

### 1. Physical Chemistry
*   **Atomic Structure:** Quantum numbers, orbitals, electronic configurations.
*   **Chemical Bonding:** VSEPR theory, hybridization, intermolecular forces.
*   **Thermodynamics & Kinetics:** Enthalpy, Gibbs free energy, rate laws, activation energy.
*   **Equilibrium:** Le Chatelier’s principle, Kp/Kc, solubility product (Ksp).

### 2. Inorganic Chemistry
*   **Periodic Trends:** Electronegativity, ionization energy, atomic radii.
*   **Main Group Elements:** Properties of s and p-block elements and their compounds.
*   **Transition Metals:** d-block properties, coordination chemistry (ligands, isomerism, color).

### 3. Organic Chemistry
*   **Fundamentals:** Nomenclature, isomerism (structural and stereoisomerism).
*   **Mechanisms:** Nucleophilic substitution (SN1/SN2), elimination (E1/E2), electrophilic addition.
*   **Functional Groups:** Alcohols, phenols, ethers, carbonyl compounds, carboxylic acids, and amines.
*   **Polymers:** Addition and condensation polymerization.

### 4. Analytical Chemistry
*   **Qualitative:** Identification of cations and anions through flame tests and precipitation.
*   **Quantitative:** Volumetric analysis (titrations: acid-base, redox, complexometric).

## 🧬 Biology (Biologiya)
Broad coverage of life sciences with a shift toward molecular biology and data interpretation (IBO style).

### 1. Cytology (Cell Biology)
*   **Structure:** Prokaryotic vs. eukaryotic cells, organelle functions, membrane structure/transport.
*   **Metabolism:** Glycolysis, Krebs cycle, Oxidative phosphorylation, Photosynthesis (Light/Dark reactions).
*   **Cell Cycle:** Mitosis, Meiosis, regulation and checkpoints.

### 2. Genetics & Evolution
*   **Mendelian Genetics:** Laws of inheritance, pedigrees, linkage, and mapping.
*   **Molecular Biology:** DNA replication, Transcription, Translation (Central Dogma), gene regulation.
*   **Evolution:** Natural selection, speciation, phylogeny, and population genetics (Hardy-Weinberg).

### 3. Plant & Animal Anatomy
*   **Plant Biology:** Tissue systems, xylem/phloem transport, plant hormones (auxins, etc.), reproduction.
*   **Animal Physiology:** Digestion, circulation (heart structure), respiration, excretion (nephron function), nervous system (action potentials).

### 4. Biochemistry
*   **Macromolecules:** Structure and function of proteins, lipids, carbohydrates, and nucleic acids.
*   **Enzymology:** Enzyme kinetics (Michaelis-Menten basics), inhibition types.
*   **Metabolic Pathways:** Interconnectivity of carbohydrate, lipid, and protein metabolism.
