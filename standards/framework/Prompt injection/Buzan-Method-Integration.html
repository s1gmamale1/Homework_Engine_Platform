Buzan Method Integration into NETS Homework Engine

Each method: What → Why → How → Where Not

Methods without "Best for" are subject-agnostic — they apply to all subjects.

Phase-Level Injections

These apply at the session/phase level, not inside individual games. All are subject-agnostic unless noted.

1. TEFCAS Learning Loop

All Phases Methods #10, #11
What

Trial → Event → Feedback → Check → Adjust → Success. Buzan's model of how the brain acquires skills through cybernetic feedback. (Source: Buzan, Scientific Research — The TEFCAS Model)

Why

The NETS session already follows this loop but doesn't name it. Naming it gives the AI tutor a consistent vocabulary and reframes all failure as "Feedback, not failure."

How

All wrong-answer responses use "Hali emas!" (Not yet!) — "Wrong," "Incorrect," "Noto'g'ri," "Xato" are banned system-wide. Phase 6 Socratic tutoring follows C→A→S. Phase 7 prompts use TEFCAS vocabulary.

Where Not

Raw performance data (correct count, XP, time) stays as numbers — TEFCAS language applies to student-facing prompts only, not analytics dashboards or teacher reports.

2. MIG — Most Important Graph

Phase 1, 3, 7 Methods #14, #26, #33
What

Recall is highest at start (Primacy) and end (Recency), dips in middle (the Sag), spikes on outstanding items (Von Restorff). (Source: Buzan, Use Your Memory — The MIG)

Why

Explains why the 7-phase structure works. Makes phase ordering intentional rather than arbitrary.

How

Phase 1 = Primacy window. Phase 3, Game Break 2 = Von Restorff Anchor (tag middle game as "outstanding"). Phase 7 = Recency window. Content authors tag at least 3 items per lesson as outstanding: true.

Where Not

Does not change Phase 2 (Story Mode) or Phase 4 (Real-Life Challenge) — those are content delivery, not recall-curve phases.

3. BOST — Buzan Organic Study Technique

Phase 0-A Methods #13, #27
What

Browse → Time → Prime → Questions → Overview → Preview → Inview → Review. (Source: Buzan, Use Your Head — BOST)

Why

NETS already maps to BOST at ~80%. Two small additions close the gap — activating prior knowledge and setting a learning goal that Phase 7 closes.

How

Panel 5 adds: "Bu mavzu haqida nimalarni bilasiz?" (Prior Knowledge). Panel 6 adds: "Bugun nimani bilmoqchisiz?" (Learning Goal). Both stored, resurfaced in Phase 7.

Where Not

Recovery Sessions (15-20 min, no time). First-ever lesson in a brand new subject.

4. Buzan Review Intervals

Phase 1 + System-wide Method #34
What

Mandatory review at ~10min → ~24h → ~1 week → ~1 month. (Source: Buzan, Use Your Memory — Review Intervals)

Why

SM-2 may schedule reviews too late for "Easy" rated items. Buzan's intervals catch the steepest part of the forgetting curve.

How

Floor constraints: min(SM-2, Buzan) — whichever is sooner wins. Missed session? Touchpoint shifts, never skipped.

Where Not

Does not override SM-2 when SM-2 schedules SOONER. Buzan intervals are a floor, not a ceiling.

5. 80/20 Keyword Extraction

Content Pipeline Method #22
What

~20% of words carry ~80% of conceptual value. (Source: Buzan, Speed Reading — 80/20 Rule)

Why

Tagged keywords feed Radiant Summary, Flash Cards, Memory Sprint, Sentence Fill. One extraction, many consumers.

How

Every narrative_segment must have keywords_80_20 array (3-8 keywords). Pipeline QA blocks segments with missing arrays.

Where Not

Only narrative segments. Game items, boss questions, checkpoints are already keyword-dense.

6. Cortical Skills Synergy

Phase 3 Selection Method #25
What

Left-brain + right-brain working together = exponential performance increase. (Source: Buzan, Use Your Head — Cortical Skills)

Why

Without a constraint, the algorithm could pick 3 verbal games — all left-brain, zero visual/spatial engagement.

How

Tag each game with a modality. Selection constraint: at least 2 of 3 games from DIFFERENT modalities.

Where Not

Recovery Sessions (only 1-2 games).

7. Schema Activation

Session Assembly Method #13
What

New info anchors better when connected to already-active knowledge. (Source: Buzan, Scientific Research — Schema Theory)

Why

If Phase 2 introduces a new concept but Phase 1 didn't activate any prerequisite, the concept floats unanchored.

How

Session Assembler checks: every Phase 2 concept must have ≥1 prerequisite activated in Phase 1 or Phase 0-B. If missing, auto-inject.

Where Not

First lesson in a subject where no prerequisites exist yet.

8. W5H Radiant Problem Solving

Phase 4 Method #4
What

Who/What/Where/When/Why/How radiate from a central problem. (Source: Buzan, Mind Map Book — Problem Solving)

Why

Students jump to answers without analyzing all factors. W5H forces structured decomposition.

How

6-branch W5H frame before answer area. Student fills ≥4. NOT scored — scaffolding. G5-6: mandatory. G9+: optional.

Best for

Science, History, Geography, Tarbiya.

Where Not

Math (pure calculation). Tasviriy Sanat (creative). Language (comprehension).

9. Decision Mapping

Phase 4 Method #5
What

Compare two competing options radiantly with weighted pros/cons. (Source: Buzan, Mind Map Book — Decision Mapping)

Why

Linear pros/cons lists hide trade-offs. Radiant comparison makes them visible.

How

Phase 4 extension of W5H — for genuine trade-offs between 2+ options. Student completes branches, assigns weights, justifies.

Where Not

Phase 6 Boss (assessment — scaffolding doesn't belong). Math. Language. Single-answer scenarios.

Phase 5 Consolidation — Technique by Content Structure

Phase 5 is Buzan's home phase. Technique selected based on content structure, not randomly.

10. Radiant Summary (Mind Mapping)

Methods #1, #2, #7
What

Partially completed mind map — student drags keywords onto branches. Central image, organic curves, one keyword per branch, 3+ colors. (Source: Buzan, Mind Map Book)

Why

Hierarchical content is best encoded as a visual hierarchy. Fills the undefined "Mind maps" slot in Phase 5.

How

3-5 BOI branches, empty sub-branch slots, 2-3 distractors. G5: max 4 branches (WM ceiling), Icon-First. Optional Supernova Sprint after.

Best for

Science classification, Math formula families, Tarbiya virtue categories, Geography biomes, Art elements.

Where Not

Sequential content (Link System). Spatial (Memory Palace). History timelines (temporal, not hierarchical).

11. Link System

Method #28
What

A vivid story chain where each item triggers the next. (Source: Buzan, Use Your Memory)

Why

Sequential content is best encoded as a chain — each item triggers recall of the next.

How

Phase 1 as "Link Chain" format. Phase 5 as consolidation technique. SMASHIN' SCOPE story using textbook's actual terms.

Best for

Science processes, History timelines, cause-effect chains.

Where Not

Unrelated vocabulary. Math formulas. Hierarchical content (Radiant Summary).

12. Peg System

Method #29
What

Number-Shape pegs (1=Candle, 2=Swan, 3=Heart, 4=Sailboat, 5=Hook) as permanent mental hooks. (Source: Buzan, Use Your Memory)

Why

Gives random-access to numbered items. Fills the undefined "Peg system" slot in Phase 5.

How

Associate concepts with peg images via "link sentences." Recall both directions. Can integrate with Tic Tac Toe.

Best for

History key facts, Language vocabulary, Science key terms.

Where Not

Sequential processes (Link System). Hierarchical (Radiant Summary). More than 7 items (overload).

13. Major System (G7+ only)

Method #30
What

Phonetic digit code: 1=T, 2=N, 3=M, 4=R... (Source: Buzan, Use Your Memory)

Why

Only mnemonic that works for raw numbers.

How

Number → consonants → word → vivid image. Example: 1441 → T-R-R-T → "Toshbaqa."

Best for

History dates, Chemistry/Physics constants.

Where Not

G5-6 (too abstract). Tarbiya, Tasviriy Sanat, Language (no numbers).

14. SMASHIN' SCOPE Quality Gate

Method #32
What

12-point vividness checklist for mnemonic content. (Source: Buzan, Use Your Memory)

Why

Dry mnemonics perform no better than rote memorization. Vivid imagery is what makes mnemonics work.

How

Every mnemonic_exercise must score 6+/12. Below 6 = returned for enrichment.

Where Not

Non-mnemonic content (quizzes, boss questions, checkpoints).

15. Dual Coding

Method #12
What

Verbal + visual encoding = two retrieval paths. (Source: Buzan, Scientific Research / Paivio)

Why

Doubles recall probability. Phase 5 IS the Dual Coding moment — verbal (Phase 2) re-encoded visually/spatially here.

How

Pipeline QA: every content block must declare 2+ encoding channels (VRB, VIS, SPA, AUD, KIN).

Where Not

Movement Breaks (physical activity, not encoding).

Game-Level Injections

How Buzan methods enhance specific games. Each includes WHY this method helps THIS specific game.

Tile Match

Dual Coding
Why

If both cards are text, the brain uses only ONE channel (verbal). If one is an image and one is text, TWO channels fire (visual + verbal). Paivio's Dual Coding shows this doubles recall. The game already requires pairs — making them cross-modal costs nothing and doubles the learning.

How

Pairs must be image↔text, not text↔text.

Where Not

Only flag pairs where both sides are text-only.

Why Chain

Radiant Thinking
Why

The "Why?" structure IS radiant thinking — branching deeper. But the UI renders it as a flat list, hiding the branching. A tree visualization lets the student SEE understanding deepen — knowledge radiating outward from a central concept.

How

Render as a downward branching tree, not a flat numbered list.

Where Not

2-level chains (too short to branch meaningfully).

Sentence Fill

80/20 Rule
Why

Blanking a random word tests one random fact. Blanking the 80/20 keyword trains the student to identify the conceptual anchor of every sentence. Over time, builds the habit of reading for meaning.

How

Blanked words = keywords_80_20. Not articles or prepositions.

Where Not

Language grammar exercises where the blank IS a grammatical element.

Memory Palace

SMASHIN' SCOPE + Buzan Intervals
Why

Already a Buzan method, but content at hotspots can be dry or vivid. Dry placements perform no better than rote memorization. The palace only works if what's placed there is memorable. Buzan intervals ensure palaces are revisited at critical forgetting curve points.

How

Hotspot content must pass SMASHIN' SCOPE (6+/12). Revisits follow Buzan intervals. "Hali emas" on missed recalls.

Where Not

Already defined in NETS-Memory-Palace-Definition.md. Just add SMASHIN' SCOPE gate + interval floor.

Tic Tac Toe

Peg System Integration
Why

Standard Tic Tac Toe: board position and question are unrelated. With Peg System merged, cell 3 = Heart peg = concept pegged to Heart. Spatial strategy fuses with mnemonic retrieval = triple coding (visual peg + verbal concept + strategic position).

How

When Peg System is also active: cells labeled with peg numbers. Place X = recall pegged concept.

Where Not

Only when Peg System is also selected. Otherwise plays normally.

Puzzle Lock

Link System
Why

Puzzle Lock = arrange tiles in order. Without a story, student relies on rote memory. With a Link Chain story, the student has a narrative scaffold explaining WHY the order matters — causal logic, not arbitrary sequence. Reduces random guessing.

How

For sequence tiles: Link Chain story as hint after 2 wrong moves.

Where Not

Number-ordering (1-2-3...8). Sentence fragment puzzles (grammar logic).

Mystery Box

Von Restorff Effect
Why

Big prize question is usually random. Making it the "outstanding" one creates a double reward: XP prize AND memorable learning moment. Von Restorff means this ONE question is remembered far better than the other 8. The game's reward structure amplifies a specific piece of knowledge.

How

Large prize box (ℐ) = outstanding: true question.

Where Not

Don't make ALL questions outstanding. Effect works because ONE stands out.

Spaced Flashcards

Buzan Review Intervals
Why

SM-2 is great for struggling items but too generous with "Easy" — pushing them weeks out. Buzan shows 24h and 1-week points are critical regardless of ease. A student rating "Easy" today may forget in 3 days. The floor catches overconfident ratings before they become gaps.

How

"Easy" cards still return at 24h, 1w, 1mo minimum.

Where Not

"Again" cards — SM-2 already returns them immediately.

Adaptive Quiz

TEFCAS
Why

The adaptive mechanism IS TEFCAS but the student doesn't see it — questions just get harder/easier with no explanation. Naming the transitions turns a confusing difficulty swing into a visible learning process.

How

On level up: "Trial muvaffaqiyatli!" On level down: "Feedback olindi — flow zonangizni topmoqdamiz."

Where Not

Only on difficulty TRANSITIONS, not every question.

Peer Teaching

TEFCAS Failure Framing
Why

Only mechanic where students PRODUCE knowledge. "Incorrect — the answer is X" kills the production impulse. "Hali emas! Covers [X] but missing [Y] — how would you Adjust?" preserves the expert identity the mechanic is built on.

How

"Hali emas! Tushuntirishingiz [X] ni qamraydi, lekin [Y] yetishmayapti. Qanday Adjust qilasiz?"

Where Not

Factually dangerous explanations (safety info) — direct correction necessary.

Reflection Journal

TEFCAS + BOST Goal Recall
Why

Generic prompts produce generic answers. TEFCAS prompts produce structured metacognition. BOST goal recall closes the loop from Phase 0-A — completion or honest "not yet."

How

TEFCAS-framed prompts by performance band. BOST goal recall at end.

Where Not

Raw performance summary unchanged. TEFCAS applies to prompts only.

Story Mode

80/20 Keyword Tagging
Why

Without tagging, downstream mechanics guess which words matter. With keywords_80_20 tagged once, every mechanic knows exactly which terms to test, blank, or use as labels. One extraction, consistency everywhere.

How

Every narrative segment has tagged keywords feeding downstream.

Where Not

Speed reading mechanics DEFERRED (see below).

Memory Sprint

Link System + Buzan Intervals
Why

5 existing formats are retrieval-based. Link Chain (6th) is encoding-based: the story EXPLAINS why the order matters. Better than "Order the Steps" for causal content. Buzan intervals ensure critical concepts always appear even if SM-2 would delay them.

How

Link Chain: 5 items + SMASHIN' SCOPE story, 15s study, recall out of order. Intervals as floor constraints.

Where Not

Link Chain not for unrelated vocabulary or math formulas.

Games Where Buzan Adds Nothing

Movement Breaks

Physical activity — no knowledge encoding. Buzan methods are cognitive, not physical.

Connect Four

Pure strategy with knowledge gating. The strategy IS the engagement. Mnemonic framing overcomplicates it.

Blackjack 21

Math-only, probability-focused. Live calculation doesn't benefit from mnemonics.

Codebreaker

Letter deduction logic. The Wordle-style process is its own cognitive method. Buzan adds friction, not value.

Notebook Capture

Physical drawing/writing. The kinesthetic act IS the encoding — Buzan's visual methods are already embedded in drawing.

Deferred Methods

CUT from active injection. May apply at G9+ after empirical validation.

Focus Guide, Sub-vocalization, Meta-Guiding, Forward Momentum, Semantic Chunking, Saccade/Peripheral Vision

Methods #16-21, #23 DEFERRED → Future Reading Fluency Module
Why Cut

G5 bottleneck is COMPREHENSION, not speed. Rayner et al. (2016) found speed reading training does NOT improve comprehension. G5 students (Lexile 830-1010L) struggle with multi-clause sentences. Pushing faster harms understanding. Regression is a legitimate comprehension strategy. These methods were designed for adults.

Validation path

A/B test with 200+ G9-11 students. Measure comprehension, not just WPM. Only ship if comprehension maintained.

Anti-Cheat Radiant Signature

Method #15 PENDING VALIDATION
Why Gated

Students explaining sequential processes naturally produce "linear" text — not cheating. False positive rate for G5 unacceptable.

Ship gate

500+ labeled responses, precision ≥ 90% or doesn't ship.
