## 1. SUBJECT IDENTITY: THE LOGIC OF CHOICE

**Iqtisodiyot (Economics)** in the NETS ecosystem is the study of **scarcity, choice, and systems**. It teaches students how individuals, businesses, and governments manage resources to maximize welfare. It bridges the gap between mathematical logic and human behavior.

### 1.1 Core Pillars of Iqtisodiyot
- **Microeconomic Reasoning:** Understanding supply, demand, and price mechanisms.
- **Macroeconomic Modeling:** Analyzing inflation, GDP, and national fiscal policy.
- **Financial Literacy:** Managing personal wealth, banking, and investment.
- **Entrepreneurial Mindset:** Identifying market opportunities and calculating risk.

## 2. SUBJECT-SPECIFIC COGNITIVE DEMANDS

Following the Ijtimoiy Fanlari profile, Iqtisodiyot prioritizes:
1. **Systemic Modeling:** Visualizing how a change in one variable (e.g., taxes) affects the whole system.
2. **Quantitative Logic:** Applying basic arithmetic to societal problems (e.g., calculating interest or inflation).
3. **Strategic Prediction:** Forecasting outcomes based on current economic trends.
4. **Ethical Evaluation:** Balancing profit motives with social responsibility.

## 3. GAME MECHANICS & PEDAGOGY (IQTISODIYOT TUNED)

### 3.1 Primary Mechanics
- **Why Chain (Sabablar Zanjiri):** The "Gold Standard" for Economics. Mapping the ripple effects of a market shift (e.g., Oil price rises → Transport costs increase → Food prices rise).
- **Connect Four vs AI:** Strategic planning where students must anticipate the "market's" next move.
- **Codebreaker:** Decoding economic indicators (inflation rates, exchange rates) to solve a puzzle.
- **Tile Match:** Categorizing types of markets (Monopoly, Oligopoly) or sorting banking products.

### 3.2 Mnemonic Systems (Buzan Integration)
- **Radiant Summary (Mind Map):** Creating a "Budget Map" for a family or a small business using Buzan's 7 laws.
- **Memory Palace:** Using the **National Bank of Uzbekistan** or a modern **Tashkent City** skyscraper as a loci environment for macroeconomic terms.
- **TEFCAS Learning Loop:** Actively using "Feedback" from failed economic simulations to "Adjust" the student's strategy.

## 5. BOSS FORMAT: THE STRATEGIC ADVISOR

- **Format:** **Economic Case Reasoning**.
- **The Task:** "A local startup is facing rising costs due to inflation. Based on the data provided, recommend a 3-step strategy to maintain their market share and justify your choice with economic principles."
- **Evaluation:** Tier 2 LLM checks for **term usage**, **causal logic**, and **strategic feasibility**.
- **No MCQs:** All G8-11 Economics sessions end with a strategic decision-making task.
