## 1. SUBJECT IDENTITY: THE DEFENDER OF CIVILIZATION

**CHQBT (Chaqiriqqacha boshlang'ich tayyorgarlik)** in the NETS ecosystem is the study of **civic duty, national defense, and civil protection**. It is the "Duty" branch of Social Sciences. It focuses on the responsibility of the individual to the state and society, as well as the practical skills of civil defense and national security.

### 1.1 Core Pillars of CHQBT
- **Constitutional Duty:** Understanding the legal and moral obligation to defend the Motherland (O'zbekiston).
- **Military History & Heritage:** Studying the military tactics of ancestors (e.g., Amir Temur, Jaloliddin Manguberdi) and the modern Armed Forces.
- **Civil Protection:** Learning how to act in emergencies (natural disasters, industrial accidents).
- **Service & Discipline:** Developing the character and organizational skills required for national service.

## 2. SUBJECT-SPECIFIC COGNITIVE DEMANDS

Following the Ijtimoiy Fanlari profile, CHQBT prioritizes:
1. **Decision Making Under Pressure:** "What is the correct procedure when a 'Red Alert' is issued?"
2. **Causal Logic (Defense):** Understanding the relationship between national security and economic/social stability.
3. **Institutional Reasoning:** Knowing the structure and role of the Ministry of Defense, Emergency Situations, and the Mahalla.
4. **Historical Comparison:** Analyzing how ancestral defense strategies inform modern national security.

## 3. GAME MECHANICS & PEDAGOGY (CHQBT TUNED)

### 3.1 Primary Mechanics
- **Escape Room (Civil Defense Simulation):** Students must solve a series of procedural riddles to "secure" a building or coordinate an evacuation.
- **Timeline Sort (Military History):** Arranging the key battles and military reforms of Uzbek history (e.g., the Timurid military structure).
- **Tile Match (Protocol Assembly):** Matching emergency signals to their correct response actions or military ranks to their duties.
- **Why Chain (Security & Stability):** Mapping why a strong defense is necessary for a peaceful "Third Renaissance" (e.g., Secure Borders → Market Stability → Investment → Growth).

### 3.2 Mnemonic Systems (Buzan Integration)
- **Radiant Summary (Mind Map):** Creating a "Civil Defense Plan" for a school or neighborhood (using symbols for danger and safety).
- **Memory Palace:** Using the **"Victory Park" (G'alaba Bog'i)** in Tashkent as a loci environment to memorize military ranks and historical defense dates.
- **SMASHIN' SCOPE:** Creating vivid mental images for specific technical procedures (e.g., First Aid steps or Civil Defense protocols).

## 4. NATIONAL PRIDE INJECTIONS (55/45 BALANCE)

CHQBT defines the student's identity as a **Defender of the Homeland (Vatan Himoyachisi)**.

- **National Focus (55%):** Deep dives into the **Modern Armed Forces of Uzbekistan**, the "Army and Youth" initiative, and the heroic legacies of Jaloliddin Manguberdi and Sabir Rakhimov.
- **Global Focus (45%):** Understanding international humanitarian law, global security challenges (terrorism, cyberwarfare), and the role of the UN in peace-keeping.
- **Wise Role:** `Vatan Himoyachisi` (Defender of the Homeland).
- **Example Injection:** "Amir Temur o'zining 'Tuzuklar'ida aytganidek: 'Davlat - kuchli va adolatli qo'shin bilan mustahkamdir'. Bugun siz ushbu hikmatning zamonaviy ma'nosini o'rganasiz."

## 5. BOSS FORMAT: THE COORDINATOR OF DEFENSE

- **Format:** **Strategic Decision Task**.
- **The Task:** "A massive earthquake has affected a fictional district. Based on the Civil Defense protocols you've learned, create a 4-step evacuation and first-response plan, justifying your priorities using the 'Mahallabay' coordination principle."
- **Evaluation:** Tier 2 LLM checks for **procedural accuracy**, **logical prioritization**, and **civic responsibility**.
- **No MCQs:** Grade 11 CHQBT sessions culminate in a formal emergency coordination proposal.
