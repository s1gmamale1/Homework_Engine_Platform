## 1. SUBJECT IDENTITY: THE ACTIVE CITIZEN

**Fuqarolik (Civics)** in the NETS ecosystem is the study of **civil society, social responsibility, and community engagement**. It focuses on how individuals interact with their communities and the state to build a prosperous and just society.

### 1.1 Core Pillars of Fuqarolik
- **Civil Society:** Understanding the role of non-governmental organizations, the Mahalla, and community groups.
- **Civic Participation:** Learning how to engage in decision-making processes at the local and national levels.
- **Social Responsibility:** Balancing individual rights with the duty to contribute to the common good.
- **Democratic Principles:** Understanding the values of transparency, accountability, and the rule of law.

---

## 2. SUBJECT-SPECIFIC COGNITIVE DEMANDS

Following the Ijtimoiy Fanlari profile, Fuqarolik prioritizes:
1. **Institutional Reasoning:** Understanding how the "Mahalla" and other social institutions function as bridges between the individual and the state.
2. **Perspective-Taking:** Evaluating social issues from the viewpoints of different community members.
3. **Problem-Solving (Civic):** Identifying a community problem and proposing a collaborative solution.
4. **Normative Analysis:** Understanding the values that underpin a stable and flourishing society.

---

## 3. GAME MECHANICS & PEDAGOGY (FUQAROLIK TUNED)

### 3.1 Primary Mechanics
- **Territory Conquest (Mahalla Map):** Students "win" territory by solving community challenges and building local infrastructure (e.g., parks, community centers).
- **Source Comparison (Community Perspectives):** Comparing different viewpoints on a local project (e.g., building a new road) to find a consensus.
- **Why Chain (Social Stability):** Mapping how individual actions (e.g., volunteering or obeying laws) lead to national prosperity.
- **Tile Match (Civic Rights & Duties):** Categorizing actions as rights, duties, or voluntary contributions.

### 3.2 Mnemonic Systems (Buzan Integration)
- **Radiant Summary (Mind Map):** Creating a "Community Asset Map" showing the resources available in a student's local area.
- **Memory Palace:** Using a digital **"Mahalla Center"** layout to memorize the steps of a civic initiative or the functions of local government.
- **SMASHIN' SCOPE:** Creating vivid mental images for specific civic values (e.g., *Sahovat* / Generosity or *Adolat* / Justice).

## 4. NATIONAL PRIDE INJECTIONS (55/45 BALANCE)

Fuqarolik prepares the student to be an active builder of the **Third Renaissance**.

- **National Focus (55%):** Deep immersion in the **Mahalla** system, the "Obod Mahalla" initiative, and the cultural traditions of mutual aid (*Hashar*).
- **Global Focus (45%):** Comparing national civic systems with global models of civil society and international cooperation (e.g., UN Sustainable Development Goals).
- **Wise Role:** `Fuqarolik Faoli` (Civic Activist).
- **Example Injection:** "Bizning ajdodlarimiz har doim jamoa bo'lib yashashni va bir-biriga 'Hashar' orqali yordam berishni qadrlashgan. Bugun siz ushbu qadriyatni zamonaviy fuqarolik jamiyatida qanday qo'llaysiz?"

## 5. BOSS FORMAT: THE COMMUNITY COORDINATOR

- **Format:** **Civic Project Proposal**.
- **The Task:** "Your local neighborhood needs a more efficient way to manage waste. Using the 'Mahallabay' coordination model, propose a 3-step community initiative that involves residents, local businesses, and the government."
- **Evaluation:** Tier 2 LLM checks for **institutional logic**, **collaborative feasibility**, and **civic value**.
- **No MCQs:** All G6+ Civics sessions culminate in a community-oriented problem-solving task.
