## 1. SUBJECT IDENTITY: THE ARCHITECTURE OF OPPORTUNITY

**Tadbirkorlik (Entrepreneurship)** in the NETS ecosystem is the practical application of economic and legal knowledge to create value. It focuses on **opportunity recognition, risk management, and strategic execution**. It is the "Action" branch of Social Sciences.

### 1.1 Core Pillars of Tadbirkorlik
- **Opportunity Recognition:** Identifying unmet needs in the market.
- **Value Proposition:** Crafting a solution that people are willing to pay for.
- **Operational Logic:** Understanding how to build and run a business (Production, Marketing, Finance).
- **Resilience & Ethics:** Learning from failure (TEFCAS) and maintaining business integrity.

## 2. SUBJECT-SPECIFIC COGNITIVE DEMANDS

Following the Ijtimoiy Fanlari profile, Tadbirkorlik prioritizes:
1. **Strategic Planning:** "If I do X, what are the likely market reactions Y and Z?"
2. **Resource Allocation:** Choosing between competing priorities with limited capital.
3. **Persuasion & Pitching:** Communicating the value of an idea clearly and convincingly.
4. **Iterative Problem Solving:** Adjusting the business model based on customer feedback.

## 3. GAME MECHANICS & PEDAGOGY (TADBIRKORLIK TUNED)

### 3.1 Primary Mechanics
- **Connect Four vs AI (Strategic Market Entry):** Students must plan several steps ahead to "block" competitors and secure their market position.
- **Why Chain (Product-Market Fit):** Mapping why a specific business idea succeeded or failed (e.g., Idea → Prototype → User Test → Pivot → Scale).
- **Codebreaker (Market Analysis):** Decoding financial statements or market trends to find the "hidden" opportunity.
- **Tile Match (Business Model Canvas):** Sorting components of a business plan into their correct segments (Value Prop, Key Partners, Cost Structure).

### 3.2 Mnemonic Systems (Buzan Integration)
- **Radiant Summary (Mind Map):** Creating a "Business Launch Map" using Buzan's 7 laws (Multi-color, imagery, associations).
- **TEFCAS Learning Loop:** Explicitly used to analyze "Failed Startups" in Case Studies — Trial, Event, Feedback, Check, Adjust, Success.
- **Memory Palace:** Using a digital **"IT Park Tashkent"** or a **"Technopark"** layout to memorize the steps of business registration and tax compliance.

## 5. BOSS FORMAT: THE STARTUP ADVISOR

- **Format:** **Business Model Pivot Task**.
- **The Task:** "A local textile company is losing sales to international competitors. Based on the provided market data, propose a 3-step 'Pivot' strategy to regain their market share and calculate the projected ROI."
- **Evaluation:** Tier 2 LLM checks for **strategic logic**, **financial realism**, and **innovation**.
- **No MCQs:** Grade 11 Entrepreneurship culminates in a formal strategic proposal.
