▶

This document defines the content catalog architecture for NETS. It classifies all subjects into families, defines difficulty levels, learning paths, cross-subject courses, and the complete library hierarchy. It does not define Basic/Premium tier mechanics — those are handled separately by colleagues.

16 Cross-Subject Courses

Premium differentiators — each maps to an employer skills gap
▶

These courses don't belong to a single textbook subject. They synthesize skills across domains and target real-world competencies the national curriculum doesn't explicitly teach. Click any card to expand.

CSC-001
Critical Thinking
G6-G11 · L1-L3 · #1 employer deficit · Fallacy detection, bias identification, media literacy

Socratic inquiry, logical fallacy detection, cognitive bias identification, news/media literacy, argument structure analysis, evidence evaluation. Source Comparison + Error Analysis + Cloze + Why Chain mechanics.

Boss: "Here is a claim with evidence. Identify fallacies, evaluate evidence quality, state if justified."

CSC-002
Academic Writing
G9-G11 · L3-L4 · #1 technical barrier · IELTS Task 1/2, academic citations

IELTS Task 1 reports (data description, process diagrams), Task 2 essays (opinion, discussion, problem-solution), academic citations, research methods, paragraph coherence. Timed essay writing (40 min) evaluated against IELTS 4-criterion rubric.

CSC-003
Debate & Rhetoric
G7-G11 · L1-L3 · Communication gap · Parliamentary/Lincoln-Douglas formats

Logical fallacy recognition, Parliamentary debate format, Lincoln-Douglas format, World Schools format, constructive speech writing, rebuttal techniques, evidence-based argumentation.

CSC-004
Financial Literacy
G5-G11 · L1-L3 · Economic literacy · Budgeting → Banking → Investing

G5-7: budgeting, saving, needs vs. wants. G8-9: banking system, compound interest, loans, credit. G10-11: personal investing, inflation, taxation in Uzbekistan, retirement planning, crypto basics.

CSC-005
IT & Programming
G7-G11 · L1-L4 · IT talent war · Python → Data → Web/Mobile/GameDev/Cybersec

L1: Python fundamentals (OMUC 120hrs). L2: Data structures + SQL/NumPy/Pandas. L3: 5 tracks — Front-End (React), Back-End (Flask/PostgreSQL/AWS), Android (Kotlin/Jetpack), Game Dev (Unity/Unreal), Cybersecurity (Kali/Wireshark). L4: Competitive programming (Robocontest.uz).

CSC-006
AI Literacy & ML
G10-G11 · L1-L3 · 74% use AI without training · Neural nets, ethics, LLMs

Neural networks (conceptual), supervised vs. unsupervised learning, ethical AI, bias in ML, LLMs (how they work, limitations). Data labeling, evaluation metrics. Hands-on no-code ML, prompt engineering.

CSC-007
Medical School Prep
G10-G11 · L3-L4 · Medical entrance · MCAT-lite: anatomy, biochem, molecular bio

Advanced anatomy (tissue level), biochemistry (amino acids, enzyme kinetics, metabolic pathways), molecular biology (Central Dogma, gene regulation), cell biology, organic chemistry (reaction mechanisms, stereochemistry).

CSC-008
Environmental Science
G7-G11 · L1-L3 · 2030 sustainability · Climate, biodiversity, Aral Sea

Climate systems and modeling, biodiversity and conservation, pollution and remediation, Aral Sea, water management, desertification, global climate agreements.

CSC-009
GIS & Cartography
G8-G11 · L1-L3 · Geospatial literacy · Map projections → GIS → remote sensing

Map projections, topographic interpretation, satellite imagery analysis, GIS fundamentals (layers, queries, spatial joins), data visualization (heat maps, choropleth, 3D terrain), remote sensing basics.

CSC-010
Law & Civic Rights
G9-G11 · L1-L3 · Civic literacy · Constitution, human rights, civil/criminal law

Constitutional law (2023 Constitution), human rights framework (UDHR), civil law basics, criminal law fundamentals, administrative law, legal reasoning, Uzbek court system.

CSC-011
Applied Economics
G10-G11 · L1-L3 · Economic literacy · Micro → Macro → Entrepreneurship

Micro: supply/demand, market structures, pricing. Macro: GDP, inflation, unemployment, monetary/fiscal policy. Uzbekistan's economy: agriculture, textiles, mining, services. Entrepreneurship: business plans, market research, risk assessment.

CSC-012
Historiography
G9-G11 · L2-L4 · Critical history · Annales/Marxist/Liberal/Post-colonial schools

Historiographical schools, source criticism methods, archival research basics, comparative history, Central Asian historiography (Silk Road, Timurid, Colonial, Soviet, Post-Soviet interpretations).

CSC-013
Creative Lab (Digital Art)
G5-G11 · L1-L3 · Digital creative skills · Illustration → UI/UX → 3D

Digital illustration fundamentals, color theory, composition, UI/UX basics, 3D modeling intro, Uzbek traditional patterns in digital form, portfolio building. No scoring — encouraging feedback only.

CSC-014
Music Production
G7-G11 · L1-L3 · Creative industry · DAW → Sound design → Digital composition

DAW basics (Logic, Ableton), sound design, MIDI and digital instruments, mixing and mastering, digital composition incorporating Uzbek traditional music elements.

CSC-015
Sports Science
G8-G11 · L1-L3 · Health literacy · Kinesiology → Nutrition → Exercise physiology

Kinesiology (muscle groups, biomechanics), sports nutrition (macros, hydration), exercise physiology (energy systems, training principles), injury prevention, sports psychology.

CSC-016
Mathematical Logic
G8-G11 · L1-L3 · Formal reasoning · Propositional logic → Proofs → Applications

Propositional logic (truth tables, connectives), predicate logic (quantifiers), boolean algebra (De Morgan's, logic gates), proof techniques (direct, contradiction, induction, pigeonhole), set theory, applications to programming and circuit design.

Placement Suggestions

Where everything goes in the product, with triggers
▶

The bridge between library (what exists) and UX (what students see).

📊 Weekly PISA Lesson

Weekly for ALL tiers. Basic: standard L1-L2 PISA session (~20 min). Premium: expanded content pool (L3-L4 items, AI analysis, personalized PISA study plans). 7-day async rolling window. Rotates: Math → Reading → Science.

Trigger: Monday or first login of week
🎓 Cross-Subject Courses

"Courses" tab in Library. Cards with title, grade, level, progress, skills gap. Recommended based on PISA weakness + direction interest.

Trigger: PISA weakness / performance pattern
📈 Extended Content (L2)

Unlocks at ≥80% L1 success (flow-state headroom — 70% is the floor, not the gate). Labeled "Chuqurroq" (Deeper). Optional extension after standard chapter. DTM path students get it automatically.

Trigger: "Ready for a deeper challenge?" after strong completion
📝 DTM Practice Exams

Standalone sessions — not 7-phase flow. 90 MCQs, 180 min timer. Results: score per block, vs. 68.0 threshold. Weekly practice recommended. ADR REQUIRED: "Assessment Mode" exception to 7-phase immutability. DTM Drills (10-15 Q, 15-20 min) CAN fit 7-phase and need no ADR.

Trigger: G9-11 on DTM path only
🏆 Olympiad Content

Requires ≥80% L2 qualification. "Chempion" challenges on Today surface. Gold border. Monthly practice events simulate district/regional conditions.

Trigger: Qualification + teacher recommendation
📚 Supplemental Libraries

On-demand reference — not sessions. Formula sheets as in-session hints. Worked examples browsable from Library. Vocab lists with spaced repetition integration.

Trigger: Student-initiated browsing or in-session hint tap

Buzan Cognitive Science

TEFCAS engine, memory systems, BOST, Mind Mapping, speed reading
▶

Tony Buzan's research on Radiant Thinking, memory systems, and the Organic Study Technique provides the cognitive science backbone for how NETS delivers content.

TEFCAS — Core Engine Logic

Trial → Event → Feedback → Check → Adjust → Success. The foundational logic for NETS's difficulty adaptation engine. TEFCAS models the student's learning cycle; IRT (from UNIFIED spec) calibrates item difficulty. They are complementary, not interchangeable.

StepDefinitionNETS Mapping
TrialInitial attemptEvery question; Final Boss is the ultimate Trial
EventThe outcomeScore, HP changes, visual/audio response
FeedbackSensory info from eventSocratic guiding feedback (not answers)
CheckComparison vs. GoalIRT checks proficiency against item difficulty
AdjustModify approachIRT adjusts difficulty to 70-80% flow zone
SuccessAchieving targetXP gain, badge, boss defeat, streak

Key Maxim: "There is no such thing as failure, only feedback." → "Hali emas!" for G5.

Memory Systems

Link System

Vivid chain of images where each item triggers the next. Best for sequential lists and process steps.

Phase 1 (Memory Sprint): Link System chains for vocabulary clusters instead of standard MCQs.

Phase 2 (Story Mode): The narrative itself IS a Link System — each story event triggers the next.

Peg System

Mental hooks for random access to data. Number-Shape: 1=Spear, 2=Swan, 3=Heart, 4=Sailboat. Number-Rhyme: 1=Sun, 2=Shoe, 3=Tree, 4=Door.

Phase 1: Quick-fire Number-Shape Pegs for math rules. Phase 3: Tic Tac Toe — convert number to Peg image to place a mark.

Major System

Phonetic code mapping digits to consonant sounds: 0=s/z, 1=t/d, 2=n, 3=m, 4=r, 5=l, 6=j/sh/ch, 7=k/g, 8=f/v, 9=p/b. Converts numbers into memorable words/images.

Example: Navoi's birth year 1441 → "TRRT" → Image: a "TuRTle" wearing a Tara.

Memory Palace (Method of Loci)

Place vivid images at specific locations in a familiar spatial environment.

Default Loci: Registan Square. Students place science concepts (cell organelles, physics formulas) in specific corners of Sher-Dor Madrasah.

Phase 5 (Consolidation): Memory Palace serves as the "mnemonic lock" before the Final Boss.

All AI-generated mnemonics must maximize these qualities:

SynaesthesiaMovementAssociationVitalityHumorImaginationNumberSymbolismColorOrderPositiveExaggeration

AI tutor prompts students to add at least 3 SMASHIN' SCOPE qualities to their own mental images.
