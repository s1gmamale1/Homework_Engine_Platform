1. What Is This Family

"Tabiat" translates to nature. This family studies how natural systems work. Every concept is either (a) an observation about the natural world, (b) a classification organizing observations, or (c) a causal model explaining why a system behaves as it does. Knowledge is built through a shared scientific method: observe → classify → hypothesize → explain → predict.

What it teaches: Scientific thinking through discovery, experimentation, and causal modeling.

What it focuses on:

  • Causal explanation and system modeling.
  • Classification, taxonomy, and diagram literacy.
  • Experimental reasoning and hypothesis formation.
  • Formal notation (equations, periodic table, force diagrams) QNT
  • Multi-step derivation and error detection QNT
  • Data interpretation from graphs, maps, satellite imagery.

What it is NOT about: Pure axiomatic derivation (now Math-only Aniq Fanlar), interpretive/opinion-based reasoning (Ijtimoiy Fanlari), linguistic form (Til Fanlari).

Dual identity (post Library Framework v0.6): Physics and Chemistry migrated into this family. Tabiy Fanlar therefore carries two coexisting cognitive modes — OBS observational (Bio/Geo/Astro/Combined) and QNT quantitative (Physics/Chemistry). Rules below apply to both unless tagged.

Permeable boundary with Aniq Fanlar (now Math-only): Quantitative natural sciences explicitly reference Math for calculation (population growth rates, stoichiometry, kinematics). The boundary is crossed by reference, not content migration.

Cognitive Demand Profile (ranked): Causal Explanation (1), Concept Model Construction (2), Classification & Taxonomy (3), Diagram Literacy (4), Formal Notation QNT (5), Multi-step Calculation QNT (6), Experimental Reasoning (7), Data Interpretation (8), Error Detection (9), Memorization (10).

3. What This Family Focuses On
  • Discovery Narrative Framing: Every concept introduced as a problem someone faced — "Scientists noticed fossils of the same species on continents separated by oceans. How could this be?"
  • Concrete-to-Abstract Progression (CPA adapted): Real specimen/photograph/map → classification system → abstract model. Mandatory for QNT formulas.
  • Uzbek-Context Anchoring: Aral Sea for environmental science, Zarafshan river for ecology, Tien Shan for geology, Ulugh Beg observatory for astronomy. Local first, global after.
  • Diagram as First-Class Representation: Unlike Math (formulas dominate), Tabiy Fanlar treats diagrams as primary. Students read, label, interpret, and produce. Notebook Capture heavily used.
  • Dual Coding Mandatory: Every concept paired with visual + verbal simultaneously. Text-only presentation banned.
  • Cross-Referencing with Aniq Fanlar: Quantitative needs (growth rates, stoichiometry, kinematics) reference Math explicitly. Boundary permeable for learning.

Do NOT focus on: Axiomatic proof (Math territory), free-form creative expression, opinion-based debates, linguistic analysis.

4. Best Methods & Games

Primary Mechanics

Story Mode (Discovery Arc)

Good: Frame photosynthesis as a historical mystery — "Why do plants grow heavier than the soil they consume?"
Bad: Start with "Photosynthesis is the process by which..."

Notebook Capture (Diagrams + Derivations)

Good OBS: Draw and label a cell's organelles by hand, photograph the result.
Good QNT: Derive kinematic equation on paper, upload photo for partial-credit rubric.
Bad: Type the definition of "nucleus."

Why Chain (Causal Reasoning)

Good: L1 "Why do leaves change color?" → L2 "Why do chlorophyll levels drop?" → L3 "Why does day length trigger this?"
Chain depth: 2 levels G5, 3 levels G6–8, 4 levels G9+.

Memory Palace (Spatial Mnemonic)

Good: Place organ systems at Registan hotspots; recall by "walking" through.
Good QNT: Map periodic-table groups onto courtyard positions.

Worked Examples QNT

Good: Step-by-step stoichiometry with two steps blanked; student fills in.

Error Analysis QNT

Good: Find the error in a "solved" kinematics problem where units were dropped.

Buzan Alternatives (Phase 5 — select by content structure)

  • Radiant Summary (Mind Map): Hierarchical content — taxonomy, organ systems, periodic groups.
  • Link System: Sequential content — water cycle, photosynthesis, reaction mechanisms, mitosis.
  • Memory Palace: Spatial content — anatomy, biomes, solar system, force diagrams.
  • Peg System: Discrete-fact content — constants, atomic numbers, geological periods.

Banned / Not Recommended

  • Word Ladder Climb: Purely linguistic. No fit.
  • Memory Match Blitz: Visual memory without cognitive gate.
  • Movement Breaks G5+: Breaks sustained cognitive effort.
  • Speed Reading: Natural-science problems need deliberate reading.

Design Principle

Every Boss question must require reasoning, not recall. For OBS: "why" and "how" dominate over "what." For QNT: the student must SHOW THEIR WORK — typed or photographed via Notebook Capture. A Boss question answerable by pattern-matching a memorized formula is a failed Boss question.

Example Stems

Biology OBS: "Explain why leaves change color in autumn. What is the underlying biological process?"
Ecology OBS: "If wolves disappear from a forest, predict what happens to the deer population and explain the cascade."
Geography OBS: "Using Earth's axial tilt, predict what season it will be in Argentina in July. Justify your reasoning."
Astronomy OBS: "Predict why Mars appears brighter in certain months, using orbital principles."
Physics QNT: "A 2 kg block slides down a 30° frictionless incline. Derive its acceleration and show all steps."
Chemistry QNT: "Balance the combustion reaction for propane. Then calculate the mass of CO₂ produced from 10 g of propane."
