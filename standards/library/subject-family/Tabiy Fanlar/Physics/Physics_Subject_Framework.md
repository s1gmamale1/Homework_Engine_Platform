## 1. What Physics Teaches

Physics in NETS is the subject where **math meets reality**. Formulas are not isolated symbols. They are models of motion, force, energy, electricity, waves, heat, and matter in the real world.

- Physics is **bidirectional** by nature:
  - **real world -> formula**: observe a phenomenon, identify variables, build the equation
  - **formula -> real world**: use the equation to predict what will happen
- Physics belongs to the **QNT branch** of Tabiy Fanlar, so it inherits:
  - **CPA progression** from Aniq Fanlar
  - **Worked Examples**
  - **Error Analysis**
- But Physics is not just formal manipulation. It is also experimental:
  - observe
  - hypothesize
  - model
  - test
  - predict
- **Critical debugging** is central. Students regularly make errors in substitution, unit conversion, sign handling, diagram reading, and derivation sequence. Physics must train them to locate the exact mistake, not just compare final answers.
- **Formula density is high.** Many chapters introduce **3-8 formulas** that must be memorized, interpreted, and applied under the correct conditions.
- **Units and dimensional analysis are non-negotiable.** A numerically correct answer with the wrong units is still physically wrong.
- **Diagrams are core content, not decoration.** Force vectors, circuit diagrams, ray paths, and wave sketches must be produced by the student.
- The agent's job is to **transform formula-heavy textbook pages into physically meaningful homework**. If the textbook presents formulas as given facts, NETS must show where the formula comes from and why it matters.

**What Physics is NOT about:**
- Memorizing formulas without understanding the physical situation
- Plugging numbers into equations with no model or diagram
- Skipping units
- Treating derivations as pure algebra detached from reality

**Core production rule:** Every new formula should feel like a **discovered tool**, not a commandment dropped from the textbook.

## 3. Content Production Rules by Grade Band

### G7-8 (Foundation)

- Introduce **3-5 new formulas** per session at most.
- Every formula must pass through **CPA**:
  - **Concrete** -> physical scenario
  - **Pictorial** -> diagram, vector sketch, or visual model
  - **Abstract** -> symbolic equation
- Use **Worked Examples with completion effect**. Show the first steps, then require the student to finish the derivation.
- Include **Error Analysis at least every 2 sessions**. Students must find both **where** and **why** the derivation broke.
- Drill **units on every answer**. `5` is incomplete. `5 m/s` is a physics answer.
- Build **bidirectional pairs** into sessions:
  - one task from observation to formula
  - one task from formula to real-world prediction
- Use **Notebook Capture** for hand-drawn force diagrams, simple machine sketches, and motion diagrams.
- If the textbook is dry, make it alive: wrap mechanics and energy problems in engineering or safety scenarios instead of flat number substitution.

**Good production pattern:**  
"A trolley rolls down a ramp. First draw the motion situation. Then identify the known quantities. Then write the equation."

**Good Boss pattern:**  
"Calculate the force needed to accelerate a 3 kg object at 4 m/s^2. Draw the force diagram and show all steps."

**Bad production pattern:**  
"Memorize these formulas and solve 20 similar exercises."

### G9-10 (Development)

- Increase to **5-8 formulas** per session when the chapter supports it, including derived relationships.
- **Circuit diagrams become mandatory** in electricity units. Students should draw them, not just read them.
- Use **dimensional analysis as a verification tool**, not as an optional add-on.
- **Error Analysis should appear every session** in this band because derivations are longer and more failure-prone.
- Push **Why Chain** to 3-4 levels for physical reasoning:
  - "Why does current decrease?"
  - "What role does resistance play?"
  - "What property of the material affects resistance?"
- Require **diagram + formula + interpretation** as a normal trio, not as special enrichment.
- If the textbook is dry, make it alive: turn flat electricity, pressure, or wave problems into engineering and system-design decisions.

**Good production pattern:**  
"A room's lighting circuit keeps overheating. Draw the circuit, calculate the total resistance, and explain why the wires warm up."

**Good Boss pattern:**  
"Design a circuit with 2 resistors in parallel. Calculate total resistance and current. Draw the circuit diagram."

**Bad production pattern:**  
"State Ohm's law and define current, voltage, and resistance."

### G10-11 (Advanced)

- Require **full multi-step derivations from first principles** where the textbook topic allows it.
- Use **combined-formula problems** that need 2-3 equations in sequence.
- Keep **Error Analysis** on long derivations: at least one 5+ step debugging task in major sessions.
- Require **real-world modeling**: the student should sometimes build the equation from the situation instead of being handed the formula.
- Expect **unit verification and physical interpretation** after calculation, not just the numeric result.
- Boss work should include **derivation + diagram/model + defense of reasoning**.
- If the textbook is dry, make it alive: frame advanced units as research lab, energy infrastructure, orbital mechanics, or systems engineering problems.

**Good production pattern:**  
"You are checking whether a satellite can maintain orbit at this altitude. Build the relationship from the forces, derive the speed, and verify the units."

**Good Boss pattern:**  
"A satellite orbits Earth at 400 km altitude. Derive its orbital velocity from first principles. Show all steps and explain what the result means physically."

**Bad production pattern:**  
"List all formulas from the chapter in your notebook."

## 4. Game Mechanics — Physics Specific Filtering

Physics inherits the QNT branch of Tabiy Fanlar and the Aniq Fanlar scaffolding set, but it uses them in a more experimental and diagram-heavy way.

**Always-primary for Physics:**
- **Worked Examples with Completion** — every session
- **Error Analysis** — primary, not secondary
- **CPA Progression** — mandatory for every new formula

### Primary Mechanics

#### **Worked Examples with Completion**
- This is the **core scaffolding mechanic** for Physics.
- Students should see the derivation path before being expected to reproduce it independently.
- **Good Example:** "Step 1: `F = ma`. Step 2: `F = 5 kg x ___`. Step 3: `F = ___ N`."
- **Bad Example:** "Solve `F = ma` for 20 different values."

#### **Error Analysis**
- This is **PRIMARY** because physics errors often hide inside multi-step derivations.
- The student must identify:
  - where the error occurred
  - what rule was violated
  - how to repair the derivation
- **Good Example:** "A student calculated `v = 10 m / 2 s = 20 m/s`. Find the error and correct it."
- **Bad Example:** "Is this answer correct? Yes or No."

#### **Tile Match**
- Use it for **formula assembly**, **equation completion**, and **unit matching**.
- **Good Example:** "Drag components into `F = m x a`: `[force] [=] [mass] [x] [acceleration]`."
- **Good Example:** "Match each unit to its quantity: `m/s -> tezlik`, `N -> kuch`, `kg -> massa`."
- **Bad Example:** "Match `Newton` to its dictionary definition."

#### **Notebook Capture**
- Use it for **force diagrams, circuit diagrams, ray diagrams, and wave sketches**.
- In Physics, diagrams are part of the answer, not optional enrichment.
- **Good Example:** "Draw the free-body diagram for a block on an incline. Label all forces with vectors."
- **Bad Example:** "Write Newton's 3 laws."
- **Frequency rule:** minimum **every 2 sessions**. In Boss work, use it **every session**.

#### **Speed Match**
- Use it for **formula recall under time pressure** once the concept has already been taught.
- **Good Example:** "`v = ? -> s/t`, `F = ? -> ma`, `P = ? -> F/A`."
- **Bad Example:** "Define velocity."

#### **CPA Progression**
- This is a **mandatory production rule**, not an optional mechanic.
- Every new formula must move through:
  - **Concrete:** real physical event
  - **Pictorial:** diagram or visual model
  - **Abstract:** symbolic relationship
- **Good Example:** ball rolling down a ramp -> motion sketch with arrows -> `v = u + at`
- **Bad Example:** "The formula for velocity is `v = u + at`. Now solve 10 problems."

### Secondary Mechanics

- **Adaptive Quiz** — formula application drill after the concept is established
- **Why Chain** — 3-4 levels for physical reasoning and cause-effect logic
- **Memory Palace** — formula families grouped spatially
- **Peer Teaching** — G9+: explain a law, derivation, or unit rule to a classmate

### Interactive Picks

| Game | Best Use |
|---|---|
| **Connect Four vs AI** | Multi-step derivation planning and strategic calculation sequence |
| **Codebreaker** | Formula and unit decoding, especially symbol-heavy chapters |
| **Puzzle Lock** | Derivation step ordering and process reconstruction |
| **Reaction Chain** | Cause-effect chains such as force -> acceleration -> velocity -> displacement |

### Phase 5 Buzan Options

- **Peg System** — formula recall and constants
- **Radiant Summary** — formula family hierarchy: mechanics -> kinematics -> dynamics -> energy
- **Link System** — derivation sequences as memorable step chains
- **Memory Palace** — lab equipment, system parts, or formula families placed in spatial anchors

### Games to Avoid

- **Word Ladder** — linguistic, not physics
- **Listening/Dictation** — language-family mechanics, not appropriate here
- **Creative Lab** — not a core fit for a QNT physics session
- **Memory Match Blitz** — usually too shallow for physics reasoning unless used narrowly for formula-unit pairing

**Dual-Catalog inheritance:** Phase 3 still requires at least **1 Interactive** and **2 Default** mechanics (see [[UNIFIED-Buzan §5.3]]).

## 5. Boss Format — Physics Specific

Physics uses the **QNT Boss archetype** from Tabiy Fanlar: **show-the-derivation**.

- **Notebook Capture is required in every Boss session** for diagram submission.
- A strong Physics Boss requires:
  - derivation
  - diagram or model
  - unit verification
  - physical interpretation
- Bosses should not stop at "find the number." They should also ask: **What does this result mean?**
- **HP, stars, hints, and damage rules** inherit unchanged from the parent specs.
- **Failure framing:** use TEFCAS language, e.g. **"Hali emas!"**

| Grade Band | Boss Shape | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **G7-8** | Multi-step derivation + hand-drawn diagram | "Calculate the force needed to accelerate a 3 kg object at 4 m/s^2. Draw the force diagram. Show all steps." |
| **G9-10** | System design or analysis + calculation + diagram | "Design a circuit with 2 resistors in parallel. Calculate total resistance and current. Draw the circuit diagram." |
| **G11** | Extended derivation + real-world application + defend reasoning | "A satellite orbits Earth at 400 km altitude. Derive its orbital velocity from first principles. Verify the units and explain the result." |

**Good Boss design:** requires derivation + diagram + physical interpretation.

**Bad Boss design:**  
"What is Newton's second law?"

## 6. Story Mode & Real-Life Challenge — Physics Adaptations

### Story Mode

Story Mode in Physics should feel like **discovery with CPA flow**, not like a textbook paragraph disguised as narrative.

- Segment A should show the **real phenomenon**.
- Segment B should turn it into a **diagram or model**.
- Segment C should move into the **equation and derivation**.

**Good Story Mode (G8):**  
"Engineers at Afrosiyob high-speed rail need to calculate braking distance. Segment A: observe the train slowing down. Segment B: draw the motion diagram. Segment C: derive the relationship using `v^2 = u^2 + 2as`."

**Bad Story Mode:**  
"The formula for braking distance is given below. Solve 5 problems."

### Real-Life Challenge

In Physics, the student must become the **engineer, analyst, or physicist**, not a spectator.

| Grade Band | Role | Scenario |
|---|---|---|
| **G7-8** | Bosh muhandis | "You are designing a playground slide. Calculate the speed a child reaches at the bottom and decide whether it is safe." |
| **G9-10** | Energetika tahlilchi | "You are analyzing an electrical setup. Compute current, identify the overload risk, and recommend a safer configuration." |
| **G10-11** | Eksperimental fizik | "You are testing a system in the lab. Build the equation from measurements, calculate the result, and explain the physical meaning." |
| **G11** | Yadro fizikasi mutaxassisi | "You are evaluating a high-level physics case involving orbital or atomic behavior. Derive the result and defend the model choice." |

**Uzbek-context anchors that fit Physics well:**
- Afrosiyob rail
- Navoi Solar Plant
- Tashkent metro
- Charvak dam hydroelectric systems
- Uzbek science and engineering infrastructure

**Bad Example:**  
"Calculate the velocity of a 2 kg object."

**Production rule:** Real-Life Challenge must give the student a role, a real system, and a reason the calculation matters.

## 7. Curriculum Touchpoints

- The **textbook chapter remains the content source**.
- Physics textbooks are often strong on formulas but weaker on physical intuition. The agent must add the **why this matters** layer without changing the chapter target.
- Every major formula should have a **real-world anchor before the abstract equation appears**.
- Physics in NETS should always preserve the **real world <-> model** connection.
- Transform rule:
  - textbook: "`F = ma`. Solve these problems."
  - NETS: "An engineer needs to know how much force is required to move this system. Let's model it, draw it, derive it, and test the result."
- This framework is **textbook-only**. Do not inject external premium-course logic or unrelated exam-prep tracks.

## Technical Rules
1. **Inheritance:** Physics inherits all Tabiy Fanlar family rules first, then applies the QNT filter and Aniq Fanlar carryovers.
2. **Starts at G7:** Physics production begins at Grade 7. Do not create G5-6 patterns for this subject.
3. **CPA Mandatory:** Every new formula must move through Concrete -> Pictorial -> Abstract.
4. **Error Analysis Priority:** Error Analysis is primary and should appear at least every 2 sessions, and effectively every session in higher grades.
5. **Bidirectional Thinking:** Every session should train both real-world-to-formula and formula-to-real-world reasoning.
6. **Units Rule:** Every numerical answer must include correct units and, where appropriate, dimensional verification.
7. **Diagram Rule:** Notebook Capture should appear in most sessions and in every Boss session because diagrams are core content.
