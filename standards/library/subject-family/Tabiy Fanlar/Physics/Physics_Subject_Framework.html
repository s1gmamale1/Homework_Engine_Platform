1. What Physics Teaches

Physics in NETS is the subject where math meets reality. Formulas are models of motion, force, energy, electricity, waves, heat, and matter in the physical world.

  • Bidirectional Thinking: Students must go real world -> formula and formula -> real world.
  • QNT Heritage: Physics inherits CPA progression, Worked Examples, and Error Analysis from its Aniq Fanlar origins.
  • Experimental Logic: Observe -> hypothesize -> model -> test -> predict.
  • Critical Debugging: Students must find where a derivation fails, not just compare final answers.
  • Formula Density: Chapters often add 3-8 formulas that must be memorized and applied in the right conditions.
  • Units Matter: A number without correct units is not a complete physics answer.
  • Diagrams Are Core: Force vectors, circuits, rays, and waves must be drawn by the student.
  • Transformation: NETS turns textbook formulas from given facts into discovered tools with physical meaning.

Core Rule: Every new formula should feel discovered through physical context, not dropped in from nowhere.

3. Content Production Rules by Grade Band

G7-8 (Foundation)

  • 3-5 new formulas per session maximum.
  • Every formula must follow CPA: Concrete -> Pictorial -> Abstract.
  • Worked Examples use the completion effect.
  • Error Analysis appears at least every 2 sessions.
  • Every answer must include correct units.
  • Notebook Capture required for force diagrams, motion sketches, and simple machines.
Good Pattern: "A trolley rolls down a ramp. Draw the motion situation, identify known values, then write the equation."
Bad Pattern: "Memorize these formulas and solve 20 similar exercises."

G9-10 (Development)

  • 5-8 formulas per session when the chapter supports it.
  • Circuit diagrams are mandatory in electricity units.
  • Dimensional analysis is used as a verification tool.
  • Error Analysis should appear every session.
  • Require diagram + formula + physical interpretation together.
Good Pattern: "A room's lighting circuit keeps overheating. Draw it, calculate total resistance, and explain the heat."
Bad Pattern: "State Ohm's law and define current, voltage, and resistance."

G10-11 (Advanced)

  • Require full multi-step derivations from first principles where appropriate.
  • Use combined-formula problems with 2-3 equations in sequence.
  • Include long-form Error Analysis for 5+ step derivations.
  • Expect modeling from scenario to equation, not just substitution.
Good Pattern: "Check whether a satellite can maintain orbit at this altitude. Build the relationship, derive the speed, and verify units."
Bad Pattern: "List all formulas from the chapter in your notebook."
4. Game Mechanics — Physics Specific

Primary Mechanics

  • Worked Examples with Completion: Mandatory every session.
  • Error Analysis: Primary, not secondary.
  • Tile Match: Formula assembly and unit matching.
  • Notebook Capture: Force diagrams, circuits, ray paths, wave sketches.
  • Speed Match: Fast formula recall after concepts are taught.
  • CPA Progression: Mandatory for every new formula.
Good Worked Example: "Step 1: F = ma. Step 2: F = 5 kg x ___. Step 3: F = ___ N."
Good Error Analysis: "A student calculated v = 10 m / 2 s = 20 m/s. Find the error and correct it."
Good Notebook Capture: "Draw the free-body diagram for a block on an incline. Label all vectors."
Bad Pattern: "Write Newton's 3 laws."

5. Boss Format

QNT Branch: Show-the-derivation + diagram + unit verification + physical interpretation.

  • G7-8: Multi-step derivation with hand-drawn force or motion diagram.
  • G9-10: Circuit or system analysis with calculation and diagram.
  • G11: Extended derivation with real-world application and defended reasoning.
  • Notebook Capture: Required in every Boss session.
  • Failure: Use TEFCAS framing ("Hali emas!").
Good Boss (G11): "A satellite orbits Earth at 400 km altitude. Derive its orbital velocity, verify units, and explain the result."
Bad Boss: "What is Newton's second law?"
6. Story Mode & Real-Life Challenge

Story Mode

Must follow CPA discovery flow: real phenomenon -> diagram/model -> equation/derivation.

Good Example: "Engineers at Afrosiyob high-speed rail need braking distance. Observe the train, draw the motion diagram, then derive with v^2 = u^2 + 2as."
Bad Example: "The formula for braking distance is given below. Solve 5 problems."

Real-Life Challenge

  • Bosh muhandis: Playground slide safety and speed calculations.
  • Energetika tahlilchi: Electrical load and efficiency scenarios.
  • Eksperimental fizik: Build equations from measured lab conditions.
  • Yadro fizikasi mutaxassisi: High-level orbital or atomic analysis.

Uzbek anchors: Afrosiyob rail, Navoi Solar Plant, Tashkent metro, Charvak dam.
