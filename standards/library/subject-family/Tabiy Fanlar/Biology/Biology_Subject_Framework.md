## 1. What Biology Teaches

Biology is an **observation-first, classification-heavy, terminology-dense, visually dependent** subject.

- Students must learn to **see** natural systems, **classify** what they observe, and then **explain** how and why those systems work.
- Biology carries the heaviest terminology load of any science subject. Chapters often introduce **15-30 new terms**.
- Diagrams are first-class representations. A student who cannot draw and label a cell, organ system, or food web has not truly mastered the concept.
- Biological causal chains are often long: photosynthesis, digestion, ecological cascades, inheritance, and molecular mechanisms all require linked reasoning.
- The agent's job is to **transform flat textbook lists of terms and labels into discovery-based homework**. Textbooks often present biology as labeled pictures plus definitions. NETS must turn that into: observation -> classification -> explanation.
- If the textbook is dry, Story Mode should begin with a strange observation or a real biological problem rather than a list of facts.

**Biology is NOT about:**
- Formula manipulation as the primary mode of learning.
- Memorizing definitions without system understanding.
- Text-only descriptions with no visual anchor.

**Core production rule:** In Biology, **observation comes before explanation** and **diagram comes before paragraph** whenever possible.

## 1b. Phase 0-A Panel Construction Guide — Biology

Biology is OBS branch: panels open with a **biological mystery or observation**, not a definition. The student should feel like a field researcher encountering something unexpected.

**Panel-by-panel recipe (8-10 sentences for Panels 1-5, 5-10 sentences for Panels 6-8):**

| Panel | Purpose | Biology Construction |
|-------|---------|---------------------|
| **1 — Hook** | Biological mystery | Open with something observable and strange. A wilting plant, a color change, an unexpected animal behavior, a disease symptom. **G5-6:** "Tashkent Botanika bog'ida bitta gul bir kechada so'ldi. Yonidagi gullar hali tirik." **G9-11:** "Laboratoriyada ikki xil hujayra namunasi bor — biri tez bo'linmoqda, ikkinchisi to'xtagan. Nima farq?" Never open with "Bugun biz ... o'rganamiz." |
| **2 — Context** | Organism/system/habitat | Name the organism, body system, or ecosystem. Set the scene with an Uzbek anchor where natural: Orol dengizi, Toshkent Botanika bog'i, Zarafshon daryosi, mahalliy flora/fauna. "Bu gul — Atirgul (Rosa). U Markaziy Osiyo iqlimiga moslashgan." Show a visual description of the specimen or habitat. |
| **3 — First Observation** | Notice biological details | Present 2-3 observable clues that connect to the chapter topic. **G5-6:** "Barglar sariq, poyada qo'ng'ir dog'lar bor, tuproq quruq." **G7-9:** "Mikroskop ostida hujayra devorining yo'qligi ko'rinmoqda. Yadro aniq, lekin xloroplastlar yo'q." **G10-11:** "Genotyp tahlili: Aa × Aa. Naslda kutilgan nisbat 3:1, lekin kuzatilgan nisbat 2.8:1.2." |
| **4 — Classify / Compare** | Sort or group observations | Ask the student to organize what they see. "Quyidagi organizmlarni guruhlang: sutemizuvchi, baliq, sudralib yuruvchi." Or: "Sog'lom va kasallangan hujayra orasidagi 3 ta farqni aniqlang." This panel trains the classification instinct before explanation. |
| **5 — Question Formation** | Core biological question | The chapter's central question emerges. Write it as a genuine puzzle. **G5-6:** "Nima uchun bu o'simlik qo'shnisiga qaraganda kamroq o'sdi?" **G7-9:** "Qaysi organoid yo'qligi hujayrani hayvon hujayrasiga aylantiradi?" **G10-11:** "Nima uchun kuzatilgan genotip nisbati kutilganidan farq qiladi?" |
| **6 — BOST Goal** | Personal learning target | "Bugun men ___ ni bilmoqchiman." Biology-specific examples: "Bugun men fotosintez bosqichlarini bilmoqchiman." / "Bugun men yurak qanday ishlashini bilmoqchiman." / "Bugun men DNK replikatsiyasini bilmoqchiman." Store for Phase 7 recall. |
| **7 — Preview** | Session roadmap teaser | "Avval organizmlarni tanib olasiz (tezkor viktorina), keyin kashfiyot hikoyasiga kirasiz, keyin Tile Match o'yinida organizmlarni tasniflaysiz." Name at least one specific game. |
| **8 — Motivational Close** | Inspiration | Connect to biological discovery. "Har bir buyuk kashfiyot kuzatishdan boshlangan — Siz bugun birinchi qadamni qo'yyapsiz." Or: "Abu Ali ibn Sino tibbiyotni kuzatishdan boshlagan. Siz ham." Always "Siz." |

## 2. Grade-Band Progression

| Grade | Focus | Key Content | Production Level | Boss |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **G5-6** | Observation + basic classification | Plants, animals, human body basics, simple ecosystems | Guided: label diagrams, sort organisms, 2-3 sentence explanations | Diagram labeling + short causal answer |
| **G7** | System-level understanding | Cell biology, plant/animal systems, basic ecology | Prompted: describe a system, explain a process in 5 sentences | Multi-part explanation with diagram |
| **G8** | Organ systems + deeper ecology | Human organ systems, food webs, environmental impact | Prompted -> Free: paragraph explaining a system | Explain a causal chain (3+ steps) |
| **G9** | Cell biology deep dive + genetics intro | Cell division, DNA basics, inheritance patterns | Free: paragraph with evidence | Explain mechanism + predict outcome |
| **G10** | Molecular biology + population ecology | Protein synthesis, population dynamics, evolution basics | Free: multi-paragraph with evidence | Analyze + predict from data |
| **G11** | Advanced genetics + molecular + ecology | Genetic engineering, molecular mechanisms, ecosystem modeling | Independent: extended analytical | Full reasoning + evidence-based prediction |

## 3. Content Production Rules by Grade Band

### G5-6

- Target **10-15 new terms** per session.
- Every new term should get: **diagram or photo + Uzbek definition + example in context**.
- Start every concept with a **concrete observation**: photo, specimen, body part, habitat, or familiar Uzbek ecological example.
- Classification tasks should be common: sort organisms, label body parts, group by visible features.
- **Notebook Capture** should appear every **2 sessions** for hand-drawn diagrams.
- Memory Sprint should revisit prior organism names, body labels, and simple category knowledge.
- If the textbook is dry, make it alive: start with a strange observation from Uzbek nature or a concrete biological mystery.

**Good production pattern:**  
"Scientists at Tashkent Botanical Garden noticed one plant in the same row growing differently. Observe the leaf shapes, compare the roots, and decide what is different."

**Good Boss pattern:**  
"Label the parts of the flower and explain what one part does in 2 sentences."

**Bad production pattern:**  
"Memorize the names of these 12 plant parts from the list."

### G7-9

- Target **15-20 new terms** per session.
- Use system-level diagrams constantly: cell structure, organ systems, ecosystems, and food webs.
- **Notebook Capture** should appear at least every **2 sessions** and often more.
- Why Chain can run to **3 levels** in G7 and deeper in G8-9 when the chapter supports it.
- Story Mode should follow discovery narratives: observe -> hypothesize -> test with evidence.
- If the textbook is dry, transform flat process descriptions into detective-style or diagnosis-style stories.

**Good production pattern:**  
"A farmer notices crops dying in one part of the field. Segment A: observe the leaves. Segment B: compare sunlight and water exposure. Segment C: infer what process is failing."

**Good Boss pattern:**  
"Draw the cell, label the organelles, then explain why the mitochondria are important in 4-5 sentences."

**Bad production pattern:**  
"Write definitions of mitosis, meiosis, and DNA from memory."

### G10-11

- Target **20+ terms**, especially at molecular and systems levels.
- Require full causal-chain reasoning from **molecular -> cellular -> organism -> population** where relevant.
- **Notebook Capture** should appear every session or nearly every session for molecular diagrams, genetic crosses, and population graphs.
- Why Chain can go to **4 levels** when the content demands it.
- Story Mode may use discovery history, modern research, medical case framing, or ecological dilemmas.
- If the textbook is dry, frame the lesson as a research scenario, diagnostic puzzle, or evidence-based prediction task.

**Good production pattern:**  
"Your lab just sequenced a gene linked to drought resistance. What changes at the molecular level, and how might that affect the plant in the field?"

**Good Boss pattern:**  
"Predict what happens if the apex predator disappears from the ecosystem. Trace the change through 3+ trophic levels and justify your answer."

**Bad production pattern:**  
"List all the stages of protein synthesis with no diagram or explanation."

## 3b. Phase 1 Memory Sprint — Format Mixing + Construction (Biology)

**Mixing Rule:** Every Phase 1 Memory Sprint MUST use **at least 2 of 3 formats**: Multiple Choice, True/False, Yes/No/Not Given. Phase 1 is warm-up from the CURRENT chapter only.

### Multiple Choice — Biology Construction
- **Stem:** Ask about organism names, body part labels, category membership, or process identification.
- **Distractors:** Use real biological terms from the same taxonomic level or system. Never use obviously wrong filler.
- **G5-6 Example:**
  > O'simlikning qaysi qismi suv shimadi?
  > A) Barg  B) Poya  C) Ildiz ✓  D) Gul
  > [Bloom: L1 | PISA: L1]
- **G7-9 Example:**
  > Fotosintez qaysi organoidda sodir bo'ladi?
  > A) Mitoxondriya  B) Xloroplast ✓  C) Ribosoma  D) Yadro
  > [Bloom: L1 | PISA: L1]
- **G10-11 Example:**
  > DNK replikatsiyasida qaysi ferment ikki zanjirni ajratadi?
  > A) DNK polimeraz  B) Gelikaz ✓  C) Ligaz  D) Primaz
  > [Bloom: L1 | PISA: L1]

### True/False — Biology Construction
- **Statement:** A factual claim about the chapter. False versions should reflect real student misconceptions.
- **G5-6 Example:**
  > "Baliqlar o'pkasi bilan nafas oladi." → Noto'g'ri (jabralar bilan)
  > [Bloom: L1 | PISA: L1]
- **G7-9 Example:**
  > "Mitoxondriya — hujayraning energiya stantsiyasi." → To'g'ri
  > [Bloom: L1 | PISA: L1]
- **G10-11 Example:**
  > "mRNK yadrodan sitoplazma ribosomalariga o'tadi." → To'g'ri
  > [Bloom: L1 | PISA: L1]

### Yes/No/Not Given — Biology Construction
- **Source text:** A 1-2 sentence excerpt about the chapter topic. **Claim** tests whether it's confirmed, denied, or absent.
- **G7 Example:**
  > Ma'lumot: "Hujayra membranasi tanlangan o'tkazuvchanlikka ega — ba'zi moddalarni o'tkazadi, ba'zilarini to'sadi."
  > Da'vo: "Hujayra membranasi barcha moddalarni erkin o'tkazadi." → Yo'q
  > [Bloom: L2 | PISA: L2]
- **G10 Example:**
  > Ma'lumot: "Genetik kod universal — barcha tirik organizmlarda bir xil kodonlar bir xil aminokislotalarni kodlaydi."
  > Da'vo: "Genetik kod faqat hayvonlarda ishlaydi." → Yo'q
  > [Bloom: L2 | PISA: L2]

**Sequencing:** 2-3 MC (organism/structure recall) → 2-3 T/F (classification distinctions) → 1-2 YNNG (comprehension). Total: 6-8 items. Timer: 3-5 minutes.

## 4. Game Mechanics — Biology Specific

Biology inherits Tabiy Fanlar OBS mechanics, but the subject filter is even sharper because terminology and diagrams are the main bottlenecks.

### Always Primary

#### **Spaced Flashcards**
- This is mandatory **every session** because terminology is the main memory bottleneck in Biology.
- Best when each term is paired with a visual, not a text block.
- **Good Example (G5):** "Front: [photo of leaf] / Back: Barg — O'simlikning fotosintez organi."
- **Bad Example:** "Front: Define chloroplast / Back: [text paragraph]."

#### **Notebook Capture**
- Hand-drawn diagrams are non-negotiable in Biology.
- Students should draw, label, and organize biological structures by hand.
- **Good Example:** "Draw the human heart. Label all 4 chambers and major vessels."
- **Bad Example:** "Write the names of the heart chambers."

#### **Tile Match**
- Use for classification sorting and structure grouping.
- **Good Example:** "Match each animal to its class: Ko'rshapalak -> Sutemizuvchi, Toshbaqa -> Sudralib yuruvchi."
- **Bad Example:** "Match the word `cell` to its definition."

#### **Why Chain**
- Biology needs deep causal reasoning more than short recall.
- **Good Example:** "Why does a cactus have thick stems?" -> "What does it store?" -> "Why is water storage critical in deserts?"
- **Bad Example:** "What is a cactus?"

#### **Memory Palace**
- Excellent for taxonomy hierarchies, anatomy layouts, and sensory systems.
- **Good Example:** "Place the 5 senses at Registan Square locations. Eyes at the minaret..."
- **Bad Example:** "List the 5 senses."

#### **Story Mode**
- Must be observation-first and discovery-based.
- **Good Example:** "Marine biologists notice coral bleaching near Muynak. Observe -> Hypothesize -> Test."
- **Bad Example:** "Coral reefs have these 5 characteristics. Memorize them."

### Secondary Mechanics

| Mechanic | Best Use |
|---|---|
| **Adaptive Quiz** | Taxonomy drill, concept reinforcement, term-feature checks |
| **Speed Match** | Species <-> feature, organ <-> function, structure <-> label |
| **Link System** | Photosynthesis steps, digestion, food chains, life cycles |
| **Peer Teaching** | G8+: explain a system or mechanism to a classmate |

### Interactive Picks

| Game | Best Use |
|---|---|
| **Puzzle Lock** | Process ordering, life cycles, sequence reconstruction |
| **Reaction Chain** | Food chains, trophic cascades, developmental sequences |
| **Memory Match Blitz** | Organism <-> classification, organ <-> function |
| **Tic Tac Toe vs AI** | Fast taxonomy recall or organ-function recall |

### Phase 3 Game Construction Recipes — Biology

These recipes specify HOW to build game items for Biology's primary mechanics. Biology slot priority: Spaced Flashcards (every session) + Tile Match (classification) + Reaction Chain (processes) + Memory Palace (taxonomy).

#### Spaced Flashcards — Biology Recipe
- **Card count:** 8-12 cards per session (matching the chapter's key terms).
- **Front:** A visual — photo, diagram snippet, or labeled illustration of the organism/structure.
- **Back:** Uzbek term + 1-sentence definition + context from the chapter.
- **G5-6 Example:**
  > Front: [photo of leaf cross-section]
  > Back: "Barg — o'simlikning fotosintez organi. Yorug'lik energiyasini kimyoviy energiyaga aylantiradi."
- **G9-11 Example:**
  > Front: [diagram of ribosome on mRNA]
  > Back: "Ribosoma — oqsil sintezi sodir bo'ladigan organoid. mRNK ma'lumotini aminokislotalar ketma-ketligiga tarjima qiladi."
- **Quality rule:** Every card MUST have a visual on the front. Text-only fronts are banned in Biology. If the textbook lacks a diagram, describe the visual the platform should render.

#### Tile Match — Biology Recipe
- **Pair count:** 6-8 pairs.
- **Left tiles:** Organism names, structure names, or process names.
- **Right tiles:** Classification category, function description, or diagram label.
- **Timer:** 2:00 (G5-6), 2:30 (G7-8), 3:00 (G9-11).
- **G5-6 Example (Classification sorting):**
  > Ko'rshapalak ↔ Sutemizuvchi
  > Toshbaqa ↔ Sudralib yuruvchi
  > Qizilbaliq ↔ Baliq
  > Qurbaqa ↔ Amfibiya
  > Burgut ↔ Qush
  > Kapalak ↔ Hasharot
- **G7-9 Example (Organoid functions):**
  > Mitoxondriya ↔ Energiya ishlab chiqarish
  > Xloroplast ↔ Fotosintez
  > Ribosoma ↔ Oqsil sintezi
  > Lizosoma ↔ Hazm qilish
  > Yadro ↔ Genetik ma'lumot saqlash
  > Endoplazmatik to'r ↔ Moddalar tashish
- **Quality rule:** All pairs from CURRENT chapter. Each tile has exactly one correct match.

#### Reaction Chain — Biology Recipe
- **Node count:** 6-8 nodes (G5-8), 8-10 nodes (G9-11).
- **Chain topic:** A sequential biological process — photosynthesis, digestion, blood circulation, cell division, food chain.
- **Each node** = one step. Gate question (MC or T/F) guards progression.
- **G7 Example (Photosynthesis chain):**
  > Node 1: "Yorug'lik barg yuzasiga tushadi"
  > Gate: "Qaysi pigment yorug'likni yutadi?" [MC: Xlorofill ✓]
  > Node 2: "Xloroplastda yorug'lik reaksiyasi boshlanadi"
  > Gate: "Yorug'lik reaksiyasida suv parchalanadi" [T/F: To'g'ri ✓]
  > Node 3: "Suv parchalanib O₂ ajraladi"
  > Gate: "Ajralgan O₂ qayerga chiqadi?" [MC: Atmosferaga ✓]
  > Node 4: "Qorong'u reaksiyada CO₂ o'zlashtiriladi"
  > ... (continue to 6-8 nodes)
- **G10-11 Example (DNA replication):** Helikaz → zanjir ajralishi → primer qo'shilishi → DNK polimeraz → yangi zanjir sintezi → ligaz → tekshirish
- **Quality rule:** Node order must match the textbook's actual process sequence. Gate questions test understanding of that step, not unrelated facts.

#### Memory Palace — Biology Recipe
- **Location count:** 5-7 locations (mapped to Uzbek landmarks or familiar school rooms).
- **Each location** holds one taxonomy category, organ, or organism group.
- **Anchor logic:** The spatial association must be meaningful — visual or functional.
- **G5-6 Example (5 Senses at Registan):**
  > Registan minorasi — Ko'z (balandga qaraydi, ko'rish)
  > Sherdor madrasasi eshigi — Quloq (eshikni taqillatganda ovoz)
  > Tillakori hovlisi — Burun (gullar hidi)
  > Suv hovuzi — Til (suv ta'mi)
  > Marmar pol — Teri (sovuq pol sezgisi)
- **G9-11 Example (Taxonomy levels):**
  > Toshkent aeroporti (kirish) — Domen (eng keng kirish nuqtasi)
  > Pasport tekshiruvi — Dunyolik (birinchi filtr)
  > Terminal — Tip (katta guruh)
  > Gate — Sinf → Turkum → Oila → Tur (tobora torayib boradi)
- **Quality rule:** Locations must be concrete places the student can visualize. Abstract "rooms" don't work. Use Uzbek landmarks, school rooms, or home layout.

## 5. Boss Format

Biology uses the **OBS branch Boss**: explain-the-phenomenon, supported by diagram evidence.

- Diagram submission through **Notebook Capture is preferred**, not merely allowed.
- **G5-6:** label a diagram and explain the role of one structure in 2-3 sentences.
- **G7-8:** explain a process, include a diagram, and justify a key condition in the process.
- **G9-11:** predict outcomes in systems, mechanisms, food webs, genetics, or ecological change.
- **HP, stars, and hints:** inherit universal rules.
- **Failure framing:** use TEFCAS language, e.g. **"Hali emas!"**

| Grade Band | Boss Shape | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **G5-6** | Diagram labeling + short causal answer | "Label this plant diagram and explain what the root does in 2 sentences." |
| **G7-8** | Multi-part explanation with diagram | "Explain photosynthesis. Draw the diagram. Why is sunlight essential?" |
| **G9-11** | Full reasoning + evidence-based prediction | "Predict what happens to a food web if the apex predator disappears. Trace the cascade through 3+ trophic levels." |

**Good Boss design:** requires reasoning plus visual evidence.

**Bad Boss design:** asks only for recall, e.g. "What are the parts of a cell?"

## 6. Story Mode & Real-Life

### Story Mode

Biology Story Mode should always start with an **observation**.

- Pattern: **Scientists noticed... -> classify -> hypothesize -> explain**
- The first segment should show or describe something observable.
- The second should push the student to classify or compare.
- The third should explain the mechanism or system.

**Good Story Mode:**  
"Scientists noticed fish decline in the Aral Sea region. What changed in the water? Which organisms were affected first? What cascade follows?"

**Bad Story Mode:**  
"List 5 types of ecosystems and memorize them."

### Real-Life Challenge

Students should take on authentic Biology roles:
- **Botanika mutaxassisi**
- **Ekologiya direktori**
- **Genetik tahlilchi**
- **Tibbiyot tadqiqotchisi**

Use Uzbek contexts where natural:
- Aral Sea
- Tashkent Botanical Garden
- local flora and fauna
- cotton genetics
- Zarafshan river ecosystem

**Good G7 example:**  
"You are an ecologist studying fish decline in the Aral Sea region. Examine the data, identify 3 possible causes, and recommend an intervention."

**Good G10 example:**  
"You are a genetics analyst reviewing a crop-resistance report. Explain what trait changed and predict the impact on yield."

**Bad example:**  
"List 5 types of ecosystems."

**Production rule:** Real-Life Challenge must always give the student a role, a biological system, and a reason to explain evidence.

- Homework must stay tied to the chapter the student studied in class.

## Phase 7 — Reflection Construction Guide (Biology)

Biology Phase 7 closes with an **observation + discovery recap** — the student reflects on what they observed, classified, and explained.

### 7.1 Session Summary (2-3 sentences)
Summarize using observation → classification → explanation flow.

**Template:** "Bugun Siz [organism/structure/system] ni kuzatdingiz, [classification task] ni bajardingiz, va [causal mechanism] ni tushuntirdingiz."

**Examples:**
- **G5-6:** "Bugun Siz o'simlik qismlarini kuzatdingiz, ildiz va bargning vazifalarini ajratdingiz, va fotosintezda yorug'lik rolini tushuntirdingiz."
- **G7-9:** "Bugun Siz hujayra organoidlarini ko'rib chiqdingiz, hayvon va o'simlik hujayralarini solishtiringiz, va mitoxondriyaning energiya ishlab chiqarishdagi rolini tushuntirdingiz."
- **G10-11:** "Bugun Siz DNK replikatsiya jarayonini kuzatdingiz, fermentlarni bosqichlar bo'yicha tasniflashni o'rgandingiz, va mutatsiyaning oqsil sinteziga ta'sirini tahlil qildingiz."

### 7.2 Metacognitive Prompts (exactly 3)
Select 3 from these Biology-specific options (adapt to chapter):

1. "Bugun kuzatgan organizmingiz/tizimingiz haqida eng hayratlanarli narsa nima edi?"
2. "Qaysi biologik tushunchani endi boshqalarga tushuntira olasiz?"
3. "Agar tabiatda shu hodisani ko'rsangiz, endi nimaga e'tibor bergan bo'lar edingiz?"
4. "Bugungi mavzuni qaysi boshqa biologik tizim bilan bog'lay olasiz?"
5. "Qaysi diagrammani chizish eng qiyin bo'ldi va nima uchun?"
6. "Agar tajriba o'tkazsangiz, qaysi o'zgaruvchini birinchi tekshirgan bo'lar edingiz?"

### 7.3 BOST Goal Recall (MANDATORY)

**Format:** "Bugun Siz «[stored goal from Phase 0-A]» ni bilmoqchi edingiz. Bildingizmi?"

**Example:** "Bugun Siz «yurak qanday ishlashini» bilmoqchi edingiz. Bildingizmi?"

Student responds with brief self-assessment. No scoring.

### 7.4 Spaced Repetition Schedule

**Format:**
- "1 kundan keyin: [2-3 key organism names/structure labels] tezkor takrorlash"
- "3 kundan keyin: [1-2 biological processes or classification rules] qisqa viktorina"
- "7 kundan keyin: [chapter topic] umumiy takrorlash"

**Example (G7 Cell Biology):**
- "1 kundan keyin: mitoxondriya, xloroplast, ribosoma — tezkor takrorlash"
- "3 kundan keyin: hayvon va o'simlik hujayralari farqi — qisqa viktorina"
- "7 kundan keyin: 'Hujayra tuzilishi' bobi — umumiy takrorlash"

### 7.5 Closing Line

- **≥60% — Third Renaissance line (observation/discovery theme):**
  - "Abu Ali ibn Sino tibbiyotni kuzatishdan boshlagan. Siz bugun xuddi shunday qildingiz."
  - "Har bir buyuk biolog tabiatni sinchiklab kuzatishdan boshlagan — Siz bugun birinchi qadamni qo'ydingiz."
  - "Beruniy tirik dunyoni o'rganishni hech to'xtatmagan. Siz ham to'xtamang."
- **<60% — Encouraging neutral:**
  - "Bugun muhim kuzatishlar qildingiz. Keyingi mashg'ulotda yanada chuqurroq tushunamiz."
  - "Biologiya sabr talab qiladi — har bir takrorlash bilimni mustahkamlaydi."
  - "Eng muhimi — Siz harakat qildingiz. Davom eting!"

**Never** use failure language, score shaming, or informal "sen."
