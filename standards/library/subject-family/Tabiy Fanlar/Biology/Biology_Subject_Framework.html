1. What Biology Teaches

Biology is an observation-first, classification-heavy, terminology-dense, visually dependent subject.

  • Observation-First: Students learn to see, classify, and then explain natural systems.
  • Terminology Load: Highest of any science (15-30 new terms per chapter).
  • Visual Mastery: Diagrams are first-class representations. Drawing and labeling are mandatory.
  • Causal Chains: Focus on photosynthesis, digestion, inheritance, and ecological cascades.
  • Transformation: Turn flat textbook labels into discovery-based discovery: observation -> classification -> explanation.

Core Rule: Observation comes before explanation; diagram comes before paragraph.

3. Content Production Rules by Grade Band

G5-6 (Foundation)

  • 10-15 new terms per session.
  • Term requirement: diagram + UZ definition + example.
  • Start with concrete observation (photo or specimen).
  • Notebook Capture required every 2 sessions for hand-drawn diagrams.
Good Pattern: "Observe these leaf shapes from Tashkent Botanical Garden. Identify what makes them different."

G7-9 (Development)

  • 15-20 new terms per session.
  • Constant use of system-level diagrams (cells, food webs).
  • Discovery narratives: observe -> hypothesize -> test.
Good Pattern: "A farmer's crops are failing. Observe leaves, compare sunlight, and infer the failing process."

G10-11 (Advanced)

  • 20+ terms, focusing on molecular and systems levels.
  • Require full causal chains: molecular -> cellular -> population.
  • Frame lessons as research scenarios or diagnostic puzzles.
Good Pattern: "Your lab sequenced a drought-resistance gene. Explain the change at the molecular level."
4. Game Mechanics — Biology Specific

Primary Mechanics

  • Spaced Flashcards: Mandatory every session (visual-focused).
  • Notebook Capture: Non-negotiable hand-drawn diagrams.
  • Tile Match: Classification sorting and structure grouping.
  • Why Chain: Deep causal reasoning (up to 4 levels in G11).
  • Memory Palace: Taxonomy hierarchies and anatomy layouts.

Interactive Picks

Note: Skip Word Ladder, Listening, and Speed Match (math-focused).

5. Boss Format

OBS Branch: Explain-the-phenomenon + visual evidence.

  • G5-6: Labeling + 2-3 sentence explanation.
  • G7-8: Multi-part explanation with required diagram.
  • G9-11: Prediction in mechanisms or food webs.
  • Notebook Capture: Preferred for all Boss diagram submissions.
  • Failure: Use TEFCAS framing ("Hali emas!").
Good Boss (G9-11): "Predict what happens to a food web if the apex predator disappears. Trace cascade through 3+ levels."
6. Story Mode & Real-Life Challenge

Story Mode

Must be observation-first. Pattern: Notice -> Classify -> Hypothesize -> Explain.

Good Example: "Marine biologists notice bleaching near Muynak. Observe, hypothesize, and test the cascade."

Real-Life Challenge

  • Botanika mutaxassisi: Help at Tashkent Botanical Garden.
  • Ekologiya direktori: Manage Aral Sea fish decline.
  • Genetik tahlilchi: Review crop-resistance reports.
  • Tibbiyot tadqiqotchisi: Diagnosis-style scenarios.
Technical Rules
  1. Inheritance: Tabiy Fanlar family rules + OBS filter.
  2. Observation Priority: Observation before explanation.
  3. Diagram Priority: Diagram before long text.
  4. Terminology: Spaced Flashcards are mandatory every session.
  5. National Pride: Uzbekistan-based ecological contexts (Aral Sea, Zarafshan river).
