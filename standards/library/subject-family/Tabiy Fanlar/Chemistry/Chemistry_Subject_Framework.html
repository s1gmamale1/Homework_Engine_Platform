1. What Chemistry Teaches

Chemistry is the subject where atoms meet reactions. It explains matter at the molecular level and shows why substances change when they interact.

  • Dual-mode subject: classification (elements, compounds, groups, periods, reaction types) plus calculation (moles, mass, concentration, yield, enthalpy).
  • QNT heritage: inherits CPA progression, Worked Examples, and Error Analysis.
  • Symbolic notation: formulas, equations, electron structures, and molecular diagrams are the language of the subject.
  • Core skill: balancing equations is the backbone and appears in every chapter from G7 onward.
  • Transformation: textbooks often present reactions as finished facts; NETS must reveal bonding, energy, particles, and conservation.

Core Rule: Reactions are not facts to memorize. They are phenomena to understand.

3. Content Production Rules by Grade Band

G7-8

  • 10-15 new terms per session.
  • Every concept follows CPA: concrete reaction -> pictorial model -> abstract equation.
  • Equation balancing every session.
  • Notebook Capture for Lewis structures and molecular sketches.
  • Error Analysis every 2 sessions minimum.
Good Pattern: "A kitchen fire flares when water hits hot oil. Build the chemistry step by step."
Bad Pattern: "Memorize the names of the first 20 elements."

G9-10

  • 15-20 terms including stoichiometric vocabulary.
  • Unit tracking on all stoichiometry tasks.
  • Lab reasoning: predict gases, products, and ion/electron behavior.
  • Error Analysis should appear every session.
Good Pattern: "You add acid to a metal sample. Predict the gas, write the equation, and explain why."
Bad Pattern: "Write the definition of molarity and solve 10 substitutions."

G10-11

  • Full multi-step stoichiometry from first principles.
  • Organic chemistry requires structural formulas and functional-group reasoning.
  • Thermochemistry must include energy interpretation, not just arithmetic.
  • Frame dry chapters as pharmaceutical, industrial, or environmental chemistry scenarios.
Good Pattern: "Your lab is testing a synthesis route. Draw the structure, calculate yield, explain loss."
Bad Pattern: "List all functional groups from the chapter."
4. Game Mechanics — Chemistry Specific

Primary Mechanics

  • Tile Match: Element-symbol, compound-formula, reactant-product matching. Periodic table fluency builder.
  • Worked Examples: Stoichiometry scaffolding with completion effect.
  • Error Analysis: Core for balancing, formula writing, mole conversion, and unit handling.
  • Notebook Capture: Lewis structures, balanced equations, apparatus sketches.
  • Speed Match: Fast periodic table and classification recall.
  • CPA Progression: Mandatory for every new reaction or symbolic structure.

Phase 5 Buzan Options

Peg System: Group numbers and families. Radiant Summary: Periodic hierarchy and reaction types. Link System: Reaction mechanisms. Memory Palace: Periodic table zones and lab stations.

Note: Skip Word Ladder, Listening/Dictation, Creative Lab, and usually Memory Match Blitz.

5. Boss Format

QNT Branch: Show-the-derivation + balance + explain.

  • Notebook Capture: Required every Boss for molecular diagrams, equations, and steps.
  • G7-8: Balance, identify, draw, explain reaction type.
  • G9-10: Balance, calculate, justify, interpret a lab scenario.
  • G11: Combine calculation, diagram, and feasibility reasoning.
  • Failure: Use TEFCAS framing ("Hali emas!").
Good Boss (G11): "Use Hess's law to calculate heat of reaction. Draw the energy diagram and explain feasibility."
Bad Boss: "What is the periodic table?"
6. Story Mode & Real-Life

Story Mode

Must combine discovery narrative + CPA: visible reaction -> particle model -> notation or calculation.

Good Example: "Mendeleev notices patterns in the elements. Observe, organize, then predict an unknown element."
Bad Example: "The periodic table has 118 elements. Here they are."

Real-Life Challenge

  • Sifat nazorati tahlilchi: abnormal pH in food production.
  • Sintez kimyogar: product synthesis and yield.
  • Farmatsevtika tadqiqotchisi: aspirin or medicine production scenarios.
  • Laboratoriya rahbari: reaction control and safety reasoning.

Uzbek contexts: Navoi Mining, Fergana oil refinery, Bukhara gas processing, water treatment, cotton fiber chemistry.
