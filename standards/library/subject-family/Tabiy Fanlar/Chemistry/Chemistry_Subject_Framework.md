## 1. What Chemistry Teaches

Chemistry is the subject where **atoms meet reactions**. It explains matter at the molecular level and shows why substances change when they interact.

- Chemistry is **dual-mode**:
  - **classification**: elements, compounds, groups, periods, reaction types
  - **calculation**: moles, mass relationships, concentration, yield, enthalpy
- It belongs to the **QNT branch**, so it inherits:
  - **CPA progression**
  - **Worked Examples**
  - **Error Analysis**
- But Chemistry is not just Physics with chemicals. It has its own signature demands:
  - symbolic notation is heavier
  - balancing equations is the core skill
  - periodic table fluency is permanent, not chapter-local
  - molecular structure drawing matters more than vector diagrams
  - lab reasoning and safety are part of the concept, not optional extras
- Chemical notation is the subject’s language. Students must read and write:
  - element symbols
  - formulas
  - reaction equations
  - electron structures
  - molecular or Lewis diagrams
- The agent’s job is to **transform equation-heavy textbook pages into molecular understanding**. If the textbook gives a reaction as a finished fact, NETS must show why it happens through particles, bonding, energy, and conservation.

**Chemistry is NOT about:**
- memorizing the periodic table as an unstructured list
- plugging numbers into mole formulas without understanding the reaction
- balancing equations mechanically with no conservation logic
- treating formulas as detached from atoms, bonds, and energy

**Core production rule:** Reactions are not facts to memorize. They are **phenomena to understand**.

## 1b. Phase 0-A Panel Construction Guide — Chemistry

Chemistry is QNT branch: panels open with an **observable reaction or phenomenon** — something fizzes, changes color, heats up, or smells. The student should feel like a lab scientist noticing something unexpected.

## 3. Content Production Rules by Grade Band

### G7-8

- Target **10-15 new terms** per session.
- Every new concept must pass through **CPA**:
  - **Concrete** -> lab demo, household reaction, or observable change
  - **Pictorial** -> molecular model, particle diagram, periodic table pattern
  - **Abstract** -> chemical formula or balanced equation
- **Equation balancing must appear every session** from G7 onward.
- Use **Notebook Capture** for molecular structures, Lewis dot diagrams, and reaction sketches.
- **Error Analysis** should appear at least every **2 sessions** and often more.
- Periodic table navigation should be active, not passive: students must locate, compare, and infer from it.
- If the textbook is dry, make it alive: use chemical events, lab surprises, and everyday reactions instead of flat lists of reaction types.

**Good production pattern:**  
"A kitchen fire flares when someone throws water on hot oil. What reaction conditions matter here? What should happen instead? Build the chemistry step by step."

**Good Boss pattern:**  
"Balance the reaction, identify the product type, and draw a simple molecular model of the product."

**Bad production pattern:**  
"Memorize the names of the first 20 elements."

### G9-10

- Target **15-20 terms** including stoichiometric and solution vocabulary.
- Use unit tracking on every stoichiometry task, similar to dimensional reasoning in Physics.
- Build lab scenario reasoning into normal practice:
  - predict gas released
  - identify likely product
  - explain what ion or electron move matters
- **Error Analysis should appear every session** in this band because balancing, mole conversion, and unit logic all break easily.
- Why Chain should reach **3-4 levels** for reaction reasoning and periodic trends.
- If the textbook is dry, turn flat reaction tables into lab investigation or process-control stories.

**Good production pattern:**  
"You add acid to a metal sample. Predict the gas produced, write the balanced equation, and explain how you know."

**Good Boss pattern:**  
"Calculate how much carbon dioxide forms from burning 10 g of propane. Show the balanced equation, mole conversion, and mass result."

**Bad production pattern:**  
"Write the definition of molarity and solve 10 similar formula substitutions."

### G10-11

- Require full multi-step stoichiometry from first principles.
- Organic chemistry must include structural formulas, functional groups, and reaction logic, not just names.
- Thermochemistry must include energy interpretation, not just arithmetic.
- Error Analysis should include **5+ step debugging** on major sessions.
- Boss tasks should combine:
  - balanced equation or mechanism
  - calculation
  - structural or energy diagram
  - real-world interpretation
- If the textbook is dry, make it alive: use pharmaceutical research, industrial chemistry, environmental treatment, and materials analysis scenarios.

**Good production pattern:**  
"Your lab is testing a synthesis route. Draw the structural formula, calculate theoretical yield, and explain why actual yield may be lower."

**Good Boss pattern:**  
"Given enthalpy values, calculate the heat of reaction using Hess's law. Draw an energy diagram and explain whether the reaction is favorable at room temperature."

**Bad production pattern:**  
"List all functional groups from the chapter."

## 3b. Phase 1 Memory Sprint — Format Mixing + Construction (Chemistry)

**Mixing Rule:** Every Phase 1 Memory Sprint MUST use **at least 2 of 3 formats**: Multiple Choice, True/False, Yes/No/Not Given. Phase 1 is warm-up from the CURRENT chapter only. Focus on element↔symbol and reaction type recall.

### Multiple Choice — Chemistry Construction
- **Stem:** Ask about element symbols, reaction types, compound formulas, or periodic table positions.
- **Distractors:** Use real chemical symbols/terms from the same group or period. Common confusions: Na/N, Cl/C, ionic/covalent.
- **G7-8 Example:**
  > Natriy elementi belgisi qaysi?
  > A) N  B) Na ✓  C) Ni  D) Ne
  > [Bloom: L1 | PISA: L1]
- **G9-10 Example:**
  > Quyidagi reaksiya qaysi turga kiradi: 2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O?
  > A) Parchalanish  B) Birikish ✓  C) Almashinish  D) O'rin olish
  > [Bloom: L1 | PISA: L1]
- **G11 Example:**
  > Gess qonuniga ko'ra, reaksiya issiqlik effekti nimaga bog'liq emas?
  > A) Boshlang'ich moddalarga  B) Oxirgi mahsulotlarga  C) Oraliq bosqichlarga ✓  D) Bosimga
  > [Bloom: L2 | PISA: L2]

### True/False — Chemistry Construction
- **Statement:** A factual claim about elements, reactions, or chemical properties. False versions must reflect real student errors.
- **G7-8 Example:**
  > "Natriy — 2-guruh elementi." → Noto'g'ri (1-guruh)
  > [Bloom: L1 | PISA: L1]
- **G9-10 Example:**
  > "Kislota va ishqor reaksiyasida suv hosil bo'ladi." → To'g'ri
  > [Bloom: L1 | PISA: L1]
- **G11 Example:**
  > "Ekzotermik reaksiyada tizim issiqlik yutadi." → Noto'g'ri (issiqlik ajratadi)
  > [Bloom: L1 | PISA: L1]

### Yes/No/Not Given — Chemistry Construction
- **Source text:** A 1-2 sentence chemistry statement or lab observation. **Claim** tests whether it's confirmed, denied, or absent.
- **G8 Example:**
  > Ma'lumot: "Magniy yonganda oq rangli oksid hosil bo'ladi: 2Mg + O₂ → 2MgO."
  > Da'vo: "Bu reaksiyada gaz ajraladi." → Ma'lumot berilmagan (reaksiyada gaz ishtirok etadi, lekin ajralmaydi)
  > [Bloom: L2 | PISA: L2]
- **G10 Example:**
  > Ma'lumot: "Etanol yonganda CO₂ va H₂O hosil bo'ladi. Reaksiya ekzotermik."
  > Da'vo: "Reaksiya natijasida energiya ajraladi." → Ha (ekzotermik = energiya ajratadi)
  > [Bloom: L2 | PISA: L2]

**Sequencing:** 2-3 MC (element/symbol/reaction type recall) → 2-3 T/F (chemical property verification) → 1-2 YNNG (reaction interpretation). Total: 6-8 items. Timer: 3-5 minutes.

## 4. Game Mechanics — Chemistry Specific

Chemistry inherits QNT mechanics from Tabiy Fanlar and Aniq Fanlar, but it applies them through symbols, structures, reactions, and periodic patterns.

### Always Primary

#### **Tile Match**
- This is the main periodic-table and notation fluency builder.
- Use it for element <-> symbol, compound <-> formula, reactant <-> product, group <-> property.
- **Good Example:** "Match: `Fe -> Temir`, `Na -> Natriy`, `Cl -> Xlor`."
- **Bad Example:** "Match `Chemistry` to its definition."

#### **Worked Examples with Completion**
- This is the main scaffolding mechanic for stoichiometry and equation-based reasoning.
- **Good Example:** "Step 1: Write balanced equation. Step 2: Convert 10 g Na to moles: `10 ÷ ___ = ___ mol`."
- **Bad Example:** "Calculate the moles in 10 g of Na." with no scaffolding.

#### **Error Analysis**
- This is primary because chemistry errors are often hidden in balancing, formula writing, mole conversion, and unit handling.
- **Good Example:** "A student balanced: `2H2 + O2 -> H2O`. What's wrong? Fix it."
- **Bad Example:** "Is this balanced? Yes/No."

#### **Notebook Capture**
- Use it for molecular structures, Lewis diagrams, balanced equations, and apparatus sketches.
- **Good Example:** "Draw the Lewis dot structure of H2O. Show all bonds and lone pairs."
- **Bad Example:** "Write the formula for water."
- **Frequency:** every **2 sessions minimum**. Boss: **every session**.

#### **Speed Match**
- Use it for fast periodic-table and classification fluency.
- **Good Example:** "`Fe -> ? (Temir)`, `Group 1 -> ? (alkali metals)`, `pH < 7 -> ? (acid)`."
- **Bad Example:** "Define neutralization."

#### **CPA Progression**
- Mandatory for every new reaction, pattern, or symbolic structure.
- **Good Example:** vinegar + baking soda fizzing -> particle-collision / gas-release diagram -> balanced equation.
- **Bad Example:** "`CH3COOH + NaHCO3 -> ...` memorize this."

### Secondary Mechanics

- **Adaptive Quiz** — formula/reaction drill after concept teaching
- **Why Chain** — 3-4 levels for reactivity, electron movement, periodic trends
- **Memory Palace** — periodic table sections, lab equipment, reaction families
- **Peer Teaching** — G9+: explain reaction logic or mechanism to another student

### Interactive Picks

| Game | Best Use |
|---|---|
| **Codebreaker** | Element and compound decoding from symbols |
| **Reaction Chain** | Reactants -> intermediates -> products |
| **Connect Four vs AI** | Multi-step stoichiometry planning and decision sequence |
| **Puzzle Lock** | Lab procedure ordering, synthesis sequence, reaction steps |

### Phase 3 Game Construction Recipes — Chemistry

Chemistry slot priority: Tile Match PRIMARY (element↔symbol, compound↔formula, reactant↔product) + Worked Examples (stoichiometry steps) + Error Analysis (balancing errors) + Notebook Capture (Lewis structures, apparatus).

#### Tile Match — Chemistry Recipe (PRIMARY)
- **Pair count:** 6-8 pairs.
- **Left tiles:** Element names, compound names, or reactant names.
- **Right tiles:** Chemical symbols, formulas, or products.
- **Timer:** 2:30 (G7-8), 3:00 (G9-11).
- **G7-8 Example (Element ↔ Symbol):**
  > Temir ↔ Fe
  > Natriy ↔ Na
  > Xlor ↔ Cl
  > Oltingugurt ↔ S
  > Kaliy ↔ K
  > Kalsiy ↔ Ca
- **G9-10 Example (Compound ↔ Formula):**
  > Suv ↔ H₂O
  > Osh tuzi ↔ NaCl
  > Sulfat kislota ↔ H₂SO₄
  > Natriy gidroksid ↔ NaOH
  > Uglerod (IV) oksid ↔ CO₂
  > Kalsiy karbonat ↔ CaCO₃
- **G11 Example (Reactant ↔ Product):**
  > CH₄ + 2O₂ ↔ CO₂ + 2H₂O
  > NaOH + HCl ↔ NaCl + H₂O
  > Zn + H₂SO₄ ↔ ZnSO₄ + H₂↑
  > 2Fe + 3Cl₂ ↔ 2FeCl₃
  > CaCO₃ → (t°) ↔ CaO + CO₂↑
  > 2KMnO₄ → (t°) ↔ K₂MnO₄ + MnO₂ + O₂↑
- **Quality rule:** All pairs from CURRENT chapter. For element chapters, use symbol matching. For reaction chapters, use reactant↔product or type↔example matching.

#### Worked Examples (Stoichiometry Steps) — Chemistry Recipe
- **Step count:** 3-5 steps (G7-8), 5-8 steps (G9-11).
- **Format:** Show a calculation with 1-3 blanks at critical steps.
- **G8 Example (Simple mass calculation):**
  > 1-qadam: Tenglamani yozing: 2Na + Cl₂ → 2NaCl
  > 2-qadam: Na molyar massasi: ___ g/mol (23)
  > 3-qadam: 46 g Na necha mol? 46 ÷ 23 = ___ mol
  > 4-qadam: Necha mol NaCl hosil bo'ladi? ___ mol (2, chunki nisbat 1:1)
  > [Bloom: L3 | PISA: L2]
- **G10 Example (Yield calculation):**
  > 1-qadam: Tenglamani muvozanatlang: C₃H₈ + ___ O₂ → 3CO₂ + 4H₂O
  > 2-qadam: 44 g propan = ___ mol (44÷44 = 1 mol)
  > 3-qadam: Nazariy CO₂ massasi: 3 × ___ = ___ g
  > 4-qadam: Amaliy hosil: 110 g. Unumdorlik: (110/___) × 100% = ___%
  > [Bloom: L3 | PISA: L3]
- **Quality rule:** Blanks must target stoichiometric reasoning — mole ratios, mass conversions, or balance coefficients. Not filler.

#### Error Analysis (Balancing Errors) — Chemistry Recipe
- **Format:** Present a student's balanced (or unbalanced) equation or calculation with exactly ONE error. Student must: (1) find it, (2) explain the rule violated, (3) fix it.
- **G7-8 Example:**
  > O'quvchi yechimi:
  > H₂ + O₂ → H₂O (muvozanatlangan deb yozilgan)
  > Savol: Bu tenglama muvozanatlanganmi? Agar yo'q, xatoni toping va tuzating.
  > Javob: Muvozanatlanmagan. Chapda 2H va 2O, o'ngda 2H va 1O. To'g'ri: 2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O
  > [Bloom: L4 | PISA: L3]
- **G10-11 Example:**
  > O'quvchi hisob-kitobi:
  > 1-qadam: CaCO₃ → CaO + CO₂ ✓
  > 2-qadam: 100 g CaCO₃ = 1 mol ✓
  > 3-qadam: 1 mol CaO massasi = 40 g ✗ ← XATO (CaO = 56 g/mol: Ca=40 + O=16)
  > 4-qadam: Javob: 40 g CaO hosil bo'ladi ✗
  > [Bloom: L4 | PISA: L4]
- **Quality rule:** Error must be a REAL common chemistry mistake — unbalanced equation, wrong molar mass, incorrect mole ratio, missing coefficient. Not a typo.

#### Notebook Capture — Chemistry Recipe
- **Prompt:** "Qo'lingiz bilan chizing va belgilang: [specific chemical diagram]."
- **Required labels:** Minimum 4 (G7-8), 6+ (G9-11).
- **Diagram types by topic:**
  - Atom tuzilishi: Protonlar, neytronlar, elektronlar, energetik darajalar
  - Lewis strukturasi: Bog'lar, yolg'iz juftlar, umumiy elektronlar
  - Laboratoriya asboblari: Kolba, quritgich naycha, spirt lampa, shtativ
  - Energiya diagrammasi: Reaktantlar, mahsulotlar, aktivatsiya energiyasi, ΔH
- **G7-8 Example:**
  > "Natriy atomining tuzilishini chizing. Yadroda protonlar va neytronlar sonini, har bir energetik darajadagi elektronlar sonini ko'rsating."
- **G10-11 Example:**
  > "Etanol molekulasining Lewis strukturasini chizing. Barcha C-H, C-O, va O-H bog'larini va kisloroddagi yolg'iz juftlarni ko'rsating."
- **Quality rule:** Every Notebook Capture must specify WHAT to draw AND what to label. List required bonds, particles, or apparatus components explicitly.

## 5. Boss Format

Chemistry uses the **QNT Boss archetype**: **show-the-derivation + balance + explain**.

- **Notebook Capture is required in every Boss session** for molecular diagrams, balanced equations, and calculation steps.
- **G7-8:** balance, identify, draw, explain the reaction type.
- **G9-10:** balance, calculate, justify, and interpret a lab scenario.
- **G11:** combine calculation, diagram, and feasibility or outcome reasoning.
- **HP, stars, hints, and damage rules** inherit unchanged from the parent specs.
- **Failure framing:** use TEFCAS language, e.g. **"Hali emas!"**

| Grade Band | Boss Shape | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **G7-8** | Balance + identify + draw | "Balance this reaction. Draw the molecular model of the product. Explain what type of reaction this is." |
| **G9-10** | Stoichiometry derivation + lab reasoning | "Calculate the mass of CO2 produced from burning 10 g of propane. Show the balanced equation and all steps." |
| **G11** | Full reasoning + calculation + real-world application | "Given enthalpy values, calculate the heat of reaction using Hess's law. Draw an energy diagram and explain whether the reaction is feasible at room temperature." |

**Good Boss design:** requires balancing, calculation, and chemical reasoning grounded in matter behavior.

**Bad Boss design:**  
"What is the periodic table?"

## 6. Story Mode & Real-Life

### Story Mode

Chemistry Story Mode should combine **discovery narrative + CPA**.

- Segment A should show the visible reaction, pattern, or material problem.
- Segment B should turn it into a particle, bonding, or periodic model.
- Segment C should express it through notation, balancing, or calculation.

**Good Story Mode (G8):**  
"Mendeleev noticed patterns in element properties. Segment A: observe the pattern. Segment B: build the table. Segment C: predict the properties of an unknown element."

**Bad Story Mode:**  
"The periodic table has 118 elements. Here they are."

### Real-Life Challenge

In Chemistry, the student should become the chemist, analyst, or lab specialist.

**Good G7 example:**  
"You are a `Sifat nazorati tahlilchi` at a food factory. A batch of juice has abnormal pH. Test it, identify the acid-base issue, and recommend a fix."

**Good G10 example:**  
"You are a `Farmatsevtika tadqiqotchisi` at a Tashkent lab. Synthesize aspirin, calculate the yield, and explain why actual yield differs from theoretical."

**Bad example:**  
"Balance these 10 equations."

### Wise Status Roles

- **Laboratoriya rahbari**
- **Sintez kimyogar**
- **Sifat nazorati tahlilchi**
- **Farmatsevtika tadqiqotchisi**

### Uzbek Context Anchors

- Navoi Mining chemistry

- cotton fiber chemistry

**Production rule:** Real-Life Challenge must always assign a role, a material system, and a reason the chemistry matters.

## 7. Curriculum Touchpoints

- This framework is **textbook-only**.
- Chemistry textbooks are often equation-heavy but weak on molecular intuition. The agent must add the **why does this atom bond / react / transfer energy** layer without changing the chapter target.
- The periodic table should be treated as a **tool the student navigates**, not a wall poster they memorize passively.
- Transform rule:
  - textbook: "`NaOH + HCl -> NaCl + H2O`, memorize this"
  - NETS: "What happens when a strong base meets a strong acid? Why does the mixture heat up? What does the balanced reaction reveal?"
- Homework must stay tied to the chapter the student studied in class.
