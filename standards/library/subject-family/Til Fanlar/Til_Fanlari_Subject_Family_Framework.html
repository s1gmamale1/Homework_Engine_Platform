1. What Is This Family

"Til" translates to tongue or language. This family is defined by communication through structured language acquisition. Unlike Aniq Fanlar, language knowledge is conventional: it depends on agreed-upon patterns, usage, and meaning in context rather than derivation from fixed laws.

What it teaches: Comprehension and production through reading, listening, writing, and speaking.

What it focuses on:

  • Vocabulary acquisition and long-term retention.
  • Grammar in context.
  • Reading fluency and textual inference.
  • Listening comprehension.
  • Writing and speaking production.
  • Narrative interpretation and genre awareness.

What it is NOT about: Derivation, formal proofs, calculations, formula manipulation, or spatial reasoning.

Cognitive Demand Profile: Vocabulary (1), Grammar (2), Reading (3), Listening (4), Writing (5), Narrative (6), Register (7).

3. What This Family Focuses On
  • Comprehensible Input (Krashen's i+1): Deliver language slightly above current level.
  • Spaced Repetition: Rhythm: 1, 3, 7, 21, 60 days.
  • Grammar in Context: Introduced through narrative use, not isolated rules.
  • Dual Coding: Pair new words with image, sound, or native-language gloss.
  • Scaffolded Production: Move from guided to prompted to independent production.

Do NOT Focus On: Calculations, Formula manipulation, Proof logic, Spatial reasoning.

5. Boss Format for Til Fanlari
  • Format: Writing production evaluated by Tier 2 LLM.
  • G5: Guided production (sentence stems).
  • G6+: Prompted free production ("Write 3-5 sentences...").
  • G9+: Extended composition.
  • Literature: Textual analysis (character, theme, voice).
Good Boss: "Write 5 sentences describing your favorite place in Uzbekistan. Use 3 adjectives."
Bad Boss: "What is the definition of an adjective?"
6. Narrative Adaptation

Story Mode (Phase 2)

Structure: 3 segments with comprehension gates.

Example: Malika reads a letter (A), plans response (B), writes response (C).

Real-Life Challenge (Phase 4)

Role: Student is the expert: translator, presenter, editor, or guide.

Example: "You are a translator at the UN Tashkent office. Translate a diplomat's speech summary."
