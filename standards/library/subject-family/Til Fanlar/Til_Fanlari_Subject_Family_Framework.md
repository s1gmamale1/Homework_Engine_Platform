## 1. What Is This Family

"Til" translates to tongue or language. This family is defined by communication through structured language acquisition. Unlike Aniq Fanlar, language knowledge is conventional: it depends on agreed-upon patterns, usage, and meaning in context rather than derivation from fixed laws.

**What it teaches:** Comprehension and production through reading, listening, writing, and speaking.

**What it focuses on:** 
- Vocabulary acquisition and long-term retention.
- Grammar in context.
- Reading fluency and textual inference.
- Listening comprehension.
- Writing and speaking production.
- Narrative interpretation and genre awareness.

**What it is NOT about:** Derivation, formal proofs, calculations, formula manipulation, or spatial reasoning. Those belong to Aniq Fanlar.

**Cognitive Demand Profile (Priority Order):**
1. Vocabulary Acquisition & Retention
2. Grammar Application in Context
3. Reading Comprehension & Inference
4. Listening Comprehension
5. Writing Production
6. Narrative Interpretation
7. Register & Genre Control

## 3. What This Family Focuses On (The Content Filter)

Content producers must prioritize these cognitive anchors:

- **Comprehensible Input (Krashen's i+1):** Deliver language slightly above the student's current level so meaning is accessible while growth is still required (see [[UNIFIED-Buzan §19]]).
- **Spaced Repetition:** New vocabulary enters the review cycle immediately. Standard review rhythm: 1, 3, 7, 21, 60 days (see [[NETS-Library-Framework.md]] v0.6 Family 2).
- **Grammar in Context:** Grammar is introduced through sentences, dialogue, and narrative use, not isolated rule recitation.
- **Dual Coding for Vocabulary:** Pair new words with image, sound, or native-language gloss so meaning is anchored through multiple channels.
- **Scaffolded Production:** Writing and speaking move from guided production to prompted response to independent composition.
- **Bidirectional Thinking:** Every session must work BOTH directions — text → grammar rule extraction (read a passage, identify the pattern) AND grammar rule → production (know the rule, write correctly). A student who knows "past tense adds -di" but can't spot it in a real text hasn't learned.
- **Error Detection is PRIMARY:** Not occasional — minimum every 2 sessions. Students must find grammar/spelling/punctuation errors in text, explain WHERE and WHY, and fix them. Proofreading is the language equivalent of math debugging.
- **Answer Completeness:** Every response must use correct register, punctuation, and structure appropriate to the grade band. A bare sentence without punctuation is incomplete.

**Do NOT Focus On:**
- Calculations or arithmetic drills.
- Formula manipulation.
- Derivation or proof logic.
- Spatial reasoning tasks disguised as language work.

### Primary Mechanics (Priority Usage)

#### **Spaced Repetition Flashcards**
- **What it is:** Vocabulary or orthography recall through timed review cycles.
- **Why:** Long-term language retention depends on repeated retrieval, not one-time exposure.
- **Good Example:** Front: "convergence" / Back: "yaqinlashish" + image of rivers meeting.
- **Bad Example:** Front: "What is 2+2?" / Back: "4".

#### **Cloze / Sentence Fill**
- **What it is:** Missing-word or missing-ending completion inside a meaningful sentence ([[UNIFIED-Buzan §6]], [[UNIFIED-Buzan §19]]).
- **Why:** Grammar should be practiced where meaning and structure interact.
- **Good Example:** "Bola maktab___ ketdi. (ga/dan/da)" with word bank.
- **Bad Example:** "Fill in: The formula for area is ___".

#### **Listening Tasks**
- **What it is:** Audio stimulus followed by comprehension or detail questions.
- **Why:** Listening is its own skill and cannot be replaced by silent reading.
- **Good Example:** "Listen to the dialogue and answer: Where is Aziza going?"
- **Bad Example:** "Read this text silently and answer."

#### **Dialogue Exercises**
- **What it is:** Prompted spoken or written responses with a clear communicative goal.
- **Why:** Productive fluency grows through bounded exchanges, not open-ended chaos.
- **Good Example:** "Your friend invites you to hang out this weekend. Respond with: accept + suggest a time and place."
- **Bad Example:** Open sandbox chat with no structure.

#### **Dictation**
- **What it is:** Hearing language and reproducing it accurately in writing.
- **Why:** It links sound, spelling, punctuation, and syntax in one task.
- **Good Example:** "Listen and write: 'O'zbekiston — buyuk kelajak yurtidur'."
- **Bad Example:** "Copy this sentence from the board."

#### **Story Mode for Literature**
- **What it is:** Narrative delivery with comprehension gates between segments (see [[UNIFIED-Buzan §5.2]]).
- **Why:** Literature and language comprehension improve when grammar and meaning unfold through a real story arc.
- **Good Example:** "Alisher Navoi yosh bola edi... [story unfolds with comprehension gates]."
- **Bad Example:** "Read pages 45-50 and answer questions."

### Phase 0-A Panel Construction Guide

Phase 0-A is an 8-panel visual story that previews the session theme. For Til Fanlari, panels serve dual duty: vocabulary preview AND cultural/communicative context setting. The student should finish Phase 0-A knowing WHAT the session is about and WHY the language content matters.

**Panel length rules:**
- Panels 1-5: 8-10 sentences each (narrative core)
- Panels 6-8: 5-10 sentences each (consolidation and transition)

**Construction recipe for each panel:**

| Panel | Purpose | Language-Family Pattern | Construction Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1 — Hook** | Open with a vivid communicative situation | A character faces a language challenge: writing a letter, reading a sign, decoding a message, giving a speech | Lead with action, not definition. "Malika stared at the letter — half the words were unfamiliar." NOT "Today we learn new vocabulary." |
| **2 — Context** | Show why this language skill matters in real life | Connect the chapter topic to a real Uzbek or global communication scenario | Ground the abstract grammar/vocab topic in a concrete need: a job interview, a school announcement, a travel situation |
| **3 — Key Vocabulary Preview** | Introduce 4-6 key terms from the chapter | Each term appears in a meaningful sentence, not a glossary list. Bold the target word. Provide L1 gloss for foreign languages | "The ambassador spoke with **eloquence** — har bir so'zi aniq va ta'sirli edi." NOT "eloquence = notiqlik" |
| **4 — Grammar/Structure Spotlight** | Show the grammar pattern or text structure in action | One clear example sentence, then a second sentence with the SAME pattern but different content. Highlight the pattern visually | Show the pattern working, not the rule name. "Bola maktab**ga** ketdi. Ota ish**ga** bordi." — the student sees the suffix pattern before being told its name |
| **5 — Challenge Preview** | Foreshadow what the student will DO in the session | Briefly describe the Real-Life role or Boss task shape without revealing exact content | "Bugun Siz muharrir bo'lasiz — maqoladagi xatolarni topasiz." This primes motivation |
| **6 — Connection** | Link today's topic to something the student already knows | Reference a prior chapter concept, a familiar word pattern, or a known grammar rule | "Eslatma: o'tgan darsda Siz kelishiklarni o'rgandingiz. Bugun ularni gap ichida ishlatamiz." |
| **7 — Cultural Anchor** | National Pride or cultural context moment | A quote, historical fact, or literary reference connected to the language topic. Draw from `quotes_database.json` or `facts` | "Alisher Navoiy 15 yoshida she'r yozgan — til kuchi yoshlikdan boshlanadi." |
| **8 — BOST Goal** | Student sets their session goal | Direct address: "Bugun Siz nimani bilmoqchisiz?" Student types or selects a goal. Store for Phase 7 recall | Always use formal "Siz". Goal must connect to the chapter topic. The system stores this response for Phase 7 BOST recall |

**Family-level rules for all language panels:**
- Foreign languages (English, Russian for UZ-stream): bilingual scaffolding allowed in panels 1-3 for G5-6, then taper to target-language-only by G8+
- Native language (Ona tili, Russian for RU-stream): all panels in the target language from G5
- Every panel must contain at least ONE sentence with a chapter keyword bolded
- Panel 3 vocabulary items MUST reappear in Phase 1 Memory Sprint — this is the spaced repetition entry point

### Phase 1 Format Mixing + Construction

Phase 1 Memory Sprint for Til Fanlari **MUST use at least 2 of the 3 allowed formats** in every session:

| Format | What It Tests | Language-Family Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Multiple Choice** | Recognition, vocabulary recall, grammar pattern ID | "Which suffix makes this word dative? (a) -ga (b) -da (c) -dan (d) -ni" |
| **True/False** | Rule confirmation, reading comprehension | "The sentence 'Bola maktabga ketdi' uses the dative case. True or False?" |
| **Yes/No/Not Given** | Inference, textual evidence checking | "Based on the passage, the author supports bilingual education. Yes / No / Not Given" |

**Construction recipe per format:**

**Multiple Choice (MC) — Language Pattern:**
1. Write the **stem** as a complete sentence or short passage with one element highlighted or blanked
2. Write 1 correct answer that demonstrates the target grammar rule, vocabulary meaning, or reading comprehension skill
3. Write 3 distractors: (a) one that is a common student error for this topic, (b) one that uses a related but wrong grammar form, (c) one that is clearly wrong but uses vocabulary from the chapter
4. Tag: `[Bloom: L1-L2 | PISA: L1-L2]` for recall items; `[Bloom: L3 | PISA: L2]` for application items

**Example MC (grammar ID):**
> "Bola maktab___ ketdi." — Bu gapda qaysi kelishik ishlatilgan?
> A) Jo'nalish (-ga) ✓  B) O'rin (-da)  C) Chiqish (-dan)  D) Tushum (-ni)
> `[Bloom: L1 | PISA: L1]`

**True/False (T/F) — Language Pattern:**
1. Write a **statement** about a grammar rule, vocabulary meaning, or reading passage claim
2. The statement must be unambiguously true OR false — no trick wording
3. If false, the student should be able to explain WHY after answering (this primes Phase 2 deeper thinking)
4. Tag: `[Bloom: L1-L2 | PISA: L1]`

**Example T/F (spelling rule):**
> "Tutuq belgisi (') faqat o'zlashma so'zlarda ishlatiladi." — True or False?
> Answer: True
> `[Bloom: L1 | PISA: L1]`

**Yes/No/Not Given (YNNG) — Language Pattern:**
1. Provide a **short passage** (3-5 sentences) from the chapter or adapted from it
2. Write a claim that requires checking against the passage text
3. "Yes" = passage explicitly supports it. "No" = passage explicitly contradicts it. "Not Given" = passage does not address it
4. Tag: `[Bloom: L2-L3 | PISA: L2]`

**Example YNNG (reading comprehension):**
> Passage: "O'zbekistonda 130 dan ortiq millat vakillari yashaydi. Ularning har biri o'z tilida gaplasha oladi."
> Claim: "O'zbekistonda faqat o'zbek tilida gaplashiladi."
> Answer: No
> `[Bloom: L2 | PISA: L2]`

**Mixing rule enforcement:** If a session uses ONLY MC for all 8-10 items, it fails validation. Minimum 2 formats per session, distributed across the item set.

### Phase 3 Game Construction Recipes

Phase 3 requires 3 games: at least 1 Interactive + at least 2 Default. Below are construction recipes for the primary Til Fanlari mechanics.

#### Sentence Fill / Cloze (Default — PRIMARY for all languages)

**What you build:** A text passage (3-8 sentences) with 3-6 strategically placed blanks. Each blank tests a specific grammar or vocabulary target.

**Construction steps:**
1. Select a passage from the chapter or write one using chapter vocabulary and grammar targets
2. Identify 3-6 positions where grammar forms, vocabulary items, or spelling patterns are tested
3. Remove those elements to create blanks
4. Build a word bank: correct answers + 2-4 distractors per blank (G2-7). For G8+, consider free recall (no word bank)
5. Tag each blank: `[Bloom: LX | PISA: LX]`

**Example (Uzbek grammar — dative suffix):**
> "Ota ish___ bordi. Bola maktab___ ketdi. Ona bozor___ yurdi."
> Word bank: `ga, da, dan, ni, ning` (3 correct: ga, ga, ga + 2 distractors)
> Correct fills highlight green, +100 XP each. First-attempt bonus: +25 per blank.

**Example (English vocabulary — G7):**
> "The scientist made an important ___. Her ___ changed the way we understand climate. The government ___ her research immediately."
> Word bank: `discovery, research, funded, ignored, invention, rejected`

**Example (Russian grammar — case endings):**
> "Мальчик пошёл в школ___. Учитель стоял у доск___. Книга лежала на стол___."
> Word bank: `у, е, и, ой, а` (correct: у, и, е + distractors)

#### Tile Match (Default — structure and pairing)

**What you build:** 6-8 pairs where left tiles match to right tiles. Timer: 2:00-3:00.

**Construction steps:**
1. Define the matching dimension: word↔definition, grammar rule↔example, L1↔L2, clause↔function
2. Write 6-8 pairs. Pairs 1-3 should be L1 (Bloom: Remember), pairs 4-5 L2 (Understand), pairs 6+ L3 (Apply)
3. Ensure no two right-side tiles could reasonably match the same left-side tile — ambiguity breaks the mechanic

**Example (Grammar rule ↔ example sentence):**
> Left: "Jo'nalish kelishigi" → Right: "Bola maktab**ga** ketdi"
> Left: "O'rin kelishigi" → Right: "Kitob stol**da** turibdi"
> Left: "Chiqish kelishigi" → Right: "U uy**dan** chiqdi"

**Example (English word ↔ definition, G6):**
> Left: "brave" → Right: "not afraid of danger"
> Left: "curious" → Right: "wanting to learn or know"
> Left: "ancient" → Right: "very old, from long ago"

#### Spaced Flashcards (Default — vocabulary retention)

**What you build:** 8-12 flashcard pairs for timed review. Front = prompt, Back = answer + reinforcement.

**Construction steps:**
1. Select 8-12 items from the chapter: new vocabulary, spelling exceptions, grammar patterns
2. Front side: the word, phrase, or grammar prompt
3. Back side: definition/translation + example sentence + image cue (where applicable)
4. Order by difficulty: first 4 cards = recall (seen in Phase 0-A), next 4 = recognition (new context), last 4 = application (use in sentence)

**Example (English vocabulary — G5):**
> Front: "library" | Back: "kutubxona — A place with many books. 'I go to the library every Saturday.'" + image of Navoi Library

**Example (Russian vocabulary — UZ-stream G6):**
> Front: "здание" | Back: "bino — A large building. 'Школа — это большое здание.'" + image of school building

#### Codebreaker (Interactive — spelling/morphology patterns)

**What you build:** A word or phrase that must be decoded by identifying spelling patterns, morphological components, or hidden rules.

**Construction steps:**
1. Choose a word with rich morphological structure (root + prefixes/suffixes)
2. Break it into components. Each component is a "code" the student must crack
3. Provide 3-4 clues that progressively reveal the structure
4. Final answer: the student assembles the complete word and explains the pattern

**Example (Uzbek morphology — G7):**
> Code: "_ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _" (10 letters)
> Clue 1: "Ildiz — 'o'qi' (read)" → o'qi
> Clue 2: "Bu qo'shimcha ishni bajaruvchi odamni bildiradi" → -tuvchi
> Answer: "o'qituvchi" (teacher) = o'qi + t + uvchi
> Student explains: root "o'qi" + causative "t" + agent suffix "uvchi"

**Example (English spelling — G6):**
> Code: "_ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _ _" (10 letters)
> Clue 1: "Opposite of 'happy'" → un + happy
> Clue 2: "Add a suffix meaning 'state of being'" → -ness
> Answer: "unhappiness" = un + happy + ness

### Phase 5 Buzan Alternatives
- **Memory Palace:** Place vocabulary clusters at Registan Square locations.
- **Link System:** Build story chains for idiomatic expressions and collocations.
- **Peg System:** Anchor irregular verb or conjugation patterns to stable cues.
- **Radiant Summary:** Use grammar-rule hierarchies and text-type maps.

### Games to AVOID
- **Speed Match:** Arithmetic focus; not natural for language-family targets.
- **Notebook Capture:** Better reserved for derivation or diagram-heavy families; Til Boss already captures writing production directly.
- **Error Analysis:** The math-style "find the bug in a solution" pattern is not the default fit here; proofreading belongs inside Cloze, editing, and writing tasks.
- **Connect Four vs AI:** Multi-step calculation planning is not a natural language-learning fit.

## 5. Boss Format for Til Fanlari

- **Core Format:** Writing production task evaluated by Tier 2 LLM against rubric.
- **G5:** Guided production with sentence stem or paragraph frame.
- **G6+:** Prompted free production such as "Write 3-5 sentences about..."
- **G9+:** Extended composition with structure requirements.
- **Literature Boss:** Textual analysis of passage, character, theme, or voice.
- **HP, stars, and hints:** inherit from [[UNIFIED-Buzan §5.6]].

**Good Boss Question:** "Write 5 sentences describing your favorite place in Uzbekistan. Use at least 3 adjectives and 1 comparison (e.g., '...dan kattaroq')."

**Bad Boss Question:** "What is the definition of an adjective?"

## 6. Story Mode & Real-Life Challenge — Narrative Adaptation

### Story Mode (Phase 2)
Story Mode must deliver comprehensible input through a true narrative arc, not textbook exposition (see [[UNIFIED-Buzan §5.2]]).

- **Role:** Story carries grammar, vocabulary, and comprehension inside meaningful context.
- **Structure:** 3 segments with comprehension gates between them.
- **Goal:** Exposure first, then comprehension check, then guided production.

**Good Example:** "Malika is writing a letter to her pen pal in Turkey. Segment A: she reads the incoming letter. Segment B: she plans her response. Segment C: she writes the response."

**Bad Example:** "Here are 10 grammar rules. Memorize them."

### Real-Life Challenge (Phase 4)
- **Role:** Student IS the language expert: translator, presenter, editor, guide, or correspondent.
- **Context:** Use authentic Uzbek or global communication scenarios, with the inherited national/global balance applied at the system layer.
- **Goal:** Transfer language skill into purposeful communication, not isolated sentence conversion.

**Good Example:** "You are a translator at the UN Tashkent office. A diplomat's speech needs translating. Read the speech, identify 3 key terms, and produce an accurate summary in Uzbek."

**Bad Example:** "Translate this sentence: The cat sat on the mat."

## Phase 7 Reflection — Language Family Pattern

Phase 7 closes the session with structured metacognition. For Til Fanlari, reflection centers on **language production awareness** — what the student can now DO with language that they could not do before.

### Construction Recipe

**Step 1 — Session Summary (2-3 sentences)**
Summarize what the student practiced. Reference the chapter topic, the grammar/vocabulary targets, and the communication skill exercised.

> **Example:** "Bugun Siz jo'nalish kelishigini gap ichida ishlatishni mashq qildingiz. Siz 8 ta yangi so'z o'rgandingiz va muharrir sifatida xatolarni topdingiz."

**Step 2 — 3 Metacognitive Prompts**
Ask the student to reflect on HOW they learned, not just WHAT. One prompt per type:

| Prompt Type | Purpose | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Strategy awareness** | What approach worked? | "Qaysi usul Sizga yangi so'zlarni eslab qolishda yordam berdi — rasm, gap ichida ishlatish, yoki takrorlash?" |
| **Difficulty reflection** | What was hardest and why? | "Qaysi topshiriq eng qiyin bo'ldi? Nima uchun?" |
| **Transfer thinking** | Where else can you use this? | "Bugun o'rgangan grammatik qoidangizni qayerda — maktubda, hikoyada, yoki suhbatda — ishlata olasiz?" |

**Step 3 — BOST Goal Recall**
Retrieve the goal the student set in Phase 0-A Panel 8. Present it back:

> **"Bugun Siz [stored goal] ni bilmoqchi edingiz. Bildingizmi?"**

The student answers Yes/No/Partially. This closes the motivation loop opened in Phase 0-A.

**Step 4 — Spaced Repetition Schedule**
Display the review schedule for items from this session:

> "Bugungi so'zlar va qoidalar quyidagi kunlarda qaytariladi: **ertaga (1-kun)**, **3 kundan keyin**, **1 haftadan keyin**, **3 haftadan keyin**, **2 oydan keyin**."

**Step 5 — Closing Line**
Score-dependent closing that reinforces motivation:

- **Score >= 60%:** Third Renaissance line connecting language mastery to Uzbekistan's future.
  > "Til bilgan — dunyo biladi. Siz Uchinchi Renessans avlodisiz — har bir o'rgangan so'zingiz kelajak eshigini ochadi."
- **Score < 60%:** Encouraging neutral — no shame, no false praise.
  > "Bugun qiyin bo'ldi, lekin har bir urinish — oldinga qadam. Ertaga yana mashq qilamiz. Siz albatta o'zlashtirasiz."

### Language-Specific Closing Variants
- **English sessions:** Closing may be bilingual (Uzbek frame + English key phrase) for G5-7, English-only for G8+
  > G6: "Great work today! Siz bugun 8 ta yangi inglizcha so'z o'rgandingiz."
  > G10: "Every word you master opens a door. Keep building."
- **Russian sessions (UZ-stream):** Uzbek frame + Russian key phrase
  > "Молодец! Bugun Siz rus tilida yangi gaplar tuza oldingiz."
- **Russian sessions (RU-stream):** Russian-only
  > "Вы сегодня разобрали сложные конструкции. Каждый урок делает Вас сильнее."
- **Ona tili sessions:** Full Uzbek
  > "Til — millatning ko'zgusi. Siz bugun ona tilingiz qoidalarini mustahkamladingiz."
