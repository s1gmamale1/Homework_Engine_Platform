## 1. What Jahon Tarixi Teaches

World History in NETS is the study of **global interactions, civilization clusters, and shared human progress**. It focuses on how discoveries, conflicts, and ideas in one part of the world influenced others, with a strict anchor to the Central Asian connection.

- **Factual Mastery (70%):** Students must memorize global milestones, dates, figures (e.g., Napoleon, Lincoln, Mao), and major eras.
- **Comparative Logic (30%):** Understanding similarities and differences between civilizations (e.g., Roman Republic vs. Athenian Democracy).
- **Global-National Link:** Every global era must be cross-referenced with what was happening in Uzbekistan at that time.
- **Exam Readiness:** Rapid recall of chronological sequences and figure-achievement pairs.

**What Jahon Tarixi is NOT about:**
- Deep historiographical theory or modern political commentary.
- Abstract "what if" scenarios without factual grounding.
- Purely Western-centric narratives (must maintain 55/45 balance).

**Core production rule:** Factual retention is the prerequisite for comparative analysis. No student should "compare" empires without knowing their dates and core characteristics.

## 3. Content Production Rules by Grade Band

### G7-9 (Development)

- **Terminology:** 10-15 new names/terms per session.
- **Chronology:** Use **Memory Palace** (The Louvre or British Museum digital layouts) to place global artifacts.
- **Error Detection:** Find errors in global maps or timelines (e.g., "The French Revolution happened in 1492. Fix it.").
- **Historical Projection:** "The Silk Road was the internet of the 14th century. Compare their impact on trade."

**Good production pattern:**  
"Step 1: Identify the year the steam engine was patented. Step 2: List three industries it changed. Step 3: Find what was happening in the Bukhara Emirate during the same decade."

**Good Boss pattern:**  
"Explain the 3 main causes of World War I. Give the start and end dates. Identify one major technological change from the war."

**Bad production pattern:**  
"You are a general at the Battle of Waterloo. Write a 500-word diary entry using modern military slang." (Too abstract/creative).

### G10-11 (Advanced)

- **Terminology:** 15-20+ academic terms (e.g., Globalization, Decolonization).
- **Complex Why Chain:** 3 levels (e.g., Invention of Printing -> Spread of Ideas -> Reformation).
- **Comparative Tasks:** Mandatory every session. Compare East vs. West or Era A vs. Era B.
- **Historical Projection:** "How does the 19th-century 'Great Game' resemble modern global energy competition?"

**Good production pattern:**  
"Analyze the Marshall Plan. Sequence the 5 steps of its implementation. Explain how it differed from post-WWII plans in the East."

**Good Boss pattern:**  
"Compare the Industrial Revolutions in Britain and Japan. List 3 similarities and 2 differences. Support your answer with specific dates and figures."

**Bad production pattern:**  
"List all countries in the UN in alphabetical order." (Busywork).

## 4. Game Mechanics — Jahon Tarixi Specific

### Always Primary (The "Big Three")

#### **Spaced Flashcards**
- Factual reinforcement of global figures and dates.
- **Good:** "Front: 1789 / Back: Bastiliyaning olinishi, Fransuz inqilobining boshlanishi."

#### **Memory Palace**
- Using global landmarks to anchor chronology.
- **Good:** "Place the eras of the Roman Empire along the Colosseum's tiers."

#### **Tile Match**
- Global Figure <-> Achievement or Ideology <-> Era.
- **Good:** "Match Leonardo da Vinci to The Last Supper."

### Secondary Mechanics
- **Speed Match:** Rapid identification of global flags, rulers, or terms.
- **Story Mode:** Narrative of an explorer, scientist, or global leader.
- **Puzzle Lock:** Reconstructing a global treaty or declaration from fragments.

### Games to Avoid
- **Blackjack:** Removed.
- **Word Ladder:** Linguistic only.
- **Connect Four:** Too strategy-heavy for factual history.

## 5. Boss Format: Comparative Recall

- **Structure:** Retrieval of Global Facts -> Comparative Reasoning -> Evidence Check.
- **HP Scaling:** G7-8: 100 | G9-11: 150.
- **Failure:** TEFCAS framing ("Hali emas!").

**Good Boss (G9):** "Explain why the Industrial Revolution began in Britain. List 3 geographical and 2 economic causes. Give the approximate dates. How did this change world trade?"
**Bad Boss:** "Draw a map of the world in 1945." (Visual task with no reasoning/recall).

## 6. Story Mode & Historical Projection

### Story Mode (Phase 2)
Focus on **Global Transitions**.
- **Good:** "You are on a ship with Magellan. The stars look different. The food is running out. Why are we doing this? Sequence the journey so far."

### Historical Projection (Phase 4)
- **Goal:** Global-National Synthesis.
- **Pattern:** "While the West was inventing X, our ancestors in Khorezm were mastering Y. How do these two paths connect today?"
