## 1. What Is This Family (The Philosophy)

Ijtimoiy Fanlari (Social Sciences) in NETS is defined by the **Memorize + Understand + Recall** model. Unlike Science or Math, where truth is observed or derived, social sciences in the national curriculum are 70% about building a robust mental database of facts (dates, names, terms, laws) and 30% about understanding systemic connections.

**The Recall-Ready Mandate:**
The primary goal of homework in this family is to build a robust knowledge base through rapid recall and understanding under pressure.
- **Memorize:** Hard facts (Who, When, Where, What).
- **Understand:** Systemic logic (Why it happened, Cause -> Effect).
- **Recall:** Performance under pressure (Phase 1 and Phase 3 drills).

**Transform Philosophy:** Textbooks are often dry lists of events or laws. NETS must transform them into **narrative experiences** (Story Mode) and **mnemonic systems** (Buzan) without diluting the factual density required for exams.

**What it is NOT about:** 
- Formula manipulation or arithmetic derivation.
- "Soft" creative expression without factual anchoring.
- Abstract modern problem-solving detached from the textbook era.

**Cognological Demand Profile:**
1. Factual Retention (Dates/Names/Terms)
2. Chronological Sequencing
3. Causal Modeling (Cause -> Effect)
4. Principle Application (Law/Economy)
5. Perspective-taking

## 3. The Content Filter (Universal Production Rules)

All subjects in this family must follow these three filters added in v0.6:

1. **Bidirectional Thinking:** Every event or principle must be tested both ways: **Event -> Cause** (Why did it happen?) and **Cause -> Prediction** (If this happens, what is the result?).
2. **Error Detection (Primary):** Mandatory every 2 sessions. Students must find the "bug" in a historical timeline, a legal argument, or an economic scenario (e.g., wrong date, wrong sequence, wrong article applied).
3. **Answer Completeness:** Claims must be supported by evidence. An answer is not "1238," but "1238, because that is when the event occurred."

## 4. Game Mechanics — Ijtimoiy Filter

### Always Primary (The "Big Three")

#### **Spaced Flashcards (THE CORE)**
- Mandatory every session. This is where dates, names, and terms live.
- **Good:** "Front: 1206 / Back: Temuchinning 'Chingizxon' unvonini olishi."
- **Bad:** "Front: Define history." (Too vague).

#### **Memory Palace (Chronology)**
- Use Registan Square or other landmarks to "place" events in chronological order.
- **Good:** "Place the 4 Timurid rulers at the minarets in order of their reign."
- **Bad:** "List the rulers."

#### **Tile Match (Pairing)**
- Connect Event <-> Date, Figure <-> Achievement, or Term <-> Definition.
- **Good:** "Match Article 1 to its Constitutional text."
- **Bad:** "Match the word to its translation." (Reserved for Languages).

### Secondary Mechanics
- **Speed Match:** Rapid-fire recall of terms/dates.
- **Story Mode:** Narrative wrap to make the era/law feel alive.
- **Why Chain:** Max 2-3 levels for historical or economic causality.

### Phase 5 Buzan Options
- **Peg System:** For memorizing lists of dates or articles.
- **Link System:** For sequential event chains.
- **Radiant Summary:** For classifying branches of law or types of economies.

### Games to AVOID
- **Blackjack / Gambling:** Removed for ethical compliance.
- **Bridge Builder / Minefield:** Too abstract for factual subjects.
- **Word Ladder:** Linguistic focus only.
- **Notebook Capture (Calculations):** Reserved for quantitative subjects.

**Dual-Catalog Rule:** Phase 3 requires ≥1 Interactive and ≥2 Default mechanics.

## 5. Boss Format: Recall + Explain

Ijtimoiy Bosses do not use abstract analysis; they test the ability to **retrieve and connect**.

- **Structure:** 1. Retrieve the fact (Who/When) -> 2. Explain the connection (Why/Result).
- **HP, damage, stars, and hints:** see [[UNIFIED-Buzan §5.6]].
- **Feedback:** Use TEFCAS **"Hali emas!"** (Not yet) framing.

**Good Boss Question:** "Identify the year the Constitution was adopted. List 3 key changes it introduced and explain how one affects your life today."
**Bad Boss Question:** "You are a diplomat at the UN. Write a speech about Uzbekistan's history." (Too abstract, lacks textbook anchoring).

---

## 6. Phase 4: Historical Projection

In this family, Phase 4 is **NOT a real-world application** scenario. It is **Historical Projection**.

- **Goal:** Connect the past/principle to today or to a "what if" scenario.
- **Pattern:** "This system Temur built is similar to modern X" or "What would have happened if X didn't occur?"
- **POV:** Student is a **Lead Analyst** or **Guardian of Heritage**.

**Good Example:** "Compare the Silk Road routes to the modern 'One Belt, One Road' initiative. What similarities do you see in the trade goods?"
**Bad Example:** "Act as a modern merchant and calculate your profit margins."
