## 1. What Is This Family

"Aniq" translates to precise, exact, and derivable. This family is defined by its reliance on formal reasoning, logical proofs, and quantitative accuracy. Unlike linguistic or social sciences, knowledge in this family is not "interpreted"—it is derived from first principles and laws.

**What it teaches:** Formal reasoning through definitions, logical derivations, and practical applications of exact laws.

**What it focuses on:** 
- Calculations and arithmetic fluency.
- Conceptual understanding of abstract laws.
- Formula manipulation and transformation.
- Multi-step derivation and proof sequences.
- Spatial and algorithmic thinking.
- Critical Thinking

**What it is NOT about:** Rote memorization of long texts, narrative interpretation, or free-form creative expression.

**Cognitive Demand Profile (Priority Order):**
1. Calculation & Execution
2. Concept Understanding
3. Formula Recall & Manipulation
4. Error Detection & Diagnosis
5. Spatial Reasoning
6. Algorithmic Thinking

## 3. What This Family Focuses On (The Content Filter)

Content producers must prioritize these cognitive anchors:

- **Concept BEFORE Procedure:** Ensure the student understands *why* a formula works before making them execute it 10 times.
- **CPA Progression is MANDATORY:** Every new concept must move through **Concrete → Pictorial → Abstract** stages (See [[UNIFIED-Buzan §5.2]]).
- **Worked Examples (Completion Effect):** Use partial solutions where students fill in the logical gaps.
- **Error Detection:** Present deliberately incorrect solutions for the student to diagnose and fix.
- **Dual Coding:** Every formula must be paired with a verbal/conceptual explanation.
- **Interleaved Practice:** Mix different problem types (e.g., area and perimeter) to prevent mindless pattern matching.

**Do NOT Focus On:**
- Free-form writing or essays.
- Subjective interpretation of stories.
- Opinion-based debates without derivation.

## 4. Best Methods & Games (Filtered from UNIFIED-Buzan)

### Primary Mechanics (Priority Usage)

#### **Tile Match (Formula Assembly)**
- **What it is:** Dragging symbolic components into correct structures ([[UNIFIED-Buzan §6]]).
- **Why:** Builds symbolic fluency without the friction of typing complex syntax.
- **Good Example:** Assemble the quadratic formula by dragging `-b`, `±`, `√`, and `2a` into their slots. Concept Matching. Definition Matching. Formula matching
- **Bad Example:** Match the word "Algebra" to its dictionary definition (L1 Recall).

#### **Notebook Capture (Handwritten Calculations)**
- **What it is:** Photo submission of handwritten work.
- **Why:** **Generation Effect**—writing math by hand improves retention by ~30% over typing.
- **Good Example:** "Solve this system of 3 equations on paper and upload the photo." Draw this graphic. Draw the outcome based on the formula. Decode the graphic and find the formula.
- **Bad Example:** "Write the definition of a triangle" (Notebook Capture is for derivation, not text).
- **Frequency:** G5-8 (1 in 3 sessions); G9-11 (1 in 2 sessions).

#### **Error Analysis (Metacognitive Fix)**
- **What it is:** Finding the "bug" in a pre-solved problem.
- **Why:** Forces students to think like a teacher/expert.
- **Good Example:** "A student calculated `2+3*5 = 25`. Find the error and state the correct rule." Give solved problems with easter eggs and have them detect it.
- **Bad Example:** "Is this answer correct? [Yes/No]" (Too binary).

#### **Worked Examples (Scaffolded Completion)**
- **What it is:** Step-by-step solutions with missing pieces.
- **Why:** Reduces cognitive load (Sweller’s Load Theory).
- **Good Example:** Step 1: `3x = 12`. Step 2: `x = 12 / __`. Step 3: `x = __`.
- **Bad Example:** Providing a blank box for a 5-step problem with no hints.

### Secondary Mechanics
- **Adaptive Quiz:** For skill-drilling once the concept is acquired.
- **Speed Match:** Arithmetic fluency (Multiplication tables, unit conversions).
- **Why Chain:** Socratic drilling. Cap at 2 levels for G5, 3 levels for G6+.

### Phase 5 Buzan Alternatives
- **Peg System:** Use Number-Shape associations for math rules.
- **Radiant Summary:** Hierarchical mind-maps for classifying number systems or shapes.
- **Link System:** For sequential processes (e.g., steps of long division).

### Interactive Catalog Picks (≥1 required per session)

| Game | Why It Fits Aniq Fanlar | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| **Connect Four vs AI** | Multi-step planning — think 2-3 moves ahead | G7+ procedural reasoning, algebra sequences |
| **Codebreaker** | Deductive reasoning + formula/constant recall | Periodic table, formula syntax, number patterns |

## 5. Boss Format for Aniq Fanlar

- **Core Format:** Open numerical or derivation-based problems.
- **No Multiple Choice (MC):** G6+ must be open-ended. G5 allows max 30% MC.
- **Partial Credit:** Rubric-based scoring allows points for correct logic despite arithmetic slips.
- **Submissions:** Notebook Capture is an accepted (and preferred) boss submission method.
- **HP Scaling:** G1-4: 50 | G5-8: 100 | G9-11: 150 ([[UNIFIED-Buzan §5.6]]).
- **Stars:** 3★ requires 1st attempt, no hints, and >80% HP remaining.
- **Hints:** Socratic hints cost -10 HP.

**Good Boss Question:** "A fountain with radius 3m has a path around it of width x. If the total area is 50m², find x to 2 decimal places. Show your working."

## 6. Narrative Adaptation

### Story Mode (Phase 2)
Must follow **CPA** across 3 narrative segments:
- **Segment A (Concrete):** Character handles physical objects (e.g., counting actual apples).
- **Segment B (Pictorial):** Character draws or maps the objects (e.g., bar models).
- **Segment C (Abstract):** Character applies the formula (e.g., `5x = 20`).

### Real-Life Challenge (Phase 4)
- **Role:** Student IS the expert (e.g., "You are the Lead Architect").
- **Context:** Must be Uzbek-specific (e.g., Samarkand planning, National Bank, Prezident's project and you are assigned to make it happen, You have a startup business growing in such industry you have done this but have problems).
- **Goal:** Transfer the textbook concept to a "messy" real-world scenario.

## 7. Subject Pattern Example (Standard Session)

- **Phase 1:** Recall of previous day's formula.
- **Phase 2:** Story: "Akmal is measuring the Registan courtyard..." (CPA flow).
- **Phase 3:** Tile Match (Formula assembly) + Adaptive Quiz.
- **Phase 4:** Real-Life: "Calculate the materials needed for a new school in Bukhara."
- **Phase 5:** Radiant Summary of the day’s rules.
- **Phase 6:** Multi-step open numerical Boss fight.
