## Section 1: What Is This Family

**"Aniq"** = precise, exact, derivable. These are the subjects where there is a right answer and a provable path to it.

Aniq Fanlar teaches **formal reasoning** — every concept is either (a) a definition/axiom, (b) a derivation from prior concepts, or (c) an application of a derived rule to solve a problem. Knowledge is cumulative and hierarchical: gaps in foundational knowledge cascade into failure at higher levels.

This family focuses on: calculations, concept understanding, formula manipulation, error detection, multi-step derivation, spatial reasoning, algorithmic thinking.

This family is **NOT about**: memorization of texts, free-form writing, narrative interpretation, opinion/debate, creative expression. Those belong to Til Fanlari, Ijtimoiy Fanlari, and Tarbiya/Sanat.

**Cognitive demand profile** (ranked):
1. Multi-step calculation and derivation
2. Concept understanding (why a formula works)
3. Formal notation fluency (equations, symbols, algorithmic syntax)
4. Error detection (where in a chain of reasoning a mistake occurred)
5. Spatial reasoning (geometry, force diagrams, molecular structures)
6. Algorithmic thinking (informatics)
7. Rule memorization for formula recall

## Section 3: What This Family Focuses On

**Do:**
- Concept understanding BEFORE procedure execution (never the reverse)
- Multi-step calculation and derivation
- CPA progression is **MANDATORY** (Concrete → Pictorial → Abstract) — see UNIFIED-Buzan §5.2
- Worked examples with completion effect (student fills gaps in solved examples)
- Error detection (deliberately wrong solutions for student to diagnose)
- Dual coding for every formula (symbolic + verbal explanation)
- Interleaved practice (mix old skills with new after acquisition)

**Do NOT focus on:**
- Free-form writing → Til Fanlari
- Narrative interpretation → Til Fanlari
- Observation/classification without derivation → Tabiy Fanlar
- Opinion/debate → Ijtimoiy Fanlar

## Section 4: Best Methods & Games — Filtered from UNIFIED-Buzan

**Phase 3 selection rule:** ≥1 Interactive Catalog (8 games), ≥2 Default Pool (16 mechanics). See UNIFIED-Buzan §5.3.

**Inherits National Pride rules** from Library Framework v0.5 Part 8 (55/45 ratio, 20% task injection, Gate Quotes, Wise Status, Third Renaissance — all uniform, not family-overridable).

### Primary Mechanics (use these first)

**Tile Match** (formula assembly)
- **Why:** students drag formula components together — builds symbolic fluency
- **Good:** `"Match each operation with its result: a² + 2ab + b² → (a+b)²"`
- **Bad:** `"Match the word 'algebra' with its definition"` ← vocabulary, not derivation. Use Sentence Fill.
- **Full spec:** UNIFIED-Buzan §6, Game Catalog

**Notebook Capture** (handwritten calculations)
- **Why:** Generation Effect — physical writing improves retention 25-40% over typing
- **Good:** `"Solve this system of equations by hand. Photograph your work."`
- **Bad:** `"Write the definition of a quadratic equation"` ← recall, not calculation. Notebook Capture is for worked solutions.
- **Frequency:** G5-8: 1 in 3 sessions. G9-11: 1 in 2 sessions.

**Error Analysis** (deliberately wrong examples)
- **Why:** builds meta-cognitive error detection — students learn to self-correct
- **Good:** `"This student solved 3x + 5 = 20 and got x = 4. Find the mistake and correct it."`
- **Bad:** `"Is this answer right or wrong?"` ← too binary. Must find WHERE and WHY.

**Worked Examples with Completion Effect**
- **Why:** reduces cognitive load (Sweller) — student sees full solution, then fills gaps
- **Good:** Step 1 shown → Step 2 shown → Step 3: `___` (student completes) → Step 4: `___`
- **Bad:** Fully blank problem with no scaffolding ← that's a quiz, not a worked example.

**CPA Progression** (Concrete → Pictorial → Abstract)
- **Why:** mandatory for Aniq Fanlar — every concept starts concrete, moves pictorial, ends abstract
- **Good** (fractions): Concrete = cutting pizza into slices → Pictorial = bar model diagram → Abstract = `¾ + ½ = ...`
- **Bad:** Starting with `"¾ + ½ = ?"` without concrete or pictorial setup ← violates CPA.

### Secondary Mechanics (use when content fits)

| Mechanic | When to Use | Caveat |
|---|---|---|
| Adaptive Quiz | Drill after concept introduction | IRT-calibrated to student's exact level |
| Speed Match | Rapid arithmetic fluency, formula recall | Multiplication tables, constant values |
| Why Chain | Understanding WHY a formula works | Cap: 2 levels G5, 3 levels G6+. Not for pure calculation. |
| Spaced Flashcards | Formula memorization, constant recall | Pair with Tile Match or Notebook Capture — flashcards alone = shallow recall |
| Memory Sprint | Phase 1 warm-up from PRIOR chapters only | Never test current-topic content in Phase 1 |
| Mystery Box | Cross-chapter retention — recognize WHICH tool to use | Best when students confuse methods (LCM vs GCD) |
| Real-Life Challenge | Transfer: apply formulas to real-world scenarios | **Must use first-person expert POV.** Student IS the engineer, accountant, analyst. |
| Peer Teaching | G5-11: explain a concept to a fictional classmate | Best G8+ where explanations are sophisticated |
| Connect Four vs AI | Multi-step procedures requiring planning ahead | G7+ where students think 2-3 steps ahead |
| Codebreaker | Deductive reasoning + recall (periodic table, formulas, syntax) | Best when "code" metaphor maps naturally |
| Reaction Chain | Process sequences: order of operations, algorithm flow | G5-G7 fluency under light pressure |
| Puzzle Lock | Spatial reasoning: geometry, diagrams | Higher render cost — skip on low-end devices |

## Section 5: Boss Format for Aniq Fanlar

**Don't redefine — reference:** Full Boss spec at UNIFIED-Buzan §5.6.

**Family-specific rules:**
- Open numerical/derivation problem. **No MC for G6+.** G5 allows up to 30% MC.
- Rubric-based partial credit: correct method with arithmetic error still earns points.
- Notebook Capture accepted for boss submissions.
- **HP:** G1-4: 50 | G5-8: 100 | G9-11: 150
- **Damage:** Easy -10 | Medium -20 | Hard -30. Distribution: 40/40/20.
- **Mastery stars:** 1★ = defeated | 2★ = ≤2 attempts, >50% HP | 3★ = first attempt, no hints, >80% HP
- **Hint cost:** -10 HP per hint. Premium (Tier 3): unlimited Socratic tutoring on failure.

**Good boss question:**
> "A rectangular garden is 12m × 8m. A path of width *x* meters is built around it. If the total area including the path is 180m², find *x*. Show your working."

**Bad boss question:**
> "What is the formula for area of a rectangle?" ← Bloom's L1 recall, not a boss-level assessment. Boss must be Apply or higher.

## Section 6: Story Mode & Real-Life Challenge — Narrative Adaptation

**Story Mode (Phase 2):**
- Must follow CPA across 3 segments
- **Good:** "Aziza is helping her father tile the bathroom floor. Segment A: she counts physical tiles (concrete). Segment B: she draws a grid on paper (pictorial). Segment C: she calculates 3.5m × 2.8m (abstract)."
- **Bad:** "The area formula is length × width. Here are 5 problems." ← no narrative, no CPA, not Story Mode.
- **Reference:** UNIFIED-Buzan §5.2

**Real-Life Challenge (Phase 4):**
- Student IS the expert (first-person POV mandatory)
- Must use Uzbek cultural context
- **Good:** "You are the Bosh Muhandis (Chief Engineer) at Tashkent City Planning. A new park needs a circular fountain with radius 4m surrounded by a walking path 1.5m wide. Calculate the path area and the amount of gravel needed at 3kg/m²."
- **Bad:** "Calculate the area of a circle with radius 4m." ← no scenario, no role, no context. Textbook problem, not Real-Life Challenge.
- **Reference:** UNIFIED-Buzan §5.4
