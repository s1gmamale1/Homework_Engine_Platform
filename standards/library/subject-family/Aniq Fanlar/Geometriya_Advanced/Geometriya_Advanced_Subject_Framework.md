## 1. Subject Identity

**Geometriya (Advanced) in Grades 10-11** is the culmination of spatial reasoning in the Aniq Fanlar family. While G7-9 Geometry established 2D shapes, theorems, and basic deductive proof, Advanced Geometry extends into **three-dimensional space, vector algebra, coordinate geometry, and complex proof construction**.

This subject sits at the intersection of two disciplines: it inherits the **visual and deductive nature** of G7-9 Geometry, but also demands **algebraic precision** through coordinate geometry and vectors. It is the most synthesis-intensive subject in the family.

**What Geometriya Advanced IS:**
- Solid geometry: prisms, pyramids, cylinders, cones, spheres — surface area, volume, cross-sections
- Three-dimensional spatial reasoning: visualizing 3D objects from 2D diagrams, projections, cross-sections
- Vectors: addition, scalar multiplication, dot product, cross product (introductory), applications to geometry
- Coordinate geometry (analytic geometry): lines, circles, conic sections (parabola, ellipse, hyperbola)
- Advanced plane geometry: circle theorems, power of a point
- Trigonometry in geometry: sine rule, cosine rule, area with trigonometry, applications in 3D
- Advanced proof: multi-step synthetic proofs, proofs by contradiction, combinatorial geometry

**What Geometriya Advanced is NOT:**
- A repeat of G7-9 Geometry — do not revisit basic 2D theorems without extension
- Differential geometry or topology — university scope
- Calculus applied to geometry — the overlap with Algebra va Analiz is minimal and bounded
- Engineering drawing or technical drafting — the subject is mathematical

**Core Cognitive Demand (Priority Order):**
1. 3D spatial visualization — mentally manipulating solid objects, reading projection diagrams
2. Vector reasoning — geometric relationships through algebraic vector operations
3. Advanced proof construction — multi-step synthetic and analytic proofs
4. Coordinate geometry — translating geometric configurations into algebraic equations
5. Trigonometric application — trig functions solving geometric problems in 3D
6. Cross-disciplinary synthesis — combining vectors, coordinates, and classical geometry in one problem

**Subject Color:** `#0F766E` (dark teal primary) · `#F59E0B` (amber accent)

**Critical distinction from G7-9 Geometry:** G7-9 asks "What is the shape, and what can we prove about it?" Advanced Geometry asks "How does this shape exist in space, how can it be described algebraically, and how does it interact with other objects?"

## 2. Audience Profile — Grade Band G10-11

| Dimension | G10 | G11 |
|---|---|---|
| **Age** | 15-16 | 16-17 |
| **3D spatial ability** | Developing; cross-sections are often the hardest concept | More confident with practice |
| **Vector fluency** | Vectors are new — algebraic interpretation of geometry unfamiliar | Vectors integrated as a natural geometric tool |
| **Working memory** | 6-7 chunks | 7 chunks |
| **Attention ceiling (gamified)** | 45-55 min | 45-60 min |
| **Key challenge** | Holding 3D solid in mind while computing its properties algebraically | Multi-step problems combining vectors, coordinates, and classical theorems |
| **Exam context** | DTM preparation — geometry and vectors both tested | DTM intensive — conics and vectors are high-yield |

**Key pedagogical implication:** The jump from 2D to 3D is the hardest conceptual jump in this subject. Students who excelled in G7-9 Geometry often struggle initially — 2D spatial intuition does not automatically transfer to 3D. Invest heavily in **interactive 3D diagrams, physical object analogies, and cross-section exploration** before any calculation.

## 4. Phase-by-Phase Tuning

### 4.1 Memory Sprint (Phase 1)

**Inherits:** UNIFIED §5.1. Duration ≤2 min (hard cap). Current chapter warm-up — key terms, prerequisite concepts, foundational facts needed for this session.

**Geometriya Advanced format priority:**

| Format | Priority | Geometriya Advanced Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Multiple Choice** | HIGH | "Bu kesim kubni diagonal bo'ylab kesganida qanday shakl hosil bo'ladi?" (4 options). 3D identification, solid recognition from cross-sections. Each item MUST include a diagram. |
| **True / False** | HIGH | "Silindrning yon yuzasi = 2πrh — To'g'ri yoki Noto'g'ri?" Formula verification, theorem checks. |
| **Yes / No / Not Given** | MEDIUM | "Bobda aytilganiga ko'ra, har qanday konus kesimi doira — Ha / Yo'q / Aytilmagan." Chapter comprehension warm-up. |

**Geometriya Advanced additions:**
- Include 1-2 **cross-section questions** — "If this cube is cut diagonally, what shape is the cross-section?" High DTM-yield, builds spatial reasoning
- Every Memory Sprint item must include its diagram
- Wrong answer feedback: show 3D diagram with error annotated: *"Lateral surface area to'g'ri, lekin asos yuzasini unutdingiz."*

### 4.2 Story Mode (Phase 2)

**Inherits:** UNIFIED §5.2. 4-beat arc mandatory. Stranger Test. IELTS-style checkpoints.

**Geometriya Advanced story arc — Spatial Engineering:**

| Arc Beat | Geometriya Advanced Version |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | A real 3D design or construction problem. "You are the lead engineer designing the roof of the new Navoi Opera House. The dome is a hemisphere on a cylindrical base. Given the dimensions, calculate the total outer shell material needed." |
| **Struggle** | The engineer tries 2D area formulas — they work for walls but fail for the curved dome. "2D tools cannot solve 3D curved surfaces." |
| **Discovery** | The hemisphere surface area derived by understanding it as a "peeled" 3D surface: S = 2πr². The physical object helps: the dome is half a full sphere. The formula connects to 3D geometry. |
| **Solution** | Total material = cylinder lateral area + hemisphere area. Calculated, interpreted in m², checked against real building scale. |

**Hard rules:**
- **3D DIAGRAM FIRST** — always open with the 3D figure before any calculation
- Student must **orient in 3D space** before the problem begins — label x, y, z axes; identify base, height, relevant edges
- CPA arc: Concrete (physical object or high-quality photograph) → Pictorial (labeled 3D diagram with projections) → Abstract (formula, vector, or coordinate equation)
- Checkpoint questions test **spatial understanding**: "Bu prizmaning kesim asosi qaysi yuz?" YES. "Hajmini hisobla." NO (Phase 3)
- Uzbek engineering contexts: Navoi Opera House dome, Tashkent metro vaulted stations, Bukhara water tower, UzAuto body panel geometry, Kamchik tunnel cross-section
- Grade narrative: **G10-11 — subject-specific professional narratives**
- National Pride: `setting_origin` tag per segment, 55/45 rolling balance

### 4.3 Game Breaks (Phase 3)

**Inherits:** UNIFIED §5.3. 3 games. Dual-Catalog Rule: ≥1 Interactive Catalog, ≥2 Default Pool.

**Geometriya Advanced game slot priority — most spatially intensive set in the family:**

| Slot | Role | Primary | Backup |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Slot 1** | 3D spatial warmup | **Puzzle Lock (Sliding Tile)** — reassemble a scrambled 3D diagram, reconstruct a cross-section, identify a solid from its three orthographic views | Mystery Box — identify a 3D solid from surface area or volume clues |
| **Slot 2 (Von Restorff Anchor)** | Vector + coordinate reasoning | **Tile Match** — vectors ↔ geometric representations, conic equations ↔ graphs, 3D solid ↔ its net | Reaction Chain — sequential vector calculation (find vector → find angle → find projection → find area) |
| **Slot 3** | Proof and construction | **Notebook Capture** — student draws 3D figure, labels, solves, uploads photo. Highest priority game for this subject. | Connect Four vs AI — gate each move with geometry or vector identification |

> **Von Restorff Anchor (Slot 2):** Preferentially selects items tagged `outstanding: true` — surprising 3D cross-sections, unexpected vector results, architectural photographs.

**Notebook Capture — defining mechanic for Geometriya Advanced:**
- G10-11: at least **1 in every 2 sessions** must include Notebook Capture
- Construction task example: *"Asosi 4cm bo'lgan to'g'ri to'rtburchak piramidasi, balandligi 6cm. Barcha qirralar uzunligini va to'la yuzasini toping. Diagrammada hisoblagan uzunliklarni ko'rsating."*
- Projection task: *"Bu L-shaklidagi prizmaning yuqori, old va yon ko'rinishlarini chizing."*
- Vector task: *"Berilgan komponentlar bo'yicha a⃗ va b⃗ vektorlarini chizing. Keyin a⃗ + b⃗ ni chizing va hosil bo'lgan uchburchakni belgilang."*

**Buzan Cortical Diversity:**
- Puzzle Lock = Visual/Spatial
- Tile Match = Visual/Spatial + Verbal/Logical
- Notebook Capture = Kinesthetic/Motor
≥2 modalities: Spatial + Kinesthetic.

**Games to AVOID for Geometriya Advanced:**
- Speed Sort with numbers — arithmetic, no spatial value
- Word Ladder — linguistic
- Codebreaker for number patterns — algebraic, not geometric
- Why Chain without a diagram — questions without diagrams have no place here
- Tic Tac Toe — too low-cognitive for G10-11

### 4.4 Real-Life Challenge (Phase 4)

**Inherits:** UNIFIED §5.4. First-person Expert POV mandatory.

**Expert roles for Geometriya Advanced:**
- Aerospace engineer calculating volume and surface area of a satellite component (composite solid)
- Architect designing the Alisher Navoi Opera Theatre interior with correct acoustic geometry (vectors, ellipse reflections)
- Robotics engineer at Tashkent Polytechnic programming 3D arm movement direction (3D vectors)
- Geologist analyzing rock crystal structures using cross-sectional diagrams
- Game developer at a Tashkent IT Park startup defining 3D bounding volumes for collision detection
- Civil engineer calculating concrete volume for a cable-stayed bridge pylon (composite 3D solids)
- Cartographer calculating the shortest flight path over Central Asia (spherical geometry, great circle arc)

**Buzan W5H Scaffold:**
- **G10:** Available via "Tahlil" button (G9+ toggleable per UNIFIED)
- **G11:** Available on request
- W5H applies for multi-factor 3D problems (Who = engineer, What = composite solid, Where = Tashkent, How = decompose into cylinders + hemispheres). Not forced on pure single-answer calculation.

**Decision Mapping:** Applied when scenario involves two design options (e.g., triangular vs rectangular pyramid roof — compare surface area vs structural strength). Student maps pros/cons, assigns weights, justifies. NOT applied to single-answer solid geometry or vector proof problems.

**Wise Status — National Pride (30% of Phase 4 tasks):**
1. **Status:** "Bosh Muhandis," "Kosmik Muhandis," "Geodezist"
2. **Arena (55/45):** National: Tashkent TV Tower geometry, Navoi Opera House dome, Afrosiyob aerodynamic design. Global: CERN detector geometry, NASA satellite design.
3. **Bridge:** The geometric proof or 3D calculation is UNTOUCHED.
4. **Closing:** National or global contribution line.

**Hard rules:**
- Problem must require **3D spatial setup** — identifying the solid, its dimensions, relevant faces
- Student must **draw or annotate the diagram** as part of the solution (Notebook Capture preferred)
- G11: vectors are the expected primary tool — "Use vectors to prove these two lines are perpendicular"
- Multi-step: at least 3 spatial/vector/coordinate steps before reaching the answer

### 4.5 Consolidation / Mnemonic Lock (Phase 5)

**Inherits:** UNIFIED §5.5. Technique by content structure. SMASHIN' SCOPE quality gate (6+/12).

**Technique selection for Geometriya Advanced:**

| Content Structure | Technique | Geometriya Advanced Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Spatial** (3D solid locations, theorem positions) | **Memory Palace** | Registan Square (default): each 3D solid placed at a hotspot with its volume/surface formula and key cross-section. Navoi Opera House as alternative palace for G11 (cone + hemisphere + cylinder). |
| **Hierarchical** (solid/theorem classification) | **Radiant Summary** | Center: "Ko'p yoqlılar" (Polyhedra). Branches: Prizmalar / Piramidalar / Yumaloq jismlar. Sub-branches: formula + cross-section shape + real example. |
| **Sequential** (steps of a 3D proof, cross-section derivation) | **Link System** | Steps of decomposing a composite solid in a vivid SMASHIN' SCOPE chain — each decomposition step "cuts" the next piece off |
| **Discrete formulas** (5 key solid formulas) | **Peg System** | Cylinder, cone, sphere, pyramid, prism formulas each pegged to number-shape images. Recalled both directions. |
| **Numbers/constants** (G10+) | **Major System** | π ≈ 3.14159, √2 ≈ 1.41421 encoded with Major System phonetic code |

**Grade-band availability:** G10-11 has access to ALL techniques.

**Geometriya Advanced addition:** After mnemonic, include a **"What would change if…?"** question: "If the radius of the hemisphere doubled, how would the surface area change? By what factor?" 30-second free response.

**National Pride:** Heritage anchor when natural: *"Al-Biruni yer shariga Kamchik dagi uchburchak usuli bilan o'lchadi — qo'shimcha asboblarsiz."*

### 4.6 Final Boss (Phase 6)

**Inherits:** UNIFIED §5.6. Three-tier system: Sub Boss (every session), Big Boss (weekly, 2× XP, cross-subject, one tier above current PISA), Mythical Boss (<5% random, PISA L5-6, zero hints, one attempt, 5× XP).

**Geometriya Advanced Sub Boss real overrides:**

- **No MC** — open-ended with full working required
- **Question types:** 3D calculation (volume/surface with full working) + Vector proof (prove geometric property using vectors) + Coordinate geometry (find equation of conic or line from geometric conditions)
- **Diagram requirement:** Boss questions MUST include a labeled 3D diagram as part of the question itself — no text-only geometry in Boss

**HP, damage, stars, hints, and combo bonus:** inherit from [[UNIFIED-Buzan §5.6]], unchanged.

**Boss design rules for Geometriya Advanced:**
- At least one question involves a **cross-section** — hardest and most exam-relevant 3D skill
- At least one question is solved using **vectors** (G10: 2D vector applied to plane geometry; G11: full 3D vector proof)
- No purely formulaic volume calculation — student must first identify the solid type and decompose it
- G11 Boss: at least one **composite solid** (two or more simple solids combined or subtracted)
- National Pride Boss framing: *"Bu — sizning professional sertifikatingiz."* On defeat: *"Sertifikatsiya muvaffaqiyatli!"*

**Partial credit rubric (Geometriya Advanced):**
- Correct 3D setup (identifies solid correctly): 1 pt
- Correct decomposition into calculable parts: 1 pt
- Correct calculation: 1 pt
- Correct interpretation or justification: 1 pt

**TEFCAS Failure Flow:**
1. *"Hali emas! Miyangiz Feedback oldi."*
2. Socratic spatial: "Bu 3D shaklga qarang — qaysi yuz asosga parallel?" or "A dan B ga yo'nalishni vektor sifatida ifodalang." — never reveals the calculation
3. Route to specific Story Mode segment
4. Regenerate with new solid/diagram (same standard)

## 5. Content Rules

### 5.1 Narrative Voice

| Element | Rule |
|---|---|
| **POV** | Second-person: "Siz muhandissiz. Oldingizda murakkab 3D jism bor. Hisoblashdan oldin, o'zingizni yo'naltiring: asos qayerda? Simmetriya o'qi qayerda?" |
| **Tone** | Precise, spatial, professional. Most technically demanding geometry in school. Voice respects student maturity and demands exactness. |
| **Language** | Uzbek or Russian. Terms: vector → "vektor", cross-section → "kesim", surface area → "to'la yuzasi", projection → "proyeksiya", normal → "normal". |
| **Diagram labeling** | 3D: x right, y up, z out of page (consistent orientation). Vertices: A, B, C... Edge midpoints: M, N. Vectors: bold or arrow notation consistently throughout a session. |
| **Cultural anchors** | Uzbekistan's architectural heritage (domes, arches, minarets), Soviet-era infrastructure (Charvak dam, Nurek dam), modern development (Tashkent City towers, Navoi Opera House), Central Asian geography (Tian Shan peaks as pyramids, Aral Sea basin as cylinder). |

### 5.2 Visuals

- **3D diagrams are mandatory for every solid geometry question** — no verbal description of a 3D object without a diagram
- **Multiple views** — complex solids show top, front, and side views in addition to 3D perspective
- **Cross-section diagrams** — always show both the original solid AND the resulting cross-section shape side by side
- **Vector arrow diagrams** — arrows with labels; parallel vectors must look parallel; perpendicular vectors must look perpendicular
- **Conic section graphs** — all conics on a coordinate plane with labeled foci, vertices, and axes
- **Color coding:** base face (blue), lateral faces (gray), cross-section (orange), vectors (green arrows)
- Interactive 3D rotation where technically feasible — student can rotate the solid to see all faces

### 5.3 Content Constraints

| Constraint | Rule |
|---|---|
| **3D diagram before calculation** | No calculation on a 3D solid without first establishing the diagram and labeling dimensions |
| **Decompose before computing** | Composite solids must be explicitly decomposed into named simple solids before any formula |
| **Vectors: 2D then 3D** | Begin with 2D vectors applied to plane geometry; extend to 3D in later G10/G11 |
| **Conics: definition before equation** | Every conic introduced through its geometric definition (focus-directrix, two foci) before its algebraic equation |
| **Scope boundary** | Solid geometry, vectors (introductory), coordinate geometry (lines, circles, conics), trig in 3D. Differential geometry, projective geometry — university scope. |
| **Cross-product scope** | Introduced at G11 only, only for finding normal vector to a plane. Do not use for torque or angular momentum — physics scope. |

### 5.4 Aniq Fanlar Family Rules (added v1.1)

*Source: `Aniq_Fanlar_Subject_Family_Framework.md` §3 update. Applies to all Aniq Fanlar subjects.*

**Bidirectional Thinking — mandatory every session:**
Every session must practice BOTH directions:
- Real 3D problem → model: "The dome of the Navoi Opera House is a hemisphere of radius 12m on a cylindrical base. Write the expressions for the surface area of each component and the total."
- Model → real world: "You calculated the total outer shell area as 1 583 m². What does this tell the construction engineer ordering materials? What practical factor should they add for waste and overlap?"

A student who can calculate a hemisphere surface area but cannot explain what the number means for a real engineering decision has not reached the learning goal.

**Error Analysis is PRIMARY — not optional:**
Minimum 1 in every 2 sessions must include an error analysis task on a 3D calculation or vector operation. The task must identify the specific incorrect step.
- Good: "A student calculated the total surface area of a cone-cylinder composite and included the circular base of the cone. Find the error: explain which face should NOT be counted and why."
- Bad: "Is this surface area calculation correct? [Yes / No]"

**Answer Completeness — enforced at all phases:**
Every numerical answer must include correct units and geometric context. Every vector answer must state what the result represents geometrically.
- Incomplete: `V = 904.8`
- Complete: `V = 904.8 m³ — this is the total volume of concrete needed for the cylindrical pylon.`
- Incomplete: `a⃗ · b⃗ = 0`
- Complete: `a⃗ · b⃗ = 0, therefore vectors a⃗ and b⃗ are perpendicular — the two structural beams meet at a right angle.`
