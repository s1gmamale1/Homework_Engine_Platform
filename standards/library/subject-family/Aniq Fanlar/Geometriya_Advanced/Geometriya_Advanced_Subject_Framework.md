## 1. Subject Identity

**Geometriya (Advanced) in Grades 10-11** is the culmination of spatial reasoning in the Aniq Fanlar family. While G7-9 Geometry established 2D shapes, theorems, and basic deductive proof, Advanced Geometry extends into **three-dimensional space, vector algebra, coordinate geometry, and complex proof construction**.

This subject sits at the intersection of two disciplines: it inherits the **visual and deductive nature** of G7-9 Geometry, but also demands **algebraic precision** through coordinate geometry and vectors. It is the most synthesis-intensive subject in the family.

**What Geometriya Advanced IS:**
- Solid geometry: prisms, pyramids, cylinders, cones, spheres — surface area, volume, cross-sections
- Three-dimensional spatial reasoning: visualizing 3D objects from 2D diagrams, projections, cross-sections
- Vectors: addition, scalar multiplication, dot product, cross product (introductory), applications to geometry
- Coordinate geometry (analytic geometry): lines, circles, conic sections (parabola, ellipse, hyperbola)
- Advanced plane geometry: circle theorems, power of a point
- Trigonometry in geometry: sine rule, cosine rule, area with trigonometry, applications in 3D
- Advanced proof: multi-step synthetic proofs, proofs by contradiction, combinatorial geometry

**What Geometriya Advanced is NOT:**
- A repeat of G7-9 Geometry — do not revisit basic 2D theorems without extension
- Differential geometry or topology — university scope
- Calculus applied to geometry — the overlap with Algebra va Analiz is minimal and bounded
- Engineering drawing or technical drafting — the subject is mathematical

**Core Cognitive Demand (Priority Order):**
1. 3D spatial visualization — mentally manipulating solid objects, reading projection diagrams
2. Vector reasoning — geometric relationships through algebraic vector operations
3. Advanced proof construction — multi-step synthetic and analytic proofs
4. Coordinate geometry — translating geometric configurations into algebraic equations
5. Trigonometric application — trig functions solving geometric problems in 3D
6. Cross-disciplinary synthesis — combining vectors, coordinates, and classical geometry in one problem

**Subject Color:** `#0F766E` (dark teal primary) · `#F59E0B` (amber accent)

**Critical distinction from G7-9 Geometry:** G7-9 asks "What is the shape, and what can we prove about it?" Advanced Geometry asks "How does this shape exist in space, how can it be described algebraically, and how does it interact with other objects?"

## 2. Audience Profile — Grade Band G10-11

| Dimension | G10 | G11 |
|---|---|---|
| **Age** | 15-16 | 16-17 |
| **3D spatial ability** | Developing; cross-sections are often the hardest concept | More confident with practice |
| **Vector fluency** | Vectors are new — algebraic interpretation of geometry unfamiliar | Vectors integrated as a natural geometric tool |
| **Working memory** | 6-7 chunks | 7 chunks |
| **Attention ceiling (gamified)** | 45-55 min | 45-60 min |
| **Key challenge** | Holding 3D solid in mind while computing its properties algebraically | Multi-step problems combining vectors, coordinates, and classical theorems |
| **Exam context** | DTM preparation — geometry and vectors both tested | DTM intensive — conics and vectors are high-yield |

**Key pedagogical implication:** The jump from 2D to 3D is the hardest conceptual jump in this subject. Students who excelled in G7-9 Geometry often struggle initially — 2D spatial intuition does not automatically transfer to 3D. Invest heavily in **interactive 3D diagrams, physical object analogies, and cross-section exploration** before any calculation.

### 4.1 Memory Sprint (Phase 1)

**Inherits:** UNIFIED §5.1. Duration ≤2 min (hard cap). Current chapter warm-up — key terms, prerequisite concepts, foundational facts needed for this session.

**Geometriya Advanced format priority:**

| Format | Priority | Geometriya Advanced Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Multiple Choice** | HIGH | "Bu kesim kubni diagonal bo'ylab kesganida qanday shakl hosil bo'ladi?" (4 options). 3D identification, solid recognition from cross-sections. Each item MUST include a diagram. |
| **True / False** | HIGH | "Silindrning yon yuzasi = 2πrh — To'g'ri yoki Noto'g'ri?" Formula verification, theorem checks. |
| **Yes / No / Not Given** | MEDIUM | "Bobda aytilganiga ko'ra, har qanday konus kesimi doira — Ha / Yo'q / Aytilmagan." Chapter comprehension warm-up. |

**Geometriya Advanced additions:**
- Include 1-2 **cross-section questions** — "If this cube is cut diagonally, what shape is the cross-section?" High DTM-yield, builds spatial reasoning
- Every Memory Sprint item must include its diagram
- Wrong answer feedback: show 3D diagram with error annotated: *"Lateral surface area to'g'ri, lekin asos yuzasini unutdingiz."*

**Phase 1 Format Mixing Rule:** MUST use at least 2 of 3 formats per sprint. Every item MUST include a 3D diagram. Include cross-section questions in every sprint.

**Per-format construction — Geometriya Advanced:**

**Multiple Choice (4 options):**
- Cross-section questions are highest priority: *"Bu kesim kubni diagonal bo'ylab kesganida qanday shakl hosil bo'ladi? [3D cube diagram with cutting plane] A) Uchburchak B) To'rtburchak C) Olti burchak D) Doira"* (Answer: depends on cut — teaches spatial reasoning)
- 3D solid identification: *"Quyidagi 3 ta ko'rinish (old, yon, yuqori) qaysi jismga tegishli? [three orthographic views] A) Silindr B) Konus C) Piramida D) Prizma"*
- Distractors: solids that share 1-2 views but differ in the third.

**True / False:**
- *"Silindrning yon yuzasi = 2πrh — To'g'ri yoki Noto'g'ri?"* [cylinder diagram with r and h labeled] (True)
- *"Konus kesimi doira yoki ellips bo'lishi mumkin — To'g'ri yoki Noto'g'ri?"* [cone with two cutting planes] (True — depends on angle)

**Yes / No / Not Given:**
- *"Bobda aytilganiga ko'ra, har qanday konus kesimi doira — Ha / Yo'q / Aytilmagan."* (Yo'q — parabola, ellipse, hyperbola also possible)

### 4.2 Story Mode (Phase 2)

**Inherits:** UNIFIED §5.2. 4-beat arc mandatory. Stranger Test. IELTS-style checkpoints.

**Geometriya Advanced story arc — Spatial Engineering:**

| Arc Beat | Geometriya Advanced Version |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | A real 3D design or construction problem. "You are the lead engineer designing the roof of the new Navoi Opera House. The dome is a hemisphere on a cylindrical base. Given the dimensions, calculate the total outer shell material needed." |
| **Struggle** | The engineer tries 2D area formulas — they work for walls but fail for the curved dome. "2D tools cannot solve 3D curved surfaces." |
| **Discovery** | The hemisphere surface area derived by understanding it as a "peeled" 3D surface: S = 2πr². The physical object helps: the dome is half a full sphere. The formula connects to 3D geometry. |
| **Solution** | Total material = cylinder lateral area + hemisphere area. Calculated, interpreted in m², checked against real building scale. |

≥2 modalities: Spatial + Kinesthetic.

**Phase 3 Game Construction Recipes — Geometriya Advanced:**

**Puzzle Lock / Sliding Tile (Slot 1 Primary):**
- **Structure:** Scrambled 3D diagram that student must reassemble.
- **What goes on each tile:** Fragments of a 3D solid diagram — faces, edges, vertices, labels, projection views.
- **Difficulty progression:**
  - L1: 4-tile — reassemble a labeled prism from its faces. *Scrambled rectangular prism → slide faces into position.*
  - L2: 6-tile — reassemble a composite solid (cylinder + hemisphere). Student must identify how the pieces connect.
  - L3: 9-tile — reconstruct a 3D solid from its three orthographic views (top, front, side) arranged as tiles. Timed.
- **Alternative mode:** "Cross-section assembly" — tiles show possible cross-sections; student arranges which cross-section comes from which cutting angle.
- **Good puzzle:** Reconstruct a pyramid with labeled height, slant height, and base diagonal — all marks must align.
- **Bad puzzle:** Reassemble a photo of a building — no geometric reasoning.

**Tile Match (Slot 2 Primary):**
- **Structure:** 6-8 pairs. Left tile = vector/solid/conic algebraic form. Right tile = geometric representation.
- **Pair types for Geometriya Advanced:**
  - Vector ↔ Arrow representation: `"a⃗ = (3, 4)"` ↔ `[arrow from origin to (3,4) on grid]`
  - Solid ↔ Net: `"Konus"` ↔ `[cone net: circle + sector]`
  - Conic equation ↔ Graph: `"x²/9 + y²/4 = 1"` ↔ `[ellipse with a=3, b=2]`
  - 3D solid ↔ Cross-section: `"Kubni diagonal tekislik bilan kesish"` ↔ `[rectangle cross-section]`
- **Difficulty progression:**
  - L1: Direct — solid name ↔ its standard 3D diagram. 6 pairs.
  - L2: Formula ↔ 3D diagram. *`"V = ⅓πr²h"` ↔ `[cone with r and h labeled]`*. 7 pairs.
  - L3: Cross-representation — vector algebraic form ↔ geometric arrow + solid formula ↔ real-world object. 8 pairs. *`"a⃗ · b⃗ = 0"` ↔ `[two perpendicular arrows]`*
- **Good pair:** `"Sfera to'la yuzasi: S = 4πr²"` ↔ `[sphere diagram with radius marked]`
- **Bad pair:** `"Vektor"` ↔ `"Yo'nalish"` (too vague, no algebraic content)

**Notebook Capture (Slot 3 Primary — defining mechanic for Geometriya Advanced):**
- **Structure:** Student draws 3D figure, labels dimensions, solves, uploads photo.
- **Task types:**
  - 3D construction: *"Asosi 4cm bo'lgan to'g'ri to'rtburchak piramidasi, balandligi 6cm. Barcha qirralar uzunligini toping. Diagrammada hisoblangan uzunliklarni ko'rsating."*
  - Projection drawing: *"Bu L-shaklidagi prizmaning yuqori, old va yon ko'rinishlarini chizing."*
  - Vector sketch: *"Berilgan a⃗ = (2, 3, 1) va b⃗ = (1, −1, 2) vektorlarini chizing. a⃗ + b⃗ ni toping va chizing."*
- **Grading criteria:** 3D diagram accuracy, correct labeling of all dimensions, calculation steps visible, final answer with units.
- **Frequency:** G10-11: 1 in every 2 sessions.

**Reaction Chain (Slot 2 Backup):**
- **Structure:** 6-10 nodes in a sequential vector or solid geometry calculation chain.
- **Geometriya Advanced chain example (finding angle between vectors):**
  - Node 1: *"a⃗ = (1, 2, 3) va b⃗ = (4, −1, 2). a⃗ · b⃗ ni toping."* → Answer: 4 + (−2) + 6 = 8
  - Node 2: *"|a⃗| ni toping."* → Answer: √(1+4+9) = √14
  - Node 3: *"|b⃗| ni toping."* → Answer: √(16+1+4) = √21
  - Node 4: *"cos θ = a⃗·b⃗ / (|a⃗|·|b⃗|) formulasiga qo'ying."* → Answer: 8 / (√14 · √21)
  - Node 5: *"8 / √294 ni soddalashtiring."* → Answer: 8 / (7√6) ≈ 0.466
  - Node 6: *"θ ≈ ? gradus."* → Answer: ≈62.2°
- **Bad chain:** Nodes are unrelated 3D facts (no sequential reasoning).

**Connect Four vs AI (Slot 3 Backup):**
- **Structure:** ~18 questions gating column placement. Each correct answer = place a disc.
- **Question types:** Solid identification from cross-section, vector dot product calculation, surface area formula recall.
- **Example gating question:** *"Kubning diagonali d = a√3 bo'lsa, a = 4 cm uchun d = ?"* (Answer: 4√3)

**Games to AVOID for Geometriya Advanced:**
- Speed Sort with numbers — arithmetic, no spatial value
- Word Ladder — linguistic
- Codebreaker for number patterns — algebraic, not geometric
- Why Chain without a diagram — questions without diagrams have no place here
- Tic Tac Toe — too low-cognitive for G10-11

## 5. Content Rules

### 5.1 Narrative Voice

| Element | Rule |
|---|---|
| **POV** | Second-person: "Siz muhandissiz. Oldingizda murakkab 3D jism bor. Hisoblashdan oldin, o'zingizni yo'naltiring: asos qayerda? Simmetriya o'qi qayerda?" |
| **Tone** | Precise, spatial, professional. Most technically demanding geometry in school. Voice respects student maturity and demands exactness. |
| **Language** | Uzbek or Russian. Terms: vector → "vektor", cross-section → "kesim", surface area → "to'la yuzasi", projection → "proyeksiya", normal → "normal". |
| **Diagram labeling** | 3D: x right, y up, z out of page (consistent orientation). Vertices: A, B, C... Edge midpoints: M, N. Vectors: bold or arrow notation consistently throughout a session. |
| **Cultural anchors** | Uzbekistan's architectural heritage (domes, arches, minarets), Soviet-era infrastructure (Charvak dam, Nurek dam), modern development (Tashkent City towers, Navoi Opera House), Central Asian geography (Tian Shan peaks as pyramids, Aral Sea basin as cylinder). |

### 5.2 Visuals

- **3D diagrams are mandatory for every solid geometry question** — no verbal description of a 3D object without a diagram
- **Multiple views** — complex solids show top, front, and side views in addition to 3D perspective
- **Cross-section diagrams** — always show both the original solid AND the resulting cross-section shape side by side
- **Vector arrow diagrams** — arrows with labels; parallel vectors must look parallel; perpendicular vectors must look perpendicular
- **Conic section graphs** — all conics on a coordinate plane with labeled foci, vertices, and axes
- **Color coding:** base face (blue), lateral faces (gray), cross-section (orange), vectors (green arrows)
- Interactive 3D rotation where technically feasible — student can rotate the solid to see all faces

### 5.3 Content Constraints

| Constraint | Rule |
|---|---|
| **3D diagram before calculation** | No calculation on a 3D solid without first establishing the diagram and labeling dimensions |
| **Decompose before computing** | Composite solids must be explicitly decomposed into named simple solids before any formula |
| **Vectors: 2D then 3D** | Begin with 2D vectors applied to plane geometry; extend to 3D in later G10/G11 |
| **Conics: definition before equation** | Every conic introduced through its geometric definition (focus-directrix, two foci) before its algebraic equation |
| **Scope boundary** | Solid geometry, vectors (introductory), coordinate geometry (lines, circles, conics), trig in 3D. Differential geometry, projective geometry — university scope. |
| **Cross-product scope** | Introduced at G11 only, only for finding normal vector to a plane. Do not use for torque or angular momentum — physics scope. |
