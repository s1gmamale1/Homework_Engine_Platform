## 1. Subject Identity

**Geometriya in Grades 7-9** is the discipline of **space, shape, and logical proof**. It is unlike every other subject in the Aniq Fanlar family because its primary medium is **visual reasoning** and its primary skill is **deductive argumentation**. Geometry is the first place in the Uzbek curriculum where students are asked not just to calculate a correct answer, but to **prove that it must be correct** — a fundamentally different cognitive demand.

**What Geometriya IS:**
- Shapes, angles, and their properties (triangles, quadrilaterals, polygons, circles)
- Measurement: perimeter, area, surface area, volume
- Congruence and similarity: when are two shapes "the same"?
- Deductive proof: constructing logical arguments from axioms and theorems
- Transformations: translation, rotation, reflection
- Coordinate geometry as a bridge between geometry and algebra
- The Pythagorean theorem and its applications

**What Geometriya is NOT:**
- Arithmetic or algebraic manipulation as the primary goal (both are tools, not the subject)
- Rote memorization of formulas — every formula must be derived geometrically before use
- 3D vector geometry and solid geometry proofs — those belong to Geometriya Advanced G10-11

**Core Cognitive Demand (Priority Order):**
1. Spatial visualization — mentally rotating, reflecting, decomposing shapes
2. Deductive reasoning — constructing multi-step logical arguments
3. Diagram reading and construction — extracting information from geometric figures
4. Theorem application — knowing which theorem applies to a given configuration
5. Measurement calculation — area, perimeter, volume with geometric understanding
6. Proof writing — communicating geometric arguments in structured form

**Subject Color:** `#0E7490` (teal primary) · `#F59E0B` (amber accent)

## 2. Audience Profile — Grade Band G7-9

| Dimension | G7 | G8 | G9 |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Age** | 12-13 | 13-14 | 14-15 |
| **Spatial ability** | Variable — mental rotation is hard for many | Improving with practice | Differentiation point for strong spatial thinkers |
| **Proof tolerance** | Can follow a 2-step argument | Can construct 3-4 step proofs with scaffolding | Can construct full formal proofs |
| **Working memory** | 5-6 chunks | 6 chunks | 6-7 chunks |
| **Attention ceiling (gamified)** | 35-45 min | 40-50 min | 40-50 min |
| **Key challenge** | "Seeing" the relationship before writing it | Congruence vs similarity | Multi-step proof without losing the thread |

**Key pedagogical implication:** Diagrams are primary; text is secondary — the exact opposite of most subjects. Diagram literacy must be trained explicitly and early.

### 3.4 Pre-Session Tuning (Phase 0)

**0-A Theme Preview — Geometriya-specific overrides:**
- Gate quote (5-sec lock): geometry/math-tagged. Example: *"Geometriya — ko'zga ko'rinmaydigan narsalarni ko'rish san'atidir." — Euclid ruhi bilan.*
- Panel 1: Structural Scan — theorem tree, shape chips, visual hierarchy
- Panel 5 Personal Hook: Islamic geometric patterns in Samarkand and Bukhara tilework, Chorsu Bazaar dome, Registan Square proportions, hexagonal honeycombs, spiral shells, snowflakes. BOST Prime: *"Geometrik shakllar haqida nimalarni bilasiz?"*
- Panel 7 Industry Application: Architect (angles, triangles, arches), civil engineer (road curves), tile craftsman (Islamic patterns), game developer (2D collision shapes), carpenter (cutting angles)
- Mathematician references: **Euclid** (mandatory — father of deductive geometry, 1 per 3 sessions), **Al-Biruni** (geodesic measurements, Earth's radius — outstanding recurring example), **Thales**, **Pythagoras**, **Archimedes** (circles and area), **Omar Khayyam**

**0-B Flash Cards — Geometriya-specific overrides:**
- **6-8 cards**
- Priority types: **Definition** (what a shape/angle/theorem IS), **Formula** (area, perimeter), **Theorem statement**
- Every card MUST include a labeled diagram — this is non-negotiable for geometry
- Theorem cards: front = statement, back = visual proof sketch
- Returnable during homework — students may reference theorem cards during proof phases (NOT a hint)

## 4. Phase-by-Phase Tuning

### 4.1 Memory Sprint (Phase 1)

**Inherits:** UNIFIED §5.1. Duration ≤2 min (hard cap). Current chapter warm-up — key terms, prerequisite concepts, foundational facts needed for this session.

**Geometriya-specific format priority:**

| Format | Priority | Geometriya Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Multiple Choice** | HIGH | "Bu diagrammaga qaysi teorema taalluqli?" (4 options). Theorem identification, shape property recognition. Each item MUST include a diagram. |
| **True / False** | HIGH | "Uchburchakning burchaklari yig'indisi 180° — To'g'ri yoki Noto'g'ri?" Theorem verification, property checks. |
| **Yes / No / Not Given** | MEDIUM | "Bobda aytilganiga ko'ra, har qanday to'rtburchak parallelogramm — Ha / Yo'q / Aytilmagan." Chapter comprehension warm-up. |

**Geometriya additions:**
- Include 1-2 "How many triangles in this figure?" spatial counting questions — high-DTM-yield, builds diagram literacy
- Every Memory Sprint item must include its diagram — no symbol-only geometry questions
- Wrong answer feedback: show annotated diagram with error highlighted

### 4.2 Story Mode (Phase 2)

**Inherits:** UNIFIED §5.2. 4-beat arc mandatory. Stranger Test. IELTS-style checkpoints.

**Geometriya story arc — Visual Discovery:**

| Arc Beat | Geometriya Version |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | A real construction or design problem with a hidden geometric constraint. "The architects designing the new Samarkand cultural center want a triangular roof. The two base angles are equal. How do they know the roof is balanced without measuring every piece?" |
| **Struggle** | Trying to verify by measurement alone — always imprecise and unreliable at scale. The need for a logical guarantee emerges. |
| **Discovery** | A theorem discovered through observation: "If two angles are equal, the sides opposite them must be equal — this is provable, not just measurable." The visual proof is shown. |
| **Solution** | The theorem applied to the problem. The architectural decision is justified with geometric logic. |

**Hard rules:**
- **DIAGRAM FIRST, every time** — no geometric concept introduced in text before the diagram is shown
- CPA arc for Geometry: Concrete (physical object or photo) → Pictorial (labeled diagram) → Abstract (theorem statement/formula)
- Checkpoint questions test **spatial understanding and theorem identification** — not calculation
  - "Bu uchburchakda qaysi burchaklar teng?" YES
  - "Yuzini hisobla" NO (belongs in Phase 3)
- IELTS-style: identify geometric relationship, compare diagram properties, vocabulary in context
- Grade narrative framework: **G7 — "The Amir Temur Campaign"**; **G8 — "Modern Tashkent"**; **G9 — subject-specific professional**
- Uzbek architectural contexts: Registan, Sher-Dor Madrasah arches, Chorsu Bazaar dome, Fergana valley field layouts
- National Pride: `setting_origin` tag per segment, 55/45 rolling balance

### 4.3 Game Breaks (Phase 3)

**Inherits:** UNIFIED §5.3. 3 games. Dual-Catalog Rule: ≥1 Interactive Catalog, ≥2 Default Pool.

**Geometriya game slot priority — most visually intensive set in the family:**

| Slot | Role | Primary | Backup |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Slot 1** | Spatial reasoning warmup | **Puzzle Lock (Sliding Tile)** — reconstruct a geometric diagram, complete a shape, assemble proof from pieces | Mystery Box — identify shape from surface area or property clues |
| **Slot 2 (Von Restorff Anchor)** | Theorem application | **Tile Match** — theorem name ↔ diagram, shape ↔ properties, formula ↔ figure | Codebreaker — identify which theorem applies to a configuration |
| **Slot 3** | Proof and strategy | **Notebook Capture** — student draws the figure, labels it, solves, uploads photo | Reaction Chain — sequential proof steps (each step unlocks the next) |

> **Von Restorff Anchor (Slot 2):** Preferentially selects items tagged `outstanding: true` — surprising theorems, unexpected relationships, architectural photographs.

**Notebook Capture — highest priority mechanic for Geometriya:**
- G7-8: at least **1 in every 2 sessions** must include a Notebook Capture construction task
- G9: 1 in every 3 sessions
- Construction task example: *"ABC uchburchagini ∠B = 90°, BC = 4cm, AB = 3cm shartida chizing. Barcha to'g'ri burchaklarni belgilang. AC ni toping."*
- Student draws, solves, uploads photo

**Buzan Cortical Diversity:**
- Puzzle Lock = Visual/Spatial
- Tile Match = Visual/Spatial + Verbal/Logical
- Notebook Capture = Kinesthetic/Motor
≥2 different modalities satisfied (Spatial + Kinesthetic).

**Games to AVOID for Geometriya:**
- Worked Examples completion without diagrams — text-only worked examples don't work in geometry
- Speed Match with numbers only — no geometric value
- Word Ladder — linguistic
- Why Chain without a diagram — no place here

### 4.4 Real-Life Challenge (Phase 4)

**Inherits:** UNIFIED §5.4. First-person Expert POV mandatory.

**Expert roles for Geometriya:**
- Architect calculating structural angles for a traditional Uzbek iwan archway
- Civil engineer determining optimal angle for a road bend at Kamchik Pass
- Tiling craftsman designing an Islamic geometric pattern for a mosque wall (girikh)
- Surveyor calculating land area for a Fergana valley farm using triangulation
- Game developer calculating collision boundaries for 2D polygon objects
- Furniture designer optimizing wood cuts to minimize waste (area and angles)
- Stadium designer ensuring sight lines from all seats reach the pitch

**Buzan W5H Scaffold:**
- **G7-8:** ON, 5 of 6 branches mandatory
- **G9:** Available via "Tahlil" button, not forced
- Geometry W5H example (architect problem): Who = architect, What = triangular roof, Where = Samarkand cultural center, Why = structural balance required, How = apply isosceles triangle theorem

**Decision Mapping:** Applied when scenario involves choosing between two design options (e.g., triangular vs rectangular roof cross-section). NOT applied to single-answer proof problems.

**Wise Status — National Pride (30% of Phase 4 tasks):**
1. **Status:** "Bosh Arxitektor," "Qurilish Muhandisi," "Geodezist"
2. **Arena (55/45):** National: Registan Square geometry, Chorsu Bazaar dome proportions, Samarkand Shah-i-Zinda corridor angles. Global: CERN detector geometry, Eiffel Tower truss structure.
3. **Bridge:** The geometric proof or calculation is UNTOUCHED.
4. **Closing:** National or global contribution line.

**Hard rules:**
- Problem must include a diagram
- Student must **name the theorem or property being used** before applying it
- Multi-step: at least one intermediate geometric result needed before the final answer
- Uzbek architectural and engineering contexts strongly preferred

### 4.5 Consolidation / Mnemonic Lock (Phase 5)

**Inherits:** UNIFIED §5.5. Technique by content structure. SMASHIN' SCOPE quality gate (6+/12).

**Technique selection for Geometriya:**

| Content Structure | Technique | Geometriya Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Hierarchical** (theorem classification, shape families) | **Radiant Summary** | Center: "Uchburchak" (Triangle). Branches: Teng yonli / To'g'ri burchakli / Ixtiyoriy. Sub-branches: properties + area formula + key theorems. 3+ colors, one keyword per branch. |
| **Spatial** (theorem locations, architectural sites) | **Memory Palace** | Registan Square default. Each theorem placed at a hotspot with its visual proof. E.g., Pythagorean theorem at the central pool (right angle = water's right angle). |
| **Sequential** (proof steps, theorem proof chains) | **Link System** | Steps of a 4-step angle proof in a vivid SMASHIN' SCOPE chain — each step "causes" the next visually |
| **Discrete facts** (5 key theorem names) | **Peg System** | 5 theorems pegged to number-shapes, recalled both directions |

**Grade-band availability:** G7-9 has access to: Full Memory Palace, Peg System, Radiant Summary, Link System, Major System (though Major System rarely useful for geometry). Memory Palace and Radiant Summary are the primary choices.

**Geometriya addition:** After mnemonic, show 4-5 diagrams — student names the theorem illustrated by each. "Theorem gallery" — 45 seconds.

**National Pride:** Heritage anchor when natural: *"Al-Biruni Yer radiusini uchburchak geometriyasi yordamida o'lchadi — dala tajribasiz."*

## 5. Content Rules

### 5.1 Narrative Voice

| Element | Rule |
|---|---|
| **POV** | Second-person (formal "Siz"): "Siz arxitektsiz. Bu uchburchakka qarang. Nima isbotlay olasiz?" |
| **Tone** | Precise, observational, structured. Geometry rewards careful attention to what is given vs what is assumed. Voice models this precision. |
| **Language** | Uzbek or Russian. Terms: angle → "burchak", perpendicular → "perpendikulyar", congruent → "tengo'lchovli", hypotenuse → "gipotenuz". |
| **Diagram labeling** | Vertices: capital letters (A, B, C). Sides: lowercase opposite letters (a, b, c). Angles: ∠ABC notation. Consistent with textbook. |
| **Cultural anchors** | Islamic geometric art (girikh, muqarnas), Central Asian architecture (Registan, Shah-i-Zinda), nature (Tian Shan profiles, Aral Sea islands, Fergana valley farmland grid). |

### 5.2 Visuals

- **Diagrams are primary content**, not illustrations — every geometric concept conveyed through a labeled figure
- **No shape described only in text** — if you name a shape, show a diagram
- **Proof diagrams:** blue for given information, orange for what is to be proved, green for construction lines
- **Mobile-legible:** angle labels ≥ 12pt, side labels ≥ 10pt, touch-friendly diagram interaction
- **Interactive diagrams preferred:** drag a vertex to see how angles change — builds dynamic spatial reasoning

### 5.3 Content Constraints

| Constraint | Rule |
|---|---|
| **Diagram before theorem** | No theorem stated without a diagram showing the configuration it describes |
| **No formula without derivation** | Triangle area derived from rectangle area before formula is used |
| **Proof must cite reasons** | Every step: "(angles on a straight line sum to 180°)" |
| **Grade-scoped proofs** | G7: 2-step argument. G8: 3-4 step formal proof. G9: full multi-step with auxiliary construction. |
| **No 3D beyond scope** | G7-9 covers 2D shapes and basic 3D measurement. Advanced 3D proof belongs to Geometriya Advanced. |
| **Pythagorean theorem is G8+** | Do not introduce Pythagorean theorem in G7 sessions. |

### 5.4 Aniq Fanlar Family Rules (added v1.1)

*Source: `Aniq_Fanlar_Subject_Family_Framework.md` §3 update. Applies to all Aniq Fanlar subjects.*

**Bidirectional Thinking — mandatory every session:**
Every session must practice BOTH directions:
- Real situation → theorem: "The architect says both base angles of the triangular roof are equal. What geometric property guarantees the roof is balanced? State the theorem."
- Theorem → real world: "You proved the triangle is isosceles using the base angle theorem. What does this guarantee about the physical roof — why can the architect trust this without re-measuring?"

A student who can cite a theorem but cannot explain what it guarantees in a real context has not learned geometric reasoning.

**Error Analysis is PRIMARY — not optional:**
Minimum 1 in every 2 sessions must include an error analysis task. The task must ask WHERE the proof step is wrong and WHY — not just whether the final answer is correct.
- Good: "A student's proof says: 'Since AB = CD, the triangles are congruent (SSS).' Find the error: what information is missing and which congruence rule actually applies?"
- Bad: "Is this proof correct? [Yes / No]"

**Answer Completeness — enforced at all phases:**
Every numerical answer must include correct units. Every proof answer must include the theorem or property cited.
- Incomplete (calculation): `Area = 24`
- Complete: `Area = 24 m²`
- Incomplete (proof step): "Therefore the angles are equal."
- Complete: "Therefore ∠ABC = ∠BCD (alternate interior angles, AB ∥ CD)."
