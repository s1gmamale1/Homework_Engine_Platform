## 1. Subject Identity

**Geometriya in Grades 7-9** is the discipline of **space, shape, and logical proof**. It is unlike every other subject in the Aniq Fanlar family because its primary medium is **visual reasoning** and its primary skill is **deductive argumentation**. Geometry is the first place in the Uzbek curriculum where students are asked not just to calculate a correct answer, but to **prove that it must be correct** — a fundamentally different cognitive demand.

**What Geometriya IS:**
- Shapes, angles, and their properties (triangles, quadrilaterals, polygons, circles)
- Measurement: perimeter, area, surface area, volume
- Congruence and similarity: when are two shapes "the same"?
- Deductive proof: constructing logical arguments from axioms and theorems
- Transformations: translation, rotation, reflection
- Coordinate geometry as a bridge between geometry and algebra
- The Pythagorean theorem and its applications

**What Geometriya is NOT:**
- Arithmetic or algebraic manipulation as the primary goal (both are tools, not the subject)
- Rote memorization of formulas — every formula must be derived geometrically before use
- 3D vector geometry and solid geometry proofs — those belong to Geometriya Advanced G10-11

**Core Cognitive Demand (Priority Order):**
1. Spatial visualization — mentally rotating, reflecting, decomposing shapes
2. Deductive reasoning — constructing multi-step logical arguments
3. Diagram reading and construction — extracting information from geometric figures
4. Theorem application — knowing which theorem applies to a given configuration
5. Measurement calculation — area, perimeter, volume with geometric understanding
6. Proof writing — communicating geometric arguments in structured form

**Subject Color:** `#0E7490` (teal primary) · `#F59E0B` (amber accent)

## 4. Phase-by-Phase Tuning

### 4.1 Memory Sprint (Phase 1)

**Inherits:** UNIFIED §5.1. Duration ≤2 min (hard cap). Current chapter warm-up — key terms, prerequisite concepts, foundational facts needed for this session.

**Geometriya-specific format priority:**

| Format | Priority | Geometriya Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Multiple Choice** | HIGH | "Bu diagrammaga qaysi teorema taalluqli?" (4 options). Theorem identification, shape property recognition. Each item MUST include a diagram. |
| **True / False** | HIGH | "Uchburchakning burchaklari yig'indisi 180° — To'g'ri yoki Noto'g'ri?" Theorem verification, property checks. |
| **Yes / No / Not Given** | MEDIUM | "Bobda aytilganiga ko'ra, har qanday to'rtburchak parallelogramm — Ha / Yo'q / Aytilmagan." Chapter comprehension warm-up. |

**Geometriya additions:**
- Include 1-2 "How many triangles in this figure?" spatial counting questions — high-DTM-yield, builds diagram literacy
- Every Memory Sprint item must include its diagram — no symbol-only geometry questions
- Wrong answer feedback: show annotated diagram with error highlighted

**Phase 1 Format Mixing Rule:** MUST use at least 2 of 3 formats per sprint. Every item MUST include a diagram — no text-only geometry in Phase 1.

**Per-format construction — Geometriya-specific:**

**Multiple Choice (4 options):**
- Every stem includes a labeled diagram. *"Bu diagrammaga qaysi teorema taalluqli? [diagram with two equal angles and two equal sides] A) Pifagor teoremasi B) Teng yonli uchburchak xossasi C) Tashqi burchak teoremasi D) O'xshashlik"*
- Distractors: theorems that COULD apply to a similar but different diagram.
- Spatial counting: *"Bu rasmda nechta uchburchak bor? [complex figure] A) 4 B) 6 C) 8 D) 10"*

**True / False:**
- *"Uchburchakning burchaklari yig'indisi 180° — To'g'ri yoki Noto'g'ri?"* [diagram of triangle with angle marks]
- *"Rombning diagonallari teng — To'g'ri yoki Noto'g'ri?"* [diagram of rhombus] (False — diagonals bisect each other at right angles but are not equal)

**Yes / No / Not Given:**
- *"Bobda aytilganiga ko'ra, har qanday to'rtburchak parallelogramm — Ha / Yo'q / Aytilmagan."* [diagram showing various quadrilaterals]
- "Not Given" must be genuinely ambiguous based on chapter scope.

### 4.2 Story Mode (Phase 2)

**Inherits:** UNIFIED §5.2. 4-beat arc mandatory. Stranger Test. IELTS-style checkpoints.

**Geometriya story arc — Visual Discovery:**

| Arc Beat | Geometriya Version |
|---|---|
| **Problem** | A real construction or design problem with a hidden geometric constraint. "The architects designing the new Samarkand cultural center want a triangular roof. The two base angles are equal. How do they know the roof is balanced without measuring every piece?" |
| **Struggle** | Trying to verify by measurement alone — always imprecise and unreliable at scale. The need for a logical guarantee emerges. |
| **Discovery** | A theorem discovered through observation: "If two angles are equal, the sides opposite them must be equal — this is provable, not just measurable." The visual proof is shown. |
| **Solution** | The theorem applied to the problem. The architectural decision is justified with geometric logic. |

**Hard rules:**
- **DIAGRAM FIRST, every time** — no geometric concept introduced in text before the diagram is shown
- CPA arc for Geometry: Concrete (physical object or photo) → Pictorial (labeled diagram) → Abstract (theorem statement/formula)
- Checkpoint questions test **spatial understanding and theorem identification** — not calculation
  - "Bu uchburchakda qaysi burchaklar teng?" YES
  - "Yuzini hisobla" NO (belongs in Phase 3)
- IELTS-style: identify geometric relationship, compare diagram properties, vocabulary in context
- Grade narrative framework: **G7 — "The Amir Temur Campaign"**; **G8 — "Modern Tashkent"**; **G9 — subject-specific professional**
- Uzbek architectural contexts: Registan, Sher-Dor Madrasah arches, Chorsu Bazaar dome, Fergana valley field layouts
- National Pride: `setting_origin` tag per segment, 55/45 rolling balance

| Slot | Role | Primary | Backup |

**Notebook Capture — highest priority mechanic for Geometriya:**
- G7-8: at least **1 in every 2 sessions** must include a Notebook Capture construction task
- G9: 1 in every 3 sessions
- Construction task example: *"ABC uchburchagini ∠B = 90°, BC = 4cm, AB = 3cm shartida chizing. Barcha to'g'ri burchaklarni belgilang. AC ni toping."*
- Student draws, solves, uploads photo

**Buzan Cortical Diversity:**
- Puzzle Lock = Visual/Spatial
- Tile Match = Visual/Spatial + Verbal/Logical
- Notebook Capture = Kinesthetic/Motor
≥2 different modalities satisfied (Spatial + Kinesthetic).

**Phase 3 Game Construction Recipes — Geometriya:**

**Puzzle Lock / Sliding Tile (Slot 1 Primary):**
- **Structure:** Scrambled geometric diagram that student must reconstruct by sliding tiles into correct positions.
- **What goes on each tile:** Diagram fragments — parts of a labeled geometric figure (vertices, sides, angle marks, labels).
- **Difficulty progression:**
  - L1: 4-tile puzzle — reconstruct a simple triangle with labeled vertices. *Scrambled ABC triangle → slide to correct positions.*
  - L2: 6-tile puzzle — reconstruct a proof diagram with auxiliary lines. Student must identify where construction lines go.
  - L3: 9-tile puzzle — full theorem diagram with labels, angle marks, and equal-side markers. Timed.
- **Alternative mode:** "Proof assembly" — tiles contain proof steps (Statement → Reason → Conclusion). Student arranges into logical order.
- **Good puzzle:** Reconstruct a diagram of two congruent triangles with shared side, all congruence marks visible.
- **Bad puzzle:** Reconstruct a photograph — no geometric reasoning involved.

**Tile Match (Slot 2 Primary):**
- **Structure:** 6-8 pairs. Left tile = theorem name or property statement. Right tile = labeled diagram illustrating it.
- **Pair types for Geometriya:**
  - Theorem ↔ Diagram: `"Pifagor teoremasi"` ↔ `[right triangle with a²+b²=c² labeled]`
  - Shape ↔ Properties: `"Parallelogramm"` ↔ `"Qarama-qarshi tomonlar teng va parallel"`
  - Formula ↔ Figure: `"S = ½ × a × h"` ↔ `[triangle with base a and height h marked]`
- **Difficulty progression:**
  - L1: Direct — theorem name ↔ its standard diagram. 6 pairs.
  - L2: Property list ↔ shape. Student must know which properties belong to which shape. 7 pairs.
  - L3: Non-obvious — theorem ↔ diagram where the theorem is NOT immediately visible (requires spatial reasoning). 8 pairs.
- **Good pair:** `"Tashqi burchak teoremasi"` ↔ `[triangle with exterior angle = sum of remote interior angles marked]`
- **Bad pair:** `"Uchburchak"` ↔ `"3 tomonli shakl"` (too trivial, no visual)

**Notebook Capture (Slot 3 Primary — highest priority for Geometriya):**
- **Structure:** Student draws geometric figure by hand, labels it, solves, uploads photo.
- **Task types:**
  - Construction: *"ABC uchburchagini ∠B = 90°, BC = 4cm, AB = 3cm shartida chizing. Barcha to'g'ri burchaklarni belgilang. AC ni toping."*
  - Proof sketch: *"Teng yonli uchburchakda asos burchaklari teng ekanini diagramma yordamida isbotlang. Barcha qadamlarni yozing."*
  - Annotation: *"Berilgan diagrammada barcha teng tomonlarni va teng burchaklarni belgilang. Keyin yuzani hisoblab yozing."*
- **Grading criteria:** Diagram accuracy (labeled vertices, correct angle marks), solution steps visible, final answer with units.
- **Frequency:** G7-8: 1 in every 2 sessions. G9: 1 in every 3 sessions.

**Reaction Chain (Slot 3 Backup):**
- **Structure:** 6-8 nodes in a sequential proof chain. Each correct step unlocks the next.
- **Geometriya chain example (proving triangle angles sum to 180°):**
  - Node 1: *"AB || CD chiziqni chizing. Bu qanday chiziq?"* → Answer: "Parallel chiziq"
  - Node 2: *"∠1 va ∠BAC qanday burchaklar?"* → Answer: "Ichki qarama-qarshi burchaklar"
  - Node 3: *"Demak ∠1 = ∠BAC. Nima uchun?"* → Answer: "Parallel chiziqlar orasidagi ichki qarama-qarshi burchaklar teng"
  - Node 4: *"Xuddi shunday, ∠2 = ∠BCA. D nuqtada ∠1 + ∠B + ∠2 = ?"* → Answer: "180°"
  - Node 5: *"∠1 = ∠A va ∠2 = ∠C bo'lgani uchun, ∠A + ∠B + ∠C = ?"* → Answer: "180°"
  - Node 6: *"Biz nima isbotladik?"* → Answer: "Uchburchak burchaklari yig'indisi 180°"
- **Bad chain:** Each node is an unrelated geometry fact (no logical flow).

**Games to AVOID for Geometriya:**
- Worked Examples completion without diagrams — text-only worked examples don't work in geometry
- Speed Match with numbers only — no geometric value
- Word Ladder — linguistic
- Why Chain without a diagram — no place here

## 5. Content Rules

### 5.1 Narrative Voice

| Element | Rule |
|---|---|
| **POV** | Second-person (formal "Siz"): "Siz arxitektsiz. Bu uchburchakka qarang. Nima isbotlay olasiz?" |
| **Tone** | Precise, observational, structured. Geometry rewards careful attention to what is given vs what is assumed. Voice models this precision. |
| **Language** | Uzbek or Russian. Terms: angle → "burchak", perpendicular → "perpendikulyar", congruent → "tengo'lchovli", hypotenuse → "gipotenuz". |
| **Diagram labeling** | Vertices: capital letters (A, B, C). Sides: lowercase opposite letters (a, b, c). Angles: ∠ABC notation. Consistent with textbook. |
| **Cultural anchors** | Islamic geometric art (girikh, muqarnas), Central Asian architecture (Registan, Shah-i-Zinda), nature (Tian Shan profiles, Aral Sea islands, Fergana valley farmland grid). |

### 5.2 Visuals

- **Diagrams are primary content**, not illustrations — every geometric concept conveyed through a labeled figure
- **No shape described only in text** — if you name a shape, show a diagram
- **Proof diagrams:** blue for given information, orange for what is to be proved, green for construction lines
- **Mobile-legible:** angle labels ≥ 12pt, side labels ≥ 10pt, touch-friendly diagram interaction
- **Interactive diagrams preferred:** drag a vertex to see how angles change — builds dynamic spatial reasoning

### 5.3 Content Constraints

| Constraint | Rule |
|---|---|
| **Diagram before theorem** | No theorem stated without a diagram showing the configuration it describes |
| **No formula without derivation** | Triangle area derived from rectangle area before formula is used |
| **Proof must cite reasons** | Every step: "(angles on a straight line sum to 180°)" |
| **Grade-scoped proofs** | G7: 2-step argument. G8: 3-4 step formal proof. G9: full multi-step with auxiliary construction. |
| **No 3D beyond scope** | G7-9 covers 2D shapes and basic 3D measurement. Advanced 3D proof belongs to Geometriya Advanced. |
| **Pythagorean theorem is G8+** | Do not introduce Pythagorean theorem in G7 sessions. |
