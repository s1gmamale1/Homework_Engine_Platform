# Sentence Fill: Cloze Test Edition

> Default Mechanic #4 — Systematic Word Removal for Contextual Vocabulary & Grammar Practice

**Players:** 1 | **Mode:** Solo Cloze | **Buzan Integrated** | **Bloom's:** Understand | **PISA:** 2 | **AI Tier:** Tier 1

---

## 1. Sentence Fill (Cloze) Mechanics

A passage (sentence, paragraph, or formula explanation) has **systematically removed words** replaced by blank lines. The student fills each blank from a **word bank** (lower grades) or by **free recall** (G8+). Based on **Cloze Testing research** (Taylor, 1953).

### Word Removal Rules

- **Grades 2-4:** Every 3rd word removed. Word bank provided with 2-3 distractors per blank.
- **Grades 5-7:** Every 4th word removed. Word bank provided with 3-4 distractors per blank.
- **Grades 8-11:** Every 5th word removed. **No word bank** — free recall only.

### Protected Words (Never Removed)

- Proper nouns (names, places, dates)
- Key formulas (if the passage is about a formula, the formula itself stays visible)
- The first and last word of each sentence (preserves sentence frame for context)
- Articles and prepositions in lower grades (G2-4) — these are grammatical, not content words

### How It Is Played

1. Passage appears with blanks numbered (1, 2, 3...).
2. Word bank displayed below (for G2-7). G8+ sees only the passage.
3. Student drags a word from the bank to a blank (or types for G8+).
4. After filling all blanks, student taps "Tekshirish" (Check).
5. Correct fills turn green (+100 XP each). Incorrect fills turn red and show the correct word (+0 XP, no penalty).
6. +25 XP first-attempt bonus for each blank filled correctly on the first try.
7. +100 bonus XP if all blanks are correct (Perfect Fill).

### Parameters

- Blanks per passage: 3-6 (depends on passage length)
- XP: 100 per correct fill + 25 first-attempt bonus per blank + 100 Perfect Fill bonus
- No timer — student-paced
- Maximum 2 attempts per blank (after 2 wrong, correct answer is revealed and the blank is locked)

## 3. Buzan Integration: Contextual Encoding

- **Color Hooks by Subject:** The passage text uses subject family colors for key terms (even in the cloze version). Math terms in blue, science terms in green. The color acts as a retrieval cue — the student's brain associates "blue word in a blank" with "this blank needs a math term."
- **Radiant Context:** When a student completes a cloze passage perfectly, the full passage (with all blanks filled correctly) briefly glows with a connecting web that links key terms to their definitions in the margins. This creates a mini "radiant map" — the passage becomes a node in the student's Knowledge Web.
- **Associative Anchors (Premium):** For abstract vocabulary, a small illustration appears next to the blank (e.g., a tiny plant icon next to a blank that needs "fotosintez"). The illustration acts as a Buzan-style imagery hook, creating visual-verbal dual encoding.

## 4. Question Styles & Interaction Mechanics

The cloze passage IS the question. Difficulty scales by grade band and PISA level:

| PISA Level | Passage Type | Removed Words | Distractor Style | Cognitive Target |
|-----------|-------------|--------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **L1** | Single sentence with concrete nouns | Nouns, obvious verbs | Obvious mismatches (wrong part of speech) | Vocabulary recognition in context |
| **L2** | 2-sentence passage with technical terms | Technical terms, key verbs | Same-category distractors (both are science terms, only one fits) | Technical vocabulary in context |
| **L3** | Paragraph with causal chain | Causal connectors ("because," "therefore," "as a result"), process verbs | Logical but incorrect connectors (both fit grammatically, only one fits the causal logic) | Causal reasoning through language |
| **L4** | Multi-paragraph analysis | Abstract nouns, qualifying language ("primarily," "to some extent"), comparative structures | Nuanced distractors (similar meaning but different degree or certainty) | Precision of academic language |
| **L5+** | Source excerpt (historical document, scientific abstract) | Key argument terms, rhetorical markers | All options are academically valid; only one matches the original author's intent | Close reading + authorial intent analysis |

> **Stranger Test Compliance:** L1 cloze passages always retain enough context words (first/last word protected, nouns protected) that visual and logical inference can guide the student even without full language comprehension. L3+ assumes reading comprehension is part of the test.

## 5. Victory Conditions & Scoring

### A. Cloze Completion Scoring

| Event | XP | Notes |
|-------|----|-------|
| Correct fill | +100 | Per blank, regardless of attempt number (1st or 2nd) |
| First-attempt bonus | +25 | Per blank filled correctly on first try |
| Perfect Fill | +100 | All blanks correct on first attempt — bonus on top of per-blank XP |
| 2nd-attempt correct | +100 (no bonus) | Correct on retry — base XP only, no first-attempt bonus |
| 2 attempts failed | 0 | Correct answer revealed, blank locked. No XP for this blank. |

### B. Subject-Specific Implementations

- **Language Sciences:** Grammar-in-context. Blanks target specific grammar rules (verb tense, article usage, adjective agreement). Word bank includes morphological variants.
- **Natural Sciences:** Science definition cloze. Blanks target technical vocabulary. Protected words include formulas and equations.
- **Exact Sciences:** Formula explanation cloze. The formula itself is visible; blanks are in the explanatory text around it.
- **Social Sciences:** Historical narrative cloze. Blanks target dates, names, causal connectors in historical analysis.
- **Arts / Tarbiya:** Values and concepts. Blanks target character education vocabulary, artistic terminology.

### C. "Hali Emas" Framing

When a student fails a blank (2 wrong attempts), the correct answer is shown with a gentle explanation. There is no penalty — the system treats the revealed answer as a learning opportunity, not a failure. The blank is counted in the student's accuracy stats but does not block session progress.
