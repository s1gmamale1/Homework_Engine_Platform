Phase 4 Demo

NETS Real-Life Challenge

This is a simple demo of the first-person expert mechanic. It follows the source rules: local context, structured evidence, a real decision, and explanation as a required part of scoring.

Your Role

You are a transport pricing advisor helping a Tashkent district office compare two taxi pricing proposals for local commuters.

A council member says: “Plan B is clearly better for everyone.” Your job is to check whether that claim is true and write a recommendation.

Math transfer PISA L2->L3 Evaluate with explanation

Case Facts

Plan Base Fare Per km
Company A 2,000 UZS 1,500 UZS
Company B 3,000 UZS 1,000 UZS

Challenge

1. At what distance do the two plans cost the same?

Enter a number in kilometers.

2. Which statement is most accurate?

3. Write your recommendation

Explain which plan is better for short commuters and which is better for longer trips. Use the numbers from the case.

Why This Demo Matches NETS

  • The student is the expert, not a spectator.
  • The case uses local context without needing outside knowledge.
  • The task moves from calculation to decision to explanation.
  • The explanation matters more than the raw choice.
  • The wrong options are plausible enough to expose shallow reasoning.

Expected Strong Answer

The plans are equal at 2 km. For trips shorter than 2 km, Company A is cheaper because its lower base fare matters more. For trips longer than 2 km, Company B becomes cheaper because the lower per-kilometer price overtakes the higher base fare.
