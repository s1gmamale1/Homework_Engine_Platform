Phase 4 Mechanic

NETS Real-Life Challenge v1

Real-Life Challenge is the transfer mechanic in Phase 4. The student applies the current lesson to a realistic Uzbek-context case, as the expert, and must explain the decision.

Transfer application PISA L2+ First-person expert POV Explanation required

Purpose

This mechanic tests transfer, not recall. It sits between practice and Final Boss:

  • Story Mode teaches
  • Game Breaks rehearse
  • Real-Life Challenge transfers
  • Final Boss certifies

Non-Negotiable Rules

  1. The student must be addressed in first-person expert POV.
  2. The student must be the expert, not the observer.
  3. The case must be solvable using current-lesson knowledge only.
  4. Explanation is required.
  1. Wrong options must be plausible.
  2. The context should feel believable and may use local or broader industry applications.
  3. Students below PISA Level 2 get extra Apply-level practice instead.

Core Structure

Expert Frame

You are a fire safety inspector...

You are a transport pricing advisor...

Situation Brief

A short case with concrete constraints, numbers, and a real decision.

Evidence Set

Use a table, short text, diagram, map, or timeline. No hidden knowledge.

Decision Prompt

Choose, compare, recommend, diagnose, prioritize, or explain.

Scoring

DimensionWeight
Decision accuracy40%
Reasoning quality60%

Reasoning is weighted higher because transfer is the point of the mechanic.

  • Off-track: wrong concept or ignores case facts
  • Partial: some correct work, weak explanation
  • Solid: correct conclusion with mostly correct reasoning
  • Strong transfer: correct conclusion, evidence-based explanation, handles constraints well

Best Variations

  • Choose the Best Option: compare 2-3 options and recommend one
  • Diagnose the Problem: find what is wrong and explain why
  • Prioritize the First Move: choose the best first action under constraints
  • Compare Claims: decide which claim is stronger and justify it

Anti-Patterns

  • Third-person spectator framing
  • Fake real-life wrapping on a normal worksheet
  • Outside-knowledge dependency
  • Single-step textbook question disguised as transfer
  • Explanation-free tasks

Acceptance Checklist

  • The student is explicitly the expert
  • The task requires transfer, not recall only
  • All required facts are supplied
  • Explanation is mandatory
  • Wrong paths are plausible
  • The context feels believable and appropriately applied
  • The task fits Phase 4 time limits

Canonical Example

Bad: A taxi costs 2000 + 1500 per km. What is the price for 5 km?
Good: You are advising the city council on taxi pricing. Company A charges 2000 UZS base + 1500 per km. Company B charges 3000 UZS base + 1000 per km. At what distance do they cost the same? A commuter says Company B is always cheaper. Is that true? Write your recommendation and explain why.
